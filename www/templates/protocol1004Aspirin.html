Aspirin

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1004

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 300.

Class: Antiplatelet

MECHANISM OF ACTION
1. Reduces overall mortality from an acute MI
2. Inhibits platelet clumping

INDICATIONS
1. Patients who present with symptoms of an acute MI
2. Chest Pain / Angina suspicious for an acute MI

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Known Allergy to Aspirin
2. CVA/TIA Symptoms
3. Any active bleeding (Oral, or Rectal)
4. Associated back pain or signs and symptoms of an Aortic Dissection
5. Not to be used to treat pediatric fevers

SIDE EFFECTS
1. GI Upset
2. GI Bleeding or other significant bleeding

Dosage and Administration

1. Adults: 4 Children's Chewable tablets (DO NOT SWALLOW) 4x 81mg total of 324mg
2. PEDS: Not to be used for fever control

CONSIDERATIONS
1. Give Early
2. May moisten mouth if necessary
3. Never use to treat a child's Fever
