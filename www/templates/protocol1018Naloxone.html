Naloxone

Narcan
Approved for Advanced CCEMS Units Only

Protocol 1018

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 306, 311, 602, 713.

Narcotic Antagonist

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Competes with Opiate Receptors in the CNS.
2. Displaces Narcotics for Receptors in the CNS.
3. Reverses Respiratory Depression Associated with Narcotic Overdose.

INDICATIONS
Antitode to all Narcotic Drugs Such as Morphine, Demerol, Codeine, Methadone, Fentanyl, Percocet, Dilaudid, Heroin, Paregoric, Darcon, Stadol, and Talwin.

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Hypersensitivity

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Hypotension.
2. Ventricular Arrythmias.
3. Nausea/Vomiting.
4. Acute Narcotic Withdrawal.

Considerations
1. Titrate Slowly if Suspect Narcotic Dependence.
2. If No Improvement, Suspect Other Cause or Toxicity Due to a Different Drug (TCA, Benzodiazepine, Barbiturate.)

Dosage and Administration

Adult Dose

1. Naloxone 1-2mg (per dose): IV/IM/IO or Intranasal (Atomizer). May Repeat x1 for Suspected Narcotic Overdose Contact OLMC for Additional Doses.

Peds Dose

1. Naloxone 0.1mg/kg (Max 1mg): IV/IM/IO or Intranasal (Atomizer) for Suspected Narcotic Overdose- Contact OLMC for Additional Doses.
2. Naloxone: 0.1 mg/kg Repeated every 2-3 Minutes as Needed for Babies of Suspected Narcotic Addicted Mothers.
