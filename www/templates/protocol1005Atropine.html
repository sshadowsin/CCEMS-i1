Atropine

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1005

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 201, 315, 709.

Class: Anticholinergic

MECHANISM OF ACTION
1. Blocks acetylcholine receptors
2. Inhibits Parasympathetic Stimulation via the Vagus Nerve
3. Chronotropic Effects/Little Inotropic Effect

INDICATIONS
1. Symptomatic Sinus Bradycardia
2. May be beneficial in presence of AV Nodal Block (1st Degree block, 2nd Degree AV Block type 1)
3. Organophosphate poisoning (Large Doses may be required)

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. High Degree Nodal Blocks (Mobitz 2, 3rd Degree)-Relative
2. Hypothermic Bradycardia

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Tachycardia
2. Confusion
3. Dilated Pupils

Dosage and Administration

Symptomatic Bradycardia

1. Adults: 0.5mg, repeat every 3-5 minutes as needed, up to 3mg MAX
2. PEDS: 0.02 mg/kg IV repeat every 3-5 mintues as needed
a. MAX SINGLE DOSE Child or Adolescent 0.5 mg
b. MAX TOTAL DOSE Child 1.0mg or Adolescent 3mg

Organophosphate Poisoning

Atropine: 2mg IV or higher may be needed - Contact OLMC for dosing instructions

CONSIDERATIONS
1. Use with caution if suspected Myocardial Ischemia - which increases O2 demand
2. Dose <0.5 mg may cause Paradoxical Bradycardia
