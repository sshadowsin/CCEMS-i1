Administration of Oral Glucose

This protocol is for the EMT First Responder only.

Determine Need for Oral Glucose:

  1. Refer to Protocol 319: GLUCOSE EMERGENCIES: HYPOGLYCEMIA/HYPERGLYCEMIA
  2. Patient exhibits altered mental status has history of diabetic problems
  3. Glucometer reading of <60

Inquire About Medication:

  1. Obtain oral glucose

Ensure the “six rights” of medication administration:

  1. “Right” patient
  2. “Right” medication
  3. “Right” dose
  4. “Right” route
  5. “Right” time
  6. “Right” documentation
  7. Check expiration date

Obtain Medical Direction:

  1. Obtain order from online medical control to administer oral glucose
  2. Ensure that patient is conscious, can swallow and protect own airway
  3. Place glucose on tongue depressor between cheek and gum or squirt from tube between patient’s cheek and gum
  4. Slowly administer small amounts over 2-3 minutes Reassess level of consciousness, respiratory status and patient response
  5. Reassess level of consciousness, respiratory status and patient response
