Valium

Diazepam
Approved for Rescue Paramedics Only

Protocol 1023

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 201, 509, 714.

Anticonvulsant / Sedative

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Suppresses Seizure Activity Through Motor Cortex of the Brain.
2. Effective Skeletal Muscle Relaxant.

INDICATIONS

1. Major Motor Seizures - Status Epilepticus.
2. Skeletal Muscle Relaxant in Isolated Orthopedic Injury.
3. Example: Femur Fracture and Severe Muscle Spasms.
4. Severe Back Pain With Prolonged Transport.

CONTRAINDICATIONS

1. Pregnancy.
2. Known Hypersensitivity.

SIDE EFFECTS

1. Respiratory Depression/Arrest.
2. Hypotension.
3. Nausea/Vomiting.

Considerations

1. Valium is Incompatible With Many Medications. Flush Line Before and After Use.
2. For Dosage Amounts Above 5mg - Medical Control Must be Contacted.

Dosage and Administration

Adult Dose

Bradycardic with Transcutaneous Pacing

1. IV/IO - 5-10mg Every 5 Minutes to the Desired Effect or Max Dose of 15mg.
2. Rectally- Same Dosage.

Anxiety Control:

1. IV/IO - 2.5-5mg Every 10 Minutes to the Desired Effect or Max Dose of 10mg.
2. Rectally Same Dosage.

Peds Dose

Anxiety Control:

1. IV 0.1mg/kg (Max Dose of 5mg).

2. Rectally 0.3mg/kg PR Max Dose 10mg.

Seizure

1. IV/IO - 0.1mg/kg (Max Dose of 5mg).

2. Rectally 0.3mg/kg PR (Max Dose 10mg).
