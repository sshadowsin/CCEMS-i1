TOXIC EXPOSURE - CYANIDE

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. Scene and patient management
  2. Safely and rapidly remove patient from source of exposure.
  3. Determine estimation of exposure time.
  4. Request HazMat response as appropriate.

  5. Industries in which to consider cyanide exposure:

    • Electroplating and Metallurgy
    • Organic chemicals production.
    • Photographic developing.
    • Manufacture of plastics.
    • Fumigation of ships.

    • Mining processes, including gold/copper.

  6. Patients and EMS providers may be exposed to cyanide in the following ways;

    • Breathing air, drinking water, touching soil, or eating foods that contain cyanide.
    • Smoking cigarettes and breathing smoke-filled air during fires are major sources of cyanide exposure.

    • Breathing air near a hazardous waste site containing cyanide.

  7. Be alert for exposure related signs and symptoms;

    • Acute dyspnea/tachypnea without cyanosis.
    • Nausea/vomiting.
    • Seizures.
    • Hyper or hypotension.
    • Total body erythema. (redness).

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam with full set of VS.
  2. Assess ABC's for evidence of apnea, airway reflex compromise or difficulty in ventilatory efforts.
  3. Assess medical conditions, burns or traumatic injuries that have the potential to compromise the airway.
  4. Administer high flow oxygen by 100% nonrebreather mask immediately and continuously.
  5. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  6. Identify and treat underlying reversible medical conditions (narcotic overdose, hypoglycemia, etc.).
  7. In general, maintain an oxygen saturation 90-94%.
  8. Always insure proper care of the C-Spine during Airway treatment.
  9. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress-Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  10. Maintain a ventilatory rate appropriate to age.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Perform a 12 ECG if possible.
  4. Maintain an oxygen saturation 90-94%.
  5. Establish vascular access-treat hypotension if indicated otherwise set at TKO rate or NS lock.
  6. Contact OLMC and transport.
  7. Normal pulse oximetry readings may be found in the face of severe cyanide poisoning.
  8. Consider carbon monoxide poisoning in patients exposed to closed space fire and smoke.
  9. For industrial exposures, request the Safety Datat Sheet (SDS) for the chemical involved and bring this to the ED.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verfy endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector.
  4. No nasotracheal intubation in a pediatric patient.
  5. Consider sedation after intubation ( Benzodiazepine) considering Protocol 509: Pain Control, Nausea, Vomiting, Anxiety.
  6. After TWO unsuccessful attempts at endotracheal intubation use a supraglottic airway device or BVM with appropriate oral/nasal airway.
