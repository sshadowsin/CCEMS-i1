ALLERGIC REACTION / ANAPHYLAXIS

INDICATIONS

  1. Patients with systemic signs/symptoms of allergic reation: hives, generalized itching or flushing, upper airway irritation or blockage, wheezing, hypotension.
  2. Patients with local reaction (itching, redness, swelling) to known allergies.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. If the patient has any respiratiory distress and is conscious, allow them to achieve a position of comfort, including leaving a child in their parents lap.
  3. ASSESS AIRWAY PATENCY and adequacy of air exchange - provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airway.
  4. Always insure proper care of the C-Spine during Airway treatment.
  5. ASSIST VENTILATION and provide OXYGEN as needed. Maintain pulse ox>94%.
  6. Keep the patient NPO. Stop any tube feedings.
  7. Safely and rapidly eliminate the source of exposure, as able.
  8. Apply cold pack to bite or sting site.
  9. Give or assist patient with Epipen (0.3mg) IM for respiratory distress and/or shock from anaphylaxis - contact OLMC prior to administering any medication.
  10. Assist patient with own Albuterol inhaler according to the prescription on the inhaler - contact OLMC prior to administering any medication.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure all above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring, when available.
  3. If the patient is stable and is >50 years old, has a history of Coronary Artery Disease, or if the patient is tachycardic obtain a 12 Lead ECG and transmit to on-line medical control prior to administering epinephrine.
  4. Contact OLMC for medication orders.
  5. Epinephrine 1:1000 0.3 mg SQ or IM X 1 dose if:
    a, Severe respiratory distress (bronchospasm, upper airway swelling).
    b. Hypotension or other evidence of poor perfusion.
  6. If giving Epinephrine IM them ensure adequate needle size (1" for deltoid - 1 1/2" for rectus femoris) Be sure to landmark appropriately to avoid nerves and blood vessels, Be sure to aspirate prior to injecting to avoid accidentally giving this into the blood vessel. If blood is returned on aspiration then discard the syringe and redraw the Epinephrine into a new syringe with a clean needle.
  7. Contac Medical Control for additional dose of Epinephrine 1:1000. If symptoms persist, may repeat every 10 minutes to max total of 1.2 mg.
  8. If significant WHEEZING is present:
    a. Albuterol 2.5 mg every 10 minutes via nebulization with oxygen @ 6 L/min. for bronchospasm/wheezing until symptoms subside.
  9. If STRIDOR is present:
    a. Epinephrine (1:1000) 2 ml mixed with 3 mlof NS via nebulizer.
  10. Establish IV access as soon as posisble - IV SALINE TKO unless hypotensive (<100 Hg SBP) then give 500cc bolus repeat as needed to 1 liter in an adult and 20 cc/kg in a pediatric patient, may repeat 20 cc/kg with OLMC permisison.
  11. Diphenhydramine (Benadryl) Adult: 25mg IV/IO/IM for moderate to severe allergic reaction if adult may repeat to maximum of 50 mg.
  12. Diphenhydramine (Benadryl) Pediatric: 1 mg/kg IV/IO/IM for moderate to severe allergic reaction maximum dose 25 mg.
  13. REASSESS AIRWAY PATENCY, vitals, lung sounds, ECG frequently while enroute.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure all above is complete
  2. INTUBATE in severe cases, or if patient is unresponsive especially if no improvement after Epinephrine .
  3. If patient becomes agitated with increased LOC after intubation may give: ADULT: Versed 2-5 mg slow IVP adult every 10 minutes to the desired effect or max dose of 10 mg. PEDS: Versed 0.1 mg/kg peds with max does 5 mg SLOW IV.
  4. Re-contact Medical Control for additional doses of Versed.
