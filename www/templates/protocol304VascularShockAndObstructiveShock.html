Vascular Shock and Obstructive Shock

Patients who meet shock criteria with findings suggestive of:

  1. Sepsis.
  2. Drug Ingestion (e.g., tricyclic antidepressants).
  3. Hypoxia.
  4. Toxic Exposure (e.g., carbon monoxide).
  5. Tension Pneumothorax.
  6. Cardiac Tamponade.

Consider Shock in patients with one or more of the following:

  1. Vital signs: HR >100, SBP of <90 mmHg for adults, SBP <70+(age in years x 2) mmHg for peds, or RR >20 BPM.
  2. Skin signs: cold clammy skin, febrile, or flash or delayed capillary refill.
  3. Mental staus: altered, lethargic, or irritable (esp. in infants).
  4. Evaluate for the source including infection, bleeding/trauma, neurologic or cardiac.
  5. Pregnancy >20 weeks gestation _ Transport in patrial L lateral decubitus position. Place multipule pillows under patient's right hip and shoulder to elevate R side 45 degrees.
  6. Signs of a Tension Pneumothorax: Sudden chest pain, shortness of breath, cyanosis, hypotension, tachypnea, distended neck veins, breath sounds absent on the affected side, tracheal deviation away from the affected side.
  7. Signs of Cardia Tamponade: Anxiety, chest pain (radiates to back/shoulder/abdomen and is stabbing in nature), dyspnea cyanosis, hypotension, weak or absent pulses.

EMT

Focused history and physical exam

  1. Blood glucose, oxygen saturation and temperature assessment.
  2. C-spine immobilization, if indicated.
  3. Place patient SUPINE and elevate legs (except in case of spinal injury).
  4. OXYGEN by nonrebreather mask at 15 L/MIN.
  5. ASSIST VENTILATION if neccessary.
  6. Keep paitent warm.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete
  2. Use 2 large bore IV's-, resuscitate with warm IV fluids when available.
  3. Continuous cardiac monitor, oximetry, Etco2-maintain saturation >94% or CO2 35-45.
  4. 12 Lead ECG if available.
  5. Begin transport as soon as posisble and contact Medical Command.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Advanced airway as appropriate.
  3. Consider needle decompression for tension pneumothorax if indicated (shock with chest trauma and appropriate symptoms).
Medical Control Option - Must contact prior to administration

1. Sodium Bicarb for Tricyclic antidepressant overdose.
2. Glucagon for beta blocker.
