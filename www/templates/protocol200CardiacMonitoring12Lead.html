Cardiac Monitoring - 12 Lead

ADVANCED EMT/PARAMEDIC

INDICATIONS:

12 Lead ECG (If available) on the following patients

  1. Pain in chest or upper abdomen
  2. Cardiac dysrhythmia- palpitations, irregular heartbeat, or other complaints of possible cardiac origin
  3. Syncope or near syncope
  4. Acute general weakness
  5. Acute dyspnea or shortness of breath
  6. Post arrest if spontaneous circulation returns
  7. Concern for ACS presentation
  8. Patients with pulse < 60 or > 100
  9. Hypotension.

Acquire and transmit EKG

  1. All ECGs suggestive of acute MI should be immediately transmitted to a STEMI/PCI Receiving Center

PROTOCOL:

  1. All patients with above indications shall have continuous cardiac monitoring from time of arrival on scene until transfer to E.D. staff.
  2. EKG strips should accompany patient and note:
    1. Arrival at patient
    2. Response to each intervention if there are changes in the patient's' clinical status.
    3. Arrival at hospital or completion of patient encounter.
  3. Describe all rhythms to command physician.
  4. Obtain 12 lead ECG as appropriate and transmit to OLMC when able.
