TOXIC SUBSTANCE EXPOSURE-OVERDOSE

History suggestive of exposure to a toxic substance or accidental-intentional overdose of any medication or drug.

EMT

  1. Ensure scene SAFETY.
  2. Focused history and physical exam
  3. Assess blood glucose, temperature, and oxygen saturation.
  4. Assess the time and circumstances of the ingestion. Document evidence of suicide attempt or deliberate attempt at self-harm.
  5. Assess scene for additional information on toxins, poisons, medications or other possible concerns.
  6. Retain any pill bottles, open containers, or potential chemicals that may have been ingested.
  7. Retain suicide notest or other pre-ingestion communications.
  8. Provide basic airway maneuvers at all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  9. Apply oxygen to maintain oxygen saturation >90%
  10. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress, consider a nasal or oral airway.
  11. Recommended pediatric ventilatory rates:
    a. Infant (0-12 month): 25 breaths per minute.
    b. 1-3 yrs: 20 breaths per minute.
    c. 4-6 yrs: 15 breaths per minute.
    d. >6 years: 12 (same as adult)
  12. Do not hyperventilate the patient.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is compete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Do not give Activated Charcoal prior to OLMC consultation and agreement.
  4. Consider a 12 Lead ECG.
  5. Adult: Naloxone 1-2 mg (per dose) IV/IM/IO or intranasal (atomizer). May repeat x 1 for suspected narcotic overdose.
  6. Pediatric: Naloxone 0.1 mg/kg (max 1 mg) IV/IM/IO or intranasal atomizer for suspected narcotic overdose-contact OLMC for additional doses.
  7. Monitor and TRANSPORT - Patients who have attempted suicide by overdose CANNOT be released withoutonline medical control approval and MAY be taken in against their will considering Protocol 801: Emergency Care to the Suicidal Patient. Police may need to assist in ensuring the transport.
  8. Bring in materials and/or containers of products ingested or exposed.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure all above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation unsing an endotracheal tube when indicated.
  3. Document 2 confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector)
  4. No nasotracheal intubation.
Medical Control Options - must contact prior to administration

PARAMEDIC

  1. NG tube insertion/lavage,
  2. Sodium Bicarb IV/IO push for tricyclic antidepressant overdose with sustained HR >120 bpm, QRS>0.10 secs, hypotension unresponsive to fluids, or ventricular dysrhythmias: Sodium Bicarb 1 mEq/kg slow IV push
  3. Adult Epinephrine gtt per protocol
  4. Administer Activated Charcoal by orders of OLMC only: Do not give for luquid ingestion or hydrocarbon ingestions.
  5. Adults: Activated Charcoal 25 grams by mouth if paitent is alert, awake and gag reflex is intact.
  6. Pediatrics: Activated Charcoal 1g/kg up to 25 grams by mouth if the patient is alert, awake and gag reflex is intact.
