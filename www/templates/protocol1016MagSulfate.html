Medical Control Option - Must Contact Prior to Administration

Mechanism of Action

Magnesium is a cofactor in more than 300 enzyme systems that regulate diverse biochemical reactions in the body, including protein synthesis, muscle and nerve function, blood glucose control, and blood pressure regulation. Magnesium is required for energy production, oxidative phosphorylation, and glycolysis. It contributes to the structural development of bone and is required for the synthesis of DNA, RNA, and the antioxidant glutathione. Magnesium also plays a role in the active transport of calcium and potassium ions across cell membranes, a process that is important to nerve impulse conduction, muscle contraction, and normal heart rhythm. As an anticonvulsant, reduces muscle contractions by interfering with release of acetylcholine at myoneural junction.

INDICATIONS
1. Eclampsia (Seizures Accompanying Pregnancy).
2. Cardiac Arrest if Torsades De Pointes or Suspected Hypomagnesemia is Present.
3. To Suppress Uterine Contractions in High Risk Labor.

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Do Not Give to Any Patient With Heart Block or Recent MI.
2. Shock.
3. Persistent Severe Hypertension.
4. Patients Undergoing Routine Dialysis.

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Hypotension With Rapid Administration.
2. Bradycardia.
3. Sweating/Flushing.
4. Respiratory Depression.

Considerations
1. Constant Cardiac Monitoring.
2. Slow IV/IO Infusion.
3. Monitor Vital Signs Frequently.

Dosage and Administration

Cardiac Arrest due to Torsades De Pointes
1. ADULT: Magnesium Sulfate 1-2gm diluted in 10mL NS give over 5 minutes IV/IO.
2. Pediatric: (<15 years of Age) For pediatic patients who are pregnant and having a seizure contact OLMC for dosage.

Medical Control Option - Must Contact Prior to Administration

Bronchospasm/Wheezing: Contact OLMC prior to administration

High-Risk preterm labor when delivery is imminent, to suppress uterine contractions
1. Magnesium Sulfate 1-gram diluted in 10mL NS give over 5 minutes. IV titrate per Medical Control Direction.
2. For Females with advanced pregnancy and seizures: Magnesium Sulfate: 2-4 grams IM or 2-4 grams in 10mL of NS infuse over 15-30 minutes IV, as directed by OLMC.
