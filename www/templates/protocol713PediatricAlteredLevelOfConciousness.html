PEDIATRIC ALTERED LEVEL OF CONSCIOUSNESS

GOALS:

The primary goal in a patient who has a serious alteration in consciousness is AIRWAY PROTECTION. Protective reflexes such as cough and gag may be lost and aspiration is an ever-present danger in these patients and can be lethal. The specific goals of protection may be stated as:

  1. To protect the patient from physical or environmental harm.
  2. To protect the patient's brain from injury due to lack of oxygen or glucose.
  3. To protect the patient from aspiration.

INDICATIONS:

  1. Alteration in normal mental status including:
    a. Confusion.
    b. Unusual behavior.
    c. Unresponsiveness to verbal or painful stimulus.

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Head injury or other trauma.
  2. shock or serious cariac arrhythmia.
  3. Apnea, cyanosis, obtundation with hypoventilation or respiratory disress.
  4. If any of above present, go immediately to appropriate portocol.
  5. AEIOUTIPPS: Possible causes of Altered Mental Status:
A - ALCOHOL T - TRAUMA/TEMP
E - ELECTROLYTES I - INFECTION
I - INSULIN P - PSYCHOGENIC
O - OPIATES P - POISON
U - UREMIA S - SHOCK/SEIZURE

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Blood glucose, Oxygen Saturation and Temperature assessment.
  3. Assess for trauma.
  4. Assess for stroke.
  5. Asses for possible overdose, substance abuse or other potential toxin. Evaluate the scene for supportive evidence.
  6. Treat any underlying enviornmental or toxin exposurse.
  7. Gather and collect any evidence on scene that may assist in the treatment of the patient.
  8. Consider non-accidental trauma especially in pediatric and elderly patients.
  9. Consider hypoglycemia, especially in pediatric patients.
  10. Assure AIRWAY PATENCY with C-Spine control (if indicated).
  11. BREATHING - oxygen (mask, nasal cannula, BVM), suction and assist ventilation as needed to mantain SPO2>94%.
  12. CIRCULATION- monitor pulse, blood pressure, respiratory rate.
  13. Establish baseline data:
    a. Pupil size and reactivity.
    b. Level of responsiveness to verbal, tactile, painful stimuli.
    c. Muscle tone.
    d. Calculate Pediatric Glasgow Coma Score.
    e. History of diabetes - If blood glucose is less than 60 mg/dl, and the patient is sufficiently alert to swallow and protect airway, five oral glucose, orange juice, or other sugared drink.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Obtain a 12 Lead ECG and transmit to receiving facility.
  4. Establish vascular access.
  5. If evidence of poor perfusion, give NS 20 mg/kl IV.
  6. Contact OLMC for medical orders:
    a. For suspected narcotic overdose - Naloxone 0.1 mg/kg IV.IM.IO.IN.
    b. May repeat once - Max dose 1 mg.
  7. If blood glucose is less than 60 mg/dl:
    a. Give D10W 2 ml/kg (200 mg/kg) for neonates <30 dats.
    b. Infants up to 1 year: Dextrose D50 10% (D10NS) 5 mL/kg IV/IO.
    c. Children greater than 1 year: Dextrose D50 25% (D25W) 2 mL/kg IV/IO.
  8. NOTE:
    a. To make D10 - Dilute 10 mL D50 in 40 mL of NS.
    b. To make D25 - Dilute D50 1:1 with NS.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is compete.
  2. Consider Chemical Restraints as needed to protect the patient and/or personnel.
  3. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endortacheal tube when indicated.
  4. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  5. No nasotracheal intubateion.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Consider Chemical Restraints as needed to protect the patient and/or personnel using. _ Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety.
  2. Contact the receiving facility.
