Ceasing Resuscitative Efforts

INDICATIONS:

  1. Patients in cardiac arrest for whom cardiac resuscitation has been initiated but has not led to spontaneous circulation.

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Hypothermia - see Protocol 105: HYPOTHERMIC CARDIAC ARREST
  2. Trauma arrest - see Protocol 104: TRAUMATIC CARDIAC ARREST
  3. Patients who meet DOA criteria Protocol 902: DEATH DETERMINATION AND PRONOUNCEMENT PROCESS

Resuscitation efforts may be discontinued if conditions are met AND the Command Physician authorizes the discontinuation.

Treatment Plan

CPR should NOT be started in a pulseless, apneic patient in the presence of:

  1. Obvious death, decomposition, and/or rigor mortis.
  2. Obvious fatal wounds without signs of life
  3. A verbal order pronouncing the patient dead by a licensed physician in the State of Utah who is present on scene.
  4. An order by the OLMC physician.
  5. A Do Not Resuscitate (DNR) written order, bracelet, or necklace from any U.S. State.
  6. A signed Physician Order for Life-Sustaining Treatment (POLST form) from any U.S. State indicating that the patient does not desire resuscitative efforts.

Termination of CPR may be done in the following circumstances:

  1. A valid DNR or POLST form is discovered after resuscitative efforts were initiated.
  2. Resuscitation efforts initiated when criteria to not resuscitate were present.
  3. A verbal order pronouncing the patient dead by a licensed physician in the State of Utah who is present on scene per State Bureau of EMS Rule 426-4-600
  4. Order by the OLMC physician.
  5. Current rhythm is asystole.
  6. No sustained spontaneous circulation (palpable pulse for > 1 min) within the last 20 minutes.
  7. No potentially reversible condition identified (e.g. antidepressant overdose).
  8. Patient has received adequate trial of advanced life support interventions as determined by Command Physician.
  9. Age > 18.

NOTE:

  1. Command Physicians may use discretion to cease resuscitation after less than 30 minutes if they determine cardiac unresponsiveness after trial of ACLS appropriate to clinical situation.
  2. It is strongly recommended that a decision be made to either transport or cease resuscitation at scene once resuscitation efforts have persisted for 30 minutes. Transport may be initiated sooner if logistics permit.
