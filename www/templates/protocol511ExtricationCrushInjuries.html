EXTRICATION / CRUSH INJURIES

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. All patients who are trapped, impaled or who are at risk of the crush syndrome.
  2. Crush Syndromes should be considered for the following:
    a. Entrapped/compressed patients under a load for more than 30 minutes.
    b. Patients with little or no movement for more than 4 hours (e.g. older patient falls, overdoses).
  3. Do not remove impaled object.
  4. Anticipate possible cardiac arrest upon extrication.

EMT

  1. Victims will often develop hypo or hyperthermia with prolonged environmental exposure - prevent heat loss as able.
  2. Focused history and physical exam.
  3. ASSURE safety of all personnel at scene.
  4. OXYGEN face mask at 15L as needed to maintain pulse ox >94% ASSIST VENTILATION.
  5. SUPPORT cervical spine with rigid collar and/or manual stabilization if indicated (neck pain or tenderness or mechanism of injury).
  6. PROTECT PATIENT from weather, rescue operations and other hazards as possible.
  7. Upon extrication, IMMOBILIZE on long-board with rigid cervical collar and head immobilization device if any potential of spinal injury.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Continuous ECG, ETC02, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  2. 12 Lead ECG when available, after extrication.
  3. Initiate IV x2 if able or hep lock, rate TKO unless hypotensive then 500 ml boluses as needed up to 2 L.
  4. During extrication, monitor vitals, level of consciousness, respiratoy and cardiovascular status and ECG as possible.
  5. Expedite transport provide update on patient status while enroute.
  6. Immediately prior to the extrication consider use of Sodium Bicarbonate as indicated below in medical control options.

CONSIDERATIONS:
1. Use of ANALGESIA when appropriate: use Protocol 509: Pain Control, Nausea, Vomiting, Anxiety

PARAMEDIC

  1. Patients often quickly develop elevated levels of potassium. Watch for the follwoing ECG changes as signs of Hyperkalemia: peaked T waves, prolonged PR intervals, ST segment depression, QRS widening, heart blocks, and ventricular arrhythmias.
  2. INTUBATE as appropriate.
  3. If patient becomes agitated with increasing LOC after intubation may give Versed 2-5 mg slow IV with Medical Control permission.

Medical Control Option

  1. Constant crush injuries greater than 30 minutes duration and if the patient develops cardiac arrhythmias or a prolonged QRS >100ms:
    a. Administer Sodium Bicarb 1 mEq/kg (Maximum of 100 mEq) IV push prior to beginning infusion and up to TWO additional times plus start Sodium Bicarb drip: 1 mEq/kg (Maximum 100 mEq) in 1000 ml NS and run wide open.
