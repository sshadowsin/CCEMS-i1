CHEST TRAUMA

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical examination.
  2. Assess ABC's for evidence of apnea, airway reflex compromise or difficulty in ventilatory efforts.
  3. Provide basic airway meneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  4. Provide 100% oxygen to the patient-maintain an oxygen saturation of 90-94%
  5. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratoy distress. Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  6. Maintain a ventilatry rate of 10-12 breaths per minute.
  7. Suction the patient appropriately.
  8. DO not hyperventilate the patient.
  9. Always insure proper care of the C-Spine during Airway treatment.
  10. Apply direct pressure to any obvious external hemorrhage.
  11. Cover open chest wounds with occlusive dressing,
  12. Seal wound with occlusive dressing at end of exhalation. Leave one side of dressing untaped to function as a one-way pressure relief valve.
  13. MONITOR FOR TENSION PNEUMOTHORAX: Decreasing blood pressure associated with worsening dyspnea, unilateral decreased breath sounds, and increasing resistance to ventilation.
  14. Immobilize any obvious injuries and penetrating object, do not remove penetrating objects.
  15. Maintain warmth to minimize heat loss. Monitor for shock and hypovolemia. Assess mental status prior to and every 15 mintues during transport (GCS/AVPU).
  16. Pediatric lowes acceptable systolic blood pressures are:
    a. Birth to 1 month = 60 mmHg.
    b. 1 month to 1 year = 70 mmHg.
    c. 1 year to 10 years = 70 mmHg + (age x 2).
    d. Over 10 years = 90 mmHg.
  17. Cover lacerations or puncture wounds to the neck near the great veessesls or trachea with an occlusive dressing.
  18. Stabilize the flail segment using pillow, folded sheet or towel and tape.
  19. The impaled objects should be stabilized in place and covered with dry sterile dressings exceptions would be:
    a. Objects through the cheek where there is the possibility of airway compromise.
    b. Objects that would interfere with chest compressions.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETC02, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. In general, maintain an oxygen saturation 90-94% and ETC02 of 35-45 mmHg.
  4. Establish vascular access x 2 if able with large bore cannula, give NS at TKO rate unless hypotensive then give 500ml boluses at need to max of 3 liters.
  5. Follow additional trauma protocols as appropriate.
  6. If traumatic arrest insert Advanced Airway
  7. Treat for pain and anxiety per Protocol 509: Pain Control, Nausea, Vomiting, Anxiety.
  8. Contact OLMC and rapid transport per destination protocol.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Suspected Tension Pneumothorax-immediate needle decompression of affected side.
  3. Traumatic Arrest-Follow Protocol 104: TRAUMATIC CARDIAC ARREST.
  4. INTUBATION - Consider orothracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated.
  5. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. ETC02, CO2 Detection Device or Esophogeal Intubation Detector).
  6. Consider sedation after intubation ( Benzodiazephone)
  7. Are TWO unsuccessful attempts to endotracheal intubation use a supraglottic airway device or BVM with approptriate oral/nasal airway.
  8. Surgical Airway-Cricothyrotomy:
    a. Consider when all other methods of oxygenation, ventilation and securing the airway have failed.
    b. Use department approved kit
