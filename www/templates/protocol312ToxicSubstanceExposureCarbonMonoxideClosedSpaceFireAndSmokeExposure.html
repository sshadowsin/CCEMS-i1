TOXIC SUBSTANCE EXPOSURE-CARBON ONOXIDE/CLOSED SPACE FIRE AND SMOKE EXPOSURE

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. Scene and patient management
  2. Safely and rapidly remove patient from source of exposure.
  3. Pulse oximetry readings are inaccurate in the face of CO poisoning.
  4. Determine estimation of exposure time.
  5. Patients exposed to closed space fires are at risk for both carbon monoxide and cyanide poisoning.
  6. Pregnant patients who have been exposed should be transported.
  7. Provide early notification to recieving ED of possible CO and/or Cyanide poisoning.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam with full set of VS.
  2. Assess ABC's for evidence of spnea, airway reflex compromise or difficulty in ventilatory efforts.
  3. Assess medical conditions, burns or traumatic injuries that have the ootential to compromise the airway.
  4. Administer high flow oxygen by 100% nonrebreather mask immediately and continuously.
  5. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  6. Identify and treat underlying reversible medical conditions (narcotic overdoes, hypogycemia, etc.).
  7. in general, maintain an oxygen saturation >94%.
  8. Always insure proper care of the C-Spine during Airway treatment.
  9. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress-Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  10. Maintain a ventilatory rate appropriate to age.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Perform a 12 lead ECG if possible.
  4. Maintain an oxygen saturation >94% and ETCO2 of 35-45 mmHg.
  5. Obtain a Rad 57 CO level (>5 shows possible exposure).
  6. Establish vascular access-treat hypotension if indicated otherwise set at TKO rate.
  7. Contact OLMC and transport.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Consider Advanced Airway.-Consider orothracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated.
  3. Document 2 confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector.
  4. No nasotracheal intubation in a pediatric patient.
  5. Consider sedation after intubation (benzodiazepine) considering Protocol 509: Pain Control, Nausea, Vomiting, Anxiety.
  6. After 2 unseccessful attempts at endotracheal intubation use a superglottic airway device or BVM with appropriate oral/nasal airway,
