PEDIATRIC RESPIRATORY DISTRESS

All Providers

  1. Contact Medical Control as soon as airway obstruction is recognized or suspected.
  2. At any point if the patient becomes unconscious or respiratory failure occurs, go to Protocol 703: Pediatric Respiratory Failure.

INDICATIONS:

Patients with evidence of respiratory distress, including:

  1. Elevated respiratory rate - elevated heart rate.
  2. Retractions - nasal flaring.
  3. Cyanosis (late sign).
  4. Toxicity (looks ill).
  5. Anxious or listless.

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. History of foreign body - Go to Protocol 701: Pediatric Airway Obstruction.
  2. Urticaria or wheezing with hypotension - Got to Protocol 704: Pediatric Anaphylaxis.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess ABC's for evidence of apnea, airway reflex compromise or difficulty in ventilatroy effort.
  3. Assess medical conditions, burns or traumatic injuries that have the potential to compromise the airway.
  4. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  5. Identify and treat underlying reversible medical conditions (narcotic overdose, hypoglycemia, etc.).
  6. In general, maintain and oxygen saturation 90-94%
  7. Always insure proper care of the C-Spine during Airway treatment.
  8. Keep the patient NPO - Stop any tube feedings and do not use feeding tube during resuscitation.
  9. Infants and young children are primary nose breathers-Suction oral and nasal passages as needed to keep clear.
  10. Provide 100% oxygen to the patient.
  11. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting repiratory distress. Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  12. Recommended pediatric ventilaory rates:
    a. Infant (0-12 months): 25 breaths per minutes.
    b. 1-3 years: 20 breaths per minutes.
    c. 4-6 years: 15 breaths per minutes.
    d. >6 years: 12 (same as adult).
  13. Do not hyperventilate the patient.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. WHEEZING - MILD TO MODERATE BREATHING PROBLEMS:
    a. Albuterol 2.5 mg in NSS 3cc nebulized with oxygen at 6L/min-may repeat in 20 minutes.
    b. if severe distress, notify Medical Control immediately for order for Epinephrine.
  4. STRIDOR: Nebulized NS mist.
  5. Monitor, keep warm and transport parent and child; allow the child to assume a position of comfort.
  6. Establish IV access if able.
  7. Initiate transport and contact Medical Control.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is compete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. After 2 unsuccessful attempts at endotracheal inubation use a suparglottic airway device or BVM with appropriate oral/nasal airway.
  5. Consider sedation after intubation (benzodiazephine)-contact OLMC
  6. No nasotracheal intubation.
  7. Tracheostomy Assistance:
    a. Provide supplemental oxygen.
    b. Suction the patient appropriately.
    c. Replace Tracheostomy tube if needed.
    d. If unable to ventilate, pass an appropriately sized ETT through the stoma 1-2 inches.
    e. If unable to pass a tracheostomy tube or endotracheal tube, use BVM, supraglottic airway device, orrotracheal intubation to ventilate patient.
  8. Ventilator Managemet:
    a. Work with the family to troubleshoot the machine.
    b. Address tracheostomy as above.
    c. If you need to disconnect for transport provide adequate BVM ventilations similar to home respiratory rate settings.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Sedation after intubation (benzodiazepine).
  2. Surgical Airway - Cricothyrotomy.
    a. Consider only when all other methods of oxygenation, ventilation and securing the airway have failed.
    b. Insert an appropriately sized endotracheal tube and secure.
    c. Contact OLMC for further instructions as needed.
  3. If severe distress and barky cough (suspected croup): NEBULIZED Epinephrine 2.0mg (2,0 ml Epinephrine 1:1,000) diluted in additional 3 ml NS via inhalation.
  4. Epinephrine 1:1,000: 0.01mg/kg (0.1 lm/kg) IM x 1 dose. If giving EPI IM then ensure adequate needle size - 1" for deltoid, rectus femoris. Be sure to landmark appropriately to avoid nerves and blod vessels. Be sure to aspirate prior to injectin to avoid accidentally giving this into a blood vessel. If blood is returned on aspiration then discard the syringe and redraw the EPI into a new syringe with a clean needle.
  5. Additional Albuterol or Epinephrine .
