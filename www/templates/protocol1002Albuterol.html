Albuterol

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1002

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 310, 401, 702, 704

Class: Bronchodilator

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Causes prompt bronchodilation
2. Relaxes bronchial smooth muscle
3. Beta-2-Selective

INDICATIONS
1. Drug of choice to acute asthmatic attack, reactive airway states, or when audible wheezing is noted and oxygen is <95% on room air.
2. Acute exacerbation of chronic bronchitis or emphysema.
3. Acute inhalation injury with associated wheezing.

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Hypersensitivity
2. Hypertension unless approved by OLMC
3. Concurrent chest pain (obtain a 12 lead and contact OLMC)

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Tachycardia
2. Tremors
3. Dizziness

Dosage and Administration

ADULTS AND CHILDREN

2.5mg Prefilled Bullet

NEBULIZER Shall be given via nebulizer or specialized mask.
a. Set oxygen @ 6L/Min to deliver drug.
b. May repeat x1 in 15 minutes if needed.
c. Monitor for compliance and effectiveness.

CONSIDERATIONS

1. Hold if baseline heart rate >100 and increases more than 20BPM.
2. Hold if systolic blood pressure >180 or diastolic BP >100
3. Monitor vital signs before and after treatment.
