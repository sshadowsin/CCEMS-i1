Administration of Nitroglycerin

This protocol is for the EMT First Responder only.

Determine Need for Nitroglycerin:

  1. Refer Protocol 300: CHEST PAIN (ACUTE CORONARY SYNDROME)
  2. Determine if patient has cardiac history and is prescribed nitroglycerin
  3. Determine if patient exhibits signs and symptoms of cardiac emergency
  4. Sudden onset of sweating
  5. Squeezing, pressure, or pain in chest radiating to jaw, neck, shoulders or arms
  6. Anxiety, irritability, appears to be in shock
  7. Obtain order from online medical control to administer nitroglycerine per protocol

Check for Contraindications:

  1. Hypotension
  2. Hypovolemia
  3. Viagra or similar drugs

Inquire About Previous Doses:

  1. Total doses taken/administered
  2. When last dose taken/administered
  3. Effects of previous dose
  4. Any other interventions

Obtain nitroglycerin:

  1. Ensure the “six rights” of medication administration:
  2. Right” patient - prescription is for this patient
  3. Right” medication - it is Nitroglycerin (trade names) Nitrostat, Nitrolingual
  4. Right” dose- one tablet or one spray
  5. Right” route- Sublingual(SL) under the tongue
  6. Right” time - expiration date
  7. Right” documentation
  8. Check if medication is discolored or flaky
  9. Assure Blood Pressure is Appropriate
  10. Systolic must be above 100 mmHg
  11. Obtain order from online medical control

Contact OLMC prior to administering for order

Administer according to patient prescription:

  1. Ask patient to lift tongue
  2. Have patient or EMT place tablet or spray dose under tongue
  3. Have patient keep mouth closed until dissolved
  4. Tell patient not to chew or swallow
  5. Administer one spray or tablet every five minutes
  6. if pain is not relieved, and systolic blood pressure remains above 100 mmHg and you are authorized by medical control may repeat NTG up to a maximum of three doses

Reassess:

  1. Reassess patient's blood pressure
  2. Do not give if systolic BP < 100
  3. Reassess patient's response to medication
