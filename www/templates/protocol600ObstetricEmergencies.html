OBSTETRIC EMERGENCIES

Definitions:

  1. Crowning: Bulging of fetal head through perineum such that the cervix is stretched around the entire circumference of the fetal head. This should be evaluated during a contraction.
  2. Eclampsia: Pre-eclampsia associated with generalized seizures; seizure may be preceded by hyper-reflexia and/or visual changes.
  3. First trimester: First three months of gregnancy.
  4. Hypertension of pregnancy: Blood pressure 140/90 in pregnant women not previously hypertensive; usually occurs in third trimester or first pregnancy and may progress to pre-eclampsia or eclampsia.
  5. Labor Regular, rhythmic crampy pains in back and abdomen due to uterine contractions which occur at least every 5 minutes and last at least 30 seonds. The three stages of labor are:
    a. Cervical dilation
    b. Delivery of fetus.
    d. Delivery of placenta.
  6. Malpresentation: When presenting part (i.e. first part of fetus to deliver out the birth canal) is not the head.
  7. Meconium: Fetal feces present in amniotic fluid; if aspirated by fetus can cause airway obstruction and/or severe pulmonary complications.
  8. Placenta Previa: Part of all of the placenta lies over cervical opening of uterus; this may lead to catastrophic hemorrhage (without pain) when labor begins or if disrupted by manual examination.
  9. Placental Abruption (abruptio placentae): Premature separation of placenta from uterine wall, usually in late third trimester or early labor, very painful; bleeding may be contained inside uterus; may lead to catastrophic hemorrhage.
  10. Pre-eclampsia: Condition of unknown etiology which generally occurs in theird trimester of first pregnancy, involving new onset of hypertenstion (>140/90) during pregnancy associated with protein loss in urine and edema of hands and face. May lead to renal, liver and CNS dysfunction.
  11. Rupture of membranes: Painless breakage of amniotic sac prior to birth leading to small, uncontrollable gush of fluid from vagina. Also called "bloody show" or "water broke".
