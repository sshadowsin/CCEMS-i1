BLEEDING/ABDOMINAL PAIN DURING PREGNANCY

  1. Bleeding or abdominal pain during any stage of pregnancy may involve life-threatening hemorrhage.
  2. Etiology depends on the stage of pregnancy:
    a. First trimester: Ectopic pregnancy, miscarriage.
    b. Third trimester: Placenta-previa, placental abruption.

INDICATIONS:

Patient in any stage of pregnancy who has vaginal bleeding or steady (non-labor) abdominal pain.

BASIC

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Obtain full set of vital signs.
  3. Take OBSTETRICAL HISTORY per Protocol 600: Obstetric Emergencies.
  4. Place supine (right side elevated if >20 weeks gestation).
  5. VISUALLY EXAMINE PERINEUM for prolapsed cord, crowning, mal-presentation.
  6. If bleeding present, assess rate of active bleeding and look for presence of any tissues.
  7. All products of conception should be carefully collected and transported with the mother to the hospital.
  8. Anything other than transport should be coordinated with on-line medical consultation and/or law enforcement.
  9. Administer OXYGEN 4-6 L per NC or 15 L nonrebreather mask maintain oxygen saturation >94%.

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Start IV NS TKO unless hypotensive then 500 ml bolus-repeat as needed,
  4. Establish 2nd IV if able.
  5. If signs of shock present, initiate RAPID TRANSPORT to appropriate facility per Transport Protocol.
  6. Refer to Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxietyfor Pain control.
  7. Monitor patient and vitals closely for signs of shock.
