PEDIATRIC AMPUTATIONS-CRUSH INJURIES

ALL PROVIDERS:

  1. This Protocol addresses the problem of amputation of body parts, and attempts to facilitate the preservation of tissue and the delivery of the patient to a trauma center.
  2. Time to re-implantation for most limbs is critical.

INDICATIONS:

Amputation or large avulsion of any body part.

EXCLUSIONS:

Major trauma to other body systems - use other appropriate Protocol and have other personnel retrieve amputated part.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Full set of vital signs including blood glucose.
  3. Maintain airway, administer 15 lpm of oxygen-maintain SPO2>94%.
  4. Unless this is an isolated injury, consider spinal motion restiction.
  5. Maintain SPO2 92-94%.
  6. Apply direct pressure to control hemorrhage-consider tourniquets if needed.
  7. Amputated Body Parts and/or Tissue.
    • If amputation is incomplete, cover stump with sterile dressing saturated in NS, splint affected digit or limb in baseline physicologic position.
    • All retrievable tissue should be transported (do not delay transport by spending an excessive amount of time looking for an amputated part).
    • Rinse part(s) with NS.
    • Wrap tisue in sterile gauze moistened with NS.
    • Place tissue into plastic bag or container.
    • Place bag/container into separate container filled with ice.
    • Do not allow tissue to come into direct contact with ice, do not freeze, and do not submerge in water.
  8. Tooth Avulsions that are out over 30 minutes, partial or cannot be re-implanted on scene.
    • Handle tooth by chewing surgace only (avoid touching the root).
    • Rinse with water. Do not scrub, dry, or wrap tooth in tissue or cloth.
    • Place tooth in container of ( in order of preference):
      • Patient's Saliva.
      • Milk.
      • Normal Saline.
      • Water.
  9. Monitor closely for signs of shock, especially in amputations above the wrist or ankle.

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Establish vascular access x 2 if possible.
  4. If hypotensive bolus with NS 20 ml/kg then reassess - may give up to 60 ml/kg if not hypotensive TKO rate with a MAX bolus of 1 L.
  5. Treat for pain and anxiety, consider Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety . Contact OLMC for medication order:
    a. Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg (max 25 mcg per dose) IV/IO.
    b. In the event and IV cannot be established, Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg may be given IM up an initial dose of 25 mcg.
    c. For additional doses, contact OLMC.
  6. Re-Implantation of permanent teeth on scene within the first 30 minutes of injury. ( Primary or baby teeth should not be re-implanted).
    • Do not try to re-implant if more than 2 teeth are involved.
    • The tooth must be cleanely avulsed with the entire root present.
    • Only re-implant if it is one of the front 6 upper or lower teeth.
    • Patient must be conscious and cooperative.
    • Gently insert tooth back into the appropriate location without forcing it. Do not worry about positioning it well.
  7. Crushed Syndromes should be considered for the following.
    • Entrapped/compressed patients under a load for more than 30 minutes.
    • Patients with little or no movement for more than 4 hours.
    • Victims will often develop hypo or hyperthermia with prolonged environmental exposure.
    • When possible, initiate IV prior to patient being freed from object or removed from floor after a porlonged immobile period of time.
    • Patients often quickly develop elevated levels of potassium. Watch for the following ECG changes as signs of Hyperkalemia:
      • Peaked T waves.
      • Prolonged PR intervals.
      • ST segment depression.
      • QRS widening, heart blocks, and ventricular arrhythmias.
    • When practical obtain a 12 Lead ECG-before and after extrication.

Medical Control Option-Must contact prior to administration.

  1. Sodium Bicarb :
    a. 1 meq/kg of 8.4% solution children > 1 month.
    b. 0.5 meq/kg of 4.2% solution for infants < 1 month.
    c. Maximum of 50 mEq IV push prior to beginning infusion and up to 2 additional times if patient develops cardiac arrhythmia or a prolonged QRS > 100ms consider Protocol 708: Pediatric Cardiac Arrythmias .
