HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCY

  1. A hypertensive emergency is the presence of organ system dysfunction due primarily to high blood pressure.

  2. Attention in pre-hospital setting should be on:

    • CNS - altered level of consciousness
    • Decrease in vision
    • Focal motor deficits
    • Cardiovascular-angina
    • Acute CHF
    • Aortic dissection.

  3. Because hypertension can be a neuro-protective reflex in the setting of increased intracranial pressure, great caution must be exercised in administering antihypertensive agents.

INDICATIONS

Adult patients with systolic BP of 220 or above, and/or diastolic of 120 or above and evidence of CNS or cardiovascular dysfunction.

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Age<16
  2. Pregnancy - Use Pre-Eclampsia and Eclampsia Protocol.
  3. Trauma, especially head injury.

EMT

  1. History, Exam, and Vitals. Emphasis on neurological and cardiopulmonary systems.
  2. OXYGEN 4-6L NC or 15L NRB mask as indicated.
  3. Blood glucose, Oxygen Saturation and Temperature assessment.

ADVANCED EMT/PARAMEDIC

  1. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  2. 12 Lead ECG if available.
  3. SALINE IV @ TKO Rate or saline lock.
  4. CONTACT OLMC and EXPEDITE TRANSPORT
  5. Keep head elevated to 45 degrees unless trauma is suspected, then immobilize as necessary.
Medical Control Option - Must contact prior to administration

1. Nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL every 3-5 minutes x3 doses MAX.
2. Morphine 2mg IV every 5-10 minutes to 10 mg MAX
