Glucagon

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1012

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 307, 308, 309, 319, 714, 715.

Class: Hormone - Anti-hypoglycemic

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Causes an increase in blood glucose concentrations.
2. Acts on liver glycogen converting it to glucose.

INDICATIONS
1. When unable to obtain IV access (preferred route).
2. Hypoglycemia defined bya blood sugar <60.
3. Altered LOC with hypoglycemia (blood sugar <60).

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Known hypersensitivity.
2. Adrenal gland tumor.

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Hypotension.
2. Nausea/Vomiting

Dosage and Administration

1. Inject solution into a vial containing glucagon powder.
2. Gently swirl until powder dissolves in solution.
3. If solution is not clear - discard.
4. Withdraw into a syringe (1mg = 1mL).

Adult: 1mg IM / SQ in arm or thigh.

Pediatric: 0.1mg/kg IM If patient is <20kg (44lbs) USE LATERAL 1/3 OF THIGH

CONSIDERATIONS

1. Usually takes 5-20 minutes to work.
2. May repeat 1x if no response in 15 minutes.
3. Monitor vital signs every 5 minutes.
4. When conscious give both fast and long acting sugars.
5. If release, ensure a resposible party will remain on scene-follow appropriate protocol.
6. Never leave a patient unattended - may become hypoglycemic again.
7. Must have approval from medical control to scene release.
