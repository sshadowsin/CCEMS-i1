Medical Cardiac Arrest - Initial Care

ALL CARDIAC ARREST VICTIMS SHOULD HAVE:

  1. CPR-Push hard and fast (100 - 120 compressions/minute).
  2. Ensure full chest recoil.
  3. Minimize interruptions in chest compressions.
  4. 1 cycle of CPR: 30 compressions/2 ventilations; 5 cycles=2 minutes.
  5. Avoid hyperventilation, 1 breath every 5-6 seconds (8-10 a minute)
  6. Secure airway and confirm placement, suction as needed
  7. After advanced airway is placed, give continuous chest compressions without pauses.
  8. Check rhythm every 2 minutes
  9. Elevate arm, give fluid bolus when administering a drug during a full arrest
  10. Always search for a reversible cause
  11. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines

Exclusions

  1. Patients fitting strict DOA Protocol 902: DEATH DETERMINATION AND PRONOUNCEMENT PROCESS.
  2. Arrest associated with major trauma - use Protocol 500: Trauma Series.
  3. Arrest associated with hypothermia - use Protocol 105: HYPOTHERMIC CARDIAC ARREST.
  4. Children < 50kg- use Pediatric Protocols.

EMT

BCLS PRIMARY SURVEY:

  1. Airway- Open airway with noninvasive techniques (head tilt-chin lift or jaw thrust without head extension if trauma is suspected) May use OPA or NPA and suction as needed.
  2. Breathing- Look, listen, and feel for adequate breathing. Begin BVM respirations. Do not hyperventilate or over-inflate. Use airway adjunct as indicated (OPA/NPA). Ventilate at a rate of 1 breath every 5-6 seconds.
  3. Circulation- Check for a carotid pulse for at least 5 seconds but not more than 10 seconds. Provide high quality CPR at a rate of 30:2 ( 100 compressions per minute) while attaching AED. Give 5 cycles before attempting to shock. Push hard, push fast and release completely, and minimize interruptions in compressions.
  4. Defibrillation: Provide shocks as indicated. Follow each shock immediately with CPR, beginning with chest compression for 2 minutes or 5 cycles. Check rhythm every 5 cycles; continue until ALS providers take over or patient regains a pulse.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Airway- Maintain airway patency with use of OPA or NPA if not in use suction if needed, insert appropriate size "King" airway and confirm placement.
  2. Breathing- Ensure supplemental oxygen is being used. Assess adequacy of oxygenation and ventilation by: Observing chest rise, auscultation, oxygen saturation, and capnography.
  3. Circulation- Obtain IV/IO access. Give fluids if needed. Monitor for arrhythmias or cardiac arrest rhythms (VF, VT, PEA, and Asystole.) Give appropriate drugs to manage rhythm ( Epinephrine , Lidocaine, Amiodarone, Atropine) following current American Heart ACLS-PALS guidelines. Per ACLS guidelines drug therapy begins after the 2nd shock.
  4. Defibrillation- Provide shocks as indicated, follow each shock with CPR for 5 cycles.

ADVANCED EMT/PARAMEDIC

  1. Supportive care of airway, vascular access and fluid therapy
  2. LVADs are preload dependent and a fluid bolus may improve perfusion
  3. ACLS medications as indicated

PARAMEDIC

  1. Airway- Ensure airway is patent, suction as needed. Intubate when appropriate if an advanced airway has not been placed.
  2. Breathing- Confirm proper placement of advanced airway by:
    1. Physical Examination
    2. Capnography
    3. Use of esophageal detector device
    4. Auscultation
    5. Secure the device to prevent dislodgement
  3. Circulation- Obtain IV/IO access if not already done.
    1. Monitor arrhythmias or cardiac arrest rhythms.
    2. Give appropriate drugs ( Epinephrine, Lidocaine or Amiodarone, Atropine, Magnesium Sulfate, and Sodium Bicarb) to manage rhythms and blood pressure.
    3. Per ACLS guidelines drug therapy begins after the 2nd shock.
  4. Defibrillation- Provide shocks as indicated, follow each shock with CPR for 5 cycles.
  5. Differential Diagnosis- Search for/find and treat reversible causes of the arrest. See Hs & Ts Table Below
  6. Defibrillation- Provide shocks as indicated, follow each shock with CPR for 5 cycles.
