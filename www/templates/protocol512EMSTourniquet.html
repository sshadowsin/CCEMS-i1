EMS TOURNIQUET

INDICATION:

  1. Hemorrhage from an extremity that cannot be controlled with direct pressure or a pressure bandage.
    a. There may be times (tactical, rescue, entrapped patient, multipule patient scene, otherwise austere environment) when tourniquet application is the best first option.
  2. There are NO contraindications.
  3. Tourniquets can be used:
    a. In the face of profuse/life threatening extremity hemorrhage that cannot be controlled with either direct pressure or a pressure dressing.
    b. In any circumstance where direct pressure or a pressure dressing cannot be applied quickly or effectively to stop profuse/life threatening extremity hemorrhage.
  4. Not all bleeding wounds may require tourniquet application such as:
    a. Venous bleeding.
    b. Hemorrhage control easily achieved with pressure dressing.

TOURNIQUET APPLICATION AND TECHNIQUES

  1. The tourniquet should be placed 2-3 inches proximal to the bleeding wound.
  2. The tourniquet should never be placed on a joint (knee or elbow) or over an impaled object.
  3. The tourniquet should be applied tightly enough to completely stop bleeding from the wound (this will be painful for the patient).
  4. The affected extremity should be exposed - a view of the tourniquet should bot be obscured.
  5. The time of the tourniquet application should be specifically noted.
  6. Inform each successive provider that a tourniquet is in place.
  7. Time of tourniquet application can also be written directly on the patient.
  8. Pain medication may be necessary and should be provided. Refer to pain control Protocol 509: Pain Control, Nausea, Vomiting, Anxiety
  9. A tourniquet placed in the prehospital environment should not be removed if transport time less than 30 minutes.
  10. If tourniquet time is to exceed 30 miuntes then contact medical control to discuss possible tourniquet removal.
  11. A Tourniquet that has been on for more than six hours should not be removed by anyone except the physician providing definitive care.

TOURNIQUET MONITORING

  1. Check wound site frequently to assess for bleeding.
  2. As the patient is resuscitated and BP increases, bleeding may begin.
  3. Tourniquet may need to be tightened.

TOURNIQUET REASSESSMENT AND REMOVAL

  1. For short transports (<30 minutes), tourniquet should generally be left in place until arrival at the E.D.
  2. For longer transports, consult medical control about tourniquet removal and replacement with pressure dressing.
  3. For amputations or near-amputations, tourniquet should not be removed by EMS.
  4. Place pressure dressing over wound but not to obscure tourniquet.
  5. Gradually loosen tourniquet and observe for bleeding.
  6. If bleeding occurs: tighten tourniquet.
  7. If no bleeding occurs: leave tourniquet loosely in place in case bleeding recurs.

EQUIPMENT

  1. Only a commercial tourniquet should be used, if possible.
  2. Avoid improvised tourniquets (belts, cravats, twine, BP Cuffs, etc.)

TOURNIQUET USE IN TRIAGE / MASS CASUALTY

In multiple patient situations, tourniquets should be applied during initial triage.
