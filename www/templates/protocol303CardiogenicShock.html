Cardiogenic Shock

INDICATION

Patients who meet shock criteria, and have historical evidence of cardiac dysfunction or failure: chest pain, dyspnea, palpitations or physical evidence such as: rales, hypotension or a serious arrhythmia

Consider Shock in patients with one or more of the following:

  1. Vital signs: HR>100, SBP of<90 mmHg for adults, SBP <70+ (age in years x2) mmHg for peds, or RR >20 BPM.
  2. Skin signs: Cold clammy skin, febrile or flash or delayed capillary refill.
  3. Mental status: Altered, lethargic, or irritable (esp. in infants).

EMT

Focused history and physical exam

  1. Blood glucose, oxygen saturation and temperature assessment.
  2. Administer 15 lpm of oxygen by Nonrebreather mask.
  3. C-spine immobilization, if indicated.
  4. Ensure patient warmth.
  5. ASSIST VENTILATION with 100% OXYGEN if necessary.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  2. APPLY ECG MONITOR/12 Lead ECG if available - if arrhythmias present go immediately to appropriate Protocol.
  3. INITIATE TRANSPORT as soon as possible.
  4. Reassess vitals, mental status, airway & respiratory status, and ECG rhythm frequently.
  5. Vascular access - 2 large bore IV's if able, if lungs "wet" or patient has marked dyspnea, start a Hep Cap or NS @ TKO Rate.

CARDIOGENIC SHOCK

  1. In patients with CHF, pulmonary edema, cardiogenic shock or kidney failure (i.e. dialysis patients), provide 500mL fluid boluses up to a maximum of 1 liter and reassess for reversal of the signs of shock. Monitor closely for signs of fluid overload (increased dyspnea, cough, frothy sputum, JVD, or inspiratiory crackles) Immediately run IV @ TKO rate.
  2. Consult OLMC

PARAMEDIC

  1. Obtain 12 lead if not already obtained and transmit to receiving facility.
  2. Advanced Airway if indicated: Nasotracheal route may be useful for awake patients.
  3. If lungs are clear and no significant respiratory distress, continue NS wide open until perfusion improves, respiratory distress develops or rales are heard on lung exam.
Medical Control Option - Must contact prior to administration

1. If lungs sound "wet", respiratory distress present or no response to fluid challenge and VS are stable: Lasix 40 mg IV/IO may repeat x1.
2. If hypotensive and refractory to fluid bolus: Epinephrine 2-10 mcg/min and titrate systolic BP until it reaches 90-100 mmHg. Mix Epinephrine 2 mg 1:1000 in 1000 ml NS which yields 0.002 mg/ml (2mcg/ml) Use Dial-a-flow extension set following the chart below:
