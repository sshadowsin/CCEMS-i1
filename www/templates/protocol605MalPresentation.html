MAL-PRESENTATION

  1. BREECH
  2. LIMB PRESENTATION
  3. SHOULDER DYSTOCIA

INDICATIONS:

  1. Patient in labor with visible mal-presentation.
  2. Patient in labor with mal-presentation suspected through pre-natal care.

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Complete set of vital signs.
  3. Provide oxygen to maintain oxygen saturation >94%.
  4. Obtain OBSTETRICAL history per Protocol 600: Obstetric Emergencies.
  5. If delivery is not in progress, place patient in Trendelenburg position with elevation of the right side.
  6. If delivery has begun or is imminent, pisition patient as for normal delivery Protocol 602: Full Term Imminent Delivery/Newborn Resuscitation.

MAL-PRESENTATIONS:

  1. Breech presentation (coming buttocks first):
    a. Position mother with her buttocks at edge of bed, legs flexed.
    b. Support baby's body as it delivers-be patient-do not pull.
    c. As the head passes the pubis, apply gentle upward pressure until the mouth appears over the perineum.
    d. Immediately suction mouth, then nose.
    e. If head does not deliver, but newborn is attemptimg to breathe, place gloved hand into the vagina, palm toward newborn's face, forming a "V" with the index and middle finger on either side of the nose.
    f. Push the vaginal wall from the face.
    g. Maintain position throughout transport.
    h. Maintain an"airway" and provide supplemental oxygen.

FACE/BROW/SHOULDER/ARM PRESENTATION:

  1. Face first may proceed as normal delivery.
  2. Any other presentation (and face first if delivery does not progress as usual) requires RAPID TRANSPORT.
  3. DO NOT ATTEMPT DELIVERY.
  4. If possible, place mother in Trendelenburg position-otherwise, use knee-chest position.

SHOULDER DYSTOCIA:

  1. Head is out but shoulder will not pass:
    a. Position mother with buttocks off the edge of the bed and thighs flexed upward as much as posiible.
    b. Apply firm, open hand pressure above the symphysis pubis.
    c. If delivery does not occur, maintain airway patency as best as possible, immediately transport.

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Establish IV @ TKO unless hypotensive then 500 ml bolus and repeat as needed.
  4. Begin rapid transport to apropriate facility.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. When delivery is imminent, to suppress uterine contractions:
    a. Rapidly infuse 1 liter of NS ( AEMT/PM)
    b. Albuterol 2.5 mg via nebulization ( AEMT/PM)
    c. Magnesium Sulfate 1 gram IV and titrate per Medical Control ( PM ONLY)
    d. PEDIATRIC (<15 years of age, PM only).
    e. Magnesium Sulfate - For pediatric patients who are pregnant and having a seizure contact OLMC for dosage instructions.
