IMMUNOCOMPROMISED PATIENTS

Key Considerations

  1. Family members are often the best resource for patient care information.
  2. Due to patient's inability to fight infection, patient may become very ill in a short period of time. These patients may present in overwhelming shock or sepsis, or respiratory distress.
  3. Protect patients from infectious exposure during transport.
  4. All EMS providers should use universal precautions (strict hand washing, gloves) and masks should be worn by providers with any possible infectious condition (URI, etc.).
  5. These patients are at risk for low platelets and anemia, bleeding is a risk.
  6. No rectal medications for treatment.
  7. Avoid Ibuprofen with these patients.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam
  2. Blood glucose, temperature and oxygen saturation assessment.
  3. Assess and document reasons the patient may be immunocompromised, such as congenital syndromes, chemotherapy, transplant surgery, autoimmune disorder, or steroid usage.
  4. Assess and treat compromised airway, respiratory distress, altered mental status.
  5. Assess for overwhelming sepsis with shock.
  6. Provide 100% oxygen to the patient maintain an oxygen saturation 90-94%.
  7. Keep the patient NPO.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. If severely febrile >then 101 and with rigors, may give Tylenol (Acetaminophen) orally and document temperature. If temperature below this level, do not treat fever.
  4. Establish vascular access-set rate @ TKO unless hypotensiev then give BS 250-500 ml boluses for Adult patients to max 2 L and 20 ml/kg boluses for pediatric patients to max of 40 ml/kg.
  5. Treat nausea/vomiting per Protocol 509: Pain Control, Nausea, Vomiting, Anxiety
  6. Transport-contact OLMC
