PEDIATRIC SYNCOPE

NOTES:

The common denominator of syncope is INADEQUATE BRAIN PERFUSION. This may be due to several reasons.
a. Vasovagal Attack
b. Hypoxia
c. Cardiac Arrhythmias.
d. Hypoglycemia.
e. Hypovolemia.
f. Hyperventilation.
g. Cerebral Ischemia or Infarction - Children can have strokes as well as adults. Some risk factors include; sickle cell disease, congenital and acquired heart disease, head and neck infections, systemic conditions, (e.g. inflammatory bowel disease and autoimmune disorders), head trauma or dehydration.
h. Ensure that history and physcial findings are conveyed to the hospital team.
i. Any patient with a chief complaint of syncope shall be discussed with OLMC prior to scene relase and an AMA form shall be completed either paper or electronic and signed by the responsible guardian.

INDICATIONS:

Patients with any transient loss of, or alteration in normal level of consciousness or mentation.

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Persistent alteration of consciousness - use Protocol 713: Pediatric Altered Level of Consciousness .
  2. Signs of shock - use Protocol 712: Pediatric Shock (General Guidelines .

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess medical conditions, burns or traumatic injuries that have the potential to comrpomise the airway.
  3. Assess ABC's for evidence of apneea, airway reflex compromise or difficulty in ventilatory effort.
  4. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all comrpomised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  5. Identify and treat underlying reversible medical conditions (narcotic overdose, hypoglycemia, etc.) Obtain Blood glucose.
  6. In general, maintain an oxygen saturation 90-94%.
  7. Always insure proper care of the C-Spine during Airway treatemnt.
  8. Keep the patient NPO.
  9. Provide 100% oxygen to the patient.
  10. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress. Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  11. Recommended pediatric ventilatory rates:
    a. Infant (0-12 month): 25 breaths per minute.
    b. 1-3 years: 20 breaths per minute.
    c. 4-6 years: 15 breaths per minute.
    d. >6 years: 12 (same as adult).
  12. Do not hyperventilate the patient.
  13. Evaluate and document Cincinnati Stroke Scale during assessment. The scale is positive (a stroke is likely) if ANY of the following are abnormal:
    • Facial Droop.
      • Normal: Both sides of face move equally.
      • Abnormal: One side of face does not move as well as the other or not at all.
    • Arm Drift:
      • Normal: Both arms move equally or not at all.
      • Abnormal: One arm does not move, or drifts down compared to the other.
    • Speech:
      • Normal: Patient uses correct words with no slurring.
      • Abnormal: Slurred, inapproprate words or mute.

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. 12 Lead ECG and transmit to receiving facility.
  4. Rapidly transport to approptiate facility per transport protocol.
  5. Establish vascular access- if hypotensive give 20 ml/kg NS then TKO rate, repeat as needed until stable or 60 ml/kg.
  6. FOR HYPOGLYCEMIA:
    a. If blood glucose is less than 60 mg/dl
    i. Children greater than 1 year: Dextrose 25% (D25W) 2 mL/kg IV.
    ii. Infants up to 1 year: Dextrose 10% (D10/NS) 5 mL/kg IV.
    iii. Neonates <30 days: Dextrose 10% (D10NS) 2 ml/kg (200 mg/kg) IV.

NOTE: If commercially prepared mediction is not available:

a. To make D10 - Dilute 10 mL D50 in 40 mL of NS.
b. To make D25 - Dilute D50 1:1 with NS.

IF UNABLE TO ESTABLISH IV ACCESS

Glucagon 0.1 mg/kg (max dose of 1 mg) IM if no IV/IO access.

For HYPERGLYCEMIA:

Normal Saline 20 mL/kg IV/IO over 30-60 minutes for hyperglycemic patient (BS>300 mg/dL)
