PEDIATRIC SHOCK (GENERAL GUIDELINES)

TYPES OF SHOCK:

  1. Hypovolemic
  2. Septic
  3. Cardiogenic
  4. Vasomotor (anaphylaxis, neurogenic)

HYPOVOLEMIC

  1. Signs (any of the following)
    • Bleeding
    • Vomiting/Diarrhea
    • Dry Mucosa/Poor Skin Turgor
    • High Fever
    • Poor Intake
    • Diabetic Ketoacidosis
  2. Associated with clear Lung Sounds
    • If Wheezing/rales, consider: Protocol 704: Pediatric Anaphylaxis

SEPTIC

  1. Signs
    • Tachypnea
    • Bounding Pulses
    • Warm, Flushed Skin
    • Widening Pulse Pressure
    • Fever or Hypothermia
    • Petechial Rash

DISTRIBUTIVE

  1. Signs
    • Anaphylaxis - Protocol 704: Pediatric Anaphylaxis
    • Pre-existing Spinal Cord Injury

Cardiogenic

  1. If Cardiac Arrythmia - Protocol 708: Pediatric Cardiac Arrythmias

INDICATIONS:

Shock should be suspected if any of the follwing signs of poor perfusion are present:

  1. Altered level of consciousness.
  2. Hypotonia ("limp baby").
  3. Weak ventral (proximal) pulses with absent periheral pulses.
  4. Capillary refill > 2 seconds.
  5. Active bleeding or a history of active bleeding from bowel, stomach or major vessel.
  6. Tachycardia.
  7. Hypotension.

EXCLUSIONS:

Trauma - use Protocol 717: Pediatric Major or Multi-System Trauma.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Blood glucose, Oxygen Saturation and Temperature assessment.
  3. Provide 100% oxygen to the patient - Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress. Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  4. Recommended pediatric ventilatory rates:
    a. Infant (0-12 months): 25 breaths per minute.
    b. 1-3 years: 20 breaths per minute.
    c. 4-6 years: 15 nreaths per minute.
    d. >6 years: 12 (same as adult).
  5. Do not hyperventilate the patient.
  6. Ensure patient warmth.
  7. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  8. Identify and treat underlying reversible medical conditions (narcotic overdose, hypoglycemia, etc.).
  9. In general, maintain an oxygen saturation 90-94%.
  10. Always insure proper care of the C-Spine during Airway treatment.
  11. Keep the patient NPO. Stop any tube feedings and do not use feeding tube during resuscitation.
  12. Infants and young children are primary nose breathers - Sunction oral and nasal passages as needed to keep clear.
  13. Bleeding from the nose (epistaxis) should be controlled by first having the paitent sit and lean forward (unless there is a need for spinal motion resriction). Apply direct pressure by pinching the fleshy portion of the nostrils.
  14. Cover lacerations or puncture wounds on the neck near the great vessels or trachea with an occlusive dressing.
  15. Cover abdominal eviscerations with a moist sterile dressing.
  16. Don not attempt to replace organs.
  17. Cover extruded eye or deflated globe with a moist sterile dressing and protective covering.
  18. Do not apply pressure or attempt to replace in socket.
  19. Cover both eyes.
  20. In large, partially attached avulsions, the tissue should be returned to its baseline position and stabilized whenever possible.
  21. Elevate the limb such that the wound is above the heart.
  22. Impaled objects should be stabilized in place and covered with dry sterile dressings. The exceptions would be:
    • Objects through the cheek where there is the possibility of airway compromise.
    • Objects that would interfere with chest compressions.
    • Sharp objects may need to be removed if their resences is causing ongoing injury, compromise, or inhibiting CPR.
  23. Tourniquets should be used to control hemorrhage not controlled with direct pressure.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Establish a 12 Lead ECG and transmit to receiving hospital.
  4. Establish vascular access - Use 2 lare bore IV's of possible. If unable to establish IV access and patient is unresponsive with unstable VS's establish IO access.
  5. TRAUMATIC SHOCK - Give fluid bolus of NS 20 mL/kg at a time, ressess and repeat t up to a maximum of 60 mL/kg total. Reasses for reversal of the signs of shock:
    a. If the patient remains hypotensive after the 60 mL/kg call OLMC.
  6. NON-TRAUMATIC SHOCK _ Proved 20 ml/kg bolus up to a maximum of 60 mL/kg and reassess for reversal of the signs of shock.
  7. If the patient remains hypotensive after administering 60 mL/kg NS, call OLMC.
  8. CARDIOGENIC SHOCK - In patients with CHF, pulmonary edema, cardiogenic shock or kidney failure (i.e. dialysis patients), provide 10 mL/kg fluid boluses up to a maximum of 20 mL/kg and reassess for reversal of the signs of shock.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated or appropriate supraglottic airway.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. Consider sedation after intubation Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety.
  5. After 2 unsuccessful attempts at endotracheal intubation use a supraglottic airway device or BVM with appropriate oral/nasal airway.
  6. No nasotracheal intubation.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Suspected Tension Pneumothorax-Immediate needle decompression using appropriate technique.
  2. Traumatic Arrest-Consider bilateral needle decompression for suspected tension pneumothorax based on mechanism of injury using appropriate technique.
  3. Surgical Airway Cricothyrotomy- only when all other methods of oxygenation, ventilation and securing the airway have failed.
