Fentanyl

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1011

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 509, 717, 720, 721, 722, 723, 724, 725, 726.

Class: Narcotic Analgesic

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Synthetic Opiate
2. IV - Peak analgesia may be as short as 90 seconds with duration of clinical effects approximately 30 minutes.
3. IM - Onset of Analgesia is within 7-15 minutes lasting for 1-2 hours.

INDICATIONS
1. For analgesia of short duration.

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Patients with known intolerance to Fentanyl.

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Respiratory depression.
2. Laryngospasm
3. Hypertension or Hypotension, Bradycardia.
4. Dizziness.
5. Rigidity of the chest/Abdominal muscle (related to dose and speed of injection)
6. Nausea/Vomiting
7. Diaphoresis

PRECUATIONS
1. Respiratory compromise and apnea have been noted - monitor oxygen saturations at all times.
2. Peak respiratory depressant effect is noted 5-15 minutes following IV dosage.
3. Use with caution in patients with COPD, Liver or Kidney dysfunction.
4. Will have additive or potentiating effects with CNS depressant drugs.

Dosage and Administration

Adult: Fentanyl 50 mcg IV/IO q 10 minutes IV/IO/IM. Max Dose 200mcg.

1. In the event an IV can not be established, Fentanyl 50 mcg may be given IM.
2. For additional doses, contact OLMC.

Pediatric: Fentanyl 1mcg/kg (Max 25mcg per dose) IV/IO

1.In the event an IV cannot be established Fentanyl 1mcg/kg may be given IM up to an initial dose of 25 mcg.
2.For additional doses, contact OLMC.

CONSIDERATIONS

1. Reversible with Naloxone.
2. Produces minimal hemodynamic effects.
