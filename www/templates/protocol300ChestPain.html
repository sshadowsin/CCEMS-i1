Chest Pain

Acute Coronary Syndrome

NOTE:

The goals of chest pain protocol include:

  1. The prevention of and resuscitation from sudden cardiac death. .
  2. Prompt Transport for reperfusion therapy (Thrombolytics) or PCI.
  3. The reduction of myocardial ischemia.
  4. The relief of pain and anxiety.
  5. Prevent sudden cardiac death due to VF or hypotension from bradycardic rhythms that can occur with acute ischemia.
  6. Half of the patients who die of ACS do so before reaching the hospital. VF or Pulseless VT is the precipitant rhythm in most of these deaths.
  7. VF is most likely to develop during the first 4 hours after onset of symptoms.

Sings and Symptoms:

The most common sign of myocardial ischemia and infarction is RETROSTERNAL CHEST DISCOMFORT. The patient may perceive this discomfort more as pressure or tightness than actual pain.

Symptoms suggestive of ACS may also include:

  1. Uncomfortable pressure, fullness, squeezing, or pain in the center of the chest lasting several minutes (usually more than a few minutes).
  2. Chest discomfort spreading to the shoulders, neck, one or both arms, or jaw.
  3. Chest discomfort spreading into the back or between the shoulder blades.
  4. Chest discomfort with lightheadedness, fainting, sweating or nausea.
  5. Unexplained, sudden shortness of breath, which may occur with or without chest discomfort.

Other potentially lethal conditions that may mimic ACS by causing acute chest pain or discomfort may include:

  1. Aortic dissection.
  2. Acute pulmonary embolism.
  3. Acute pericardial effusion with tamponade.
  4. Tension pneumothorax.

EMT

  1. Conduct a prompt and targeted evaluation.
  2. Obtain brief history including "OPQRST" and "SAMPLE."
  3. Obtain past medical history and secure all medications to be taken with patient.
  4. Determine whether the patient (male or female) has taken erectile dysfunction medications such as Viagra, Levitra, or Cialis within the last 24 hours.
  5. Brief physical exam and obtain all Vital Signs with oximetry (baseline saturation before oxygen, if possible).
  6. Provide oxygen (use non-rebreather mask or nasal cannula as needed to maintain 02 saturations at 94%).

  7. Apply ECG patches if able.

    Contact OLMC if patient assisted medications are to be used

  8. 324mg Aspirin , preferably chewed, if patient is >18 years old and no reported allergies to aspirin. Adminster even if patient takes a daily aspirin dose.

  9. Assist patient with prescribed nitroglycerin: 1 pill sublingual every 5 minutes, up to 3 doses, as long as symptoms persist and SBP >100mmHg.
  10. Do not administer Nitroglycerin if patient (male or female) has taken erectile dysfunction medications within 24 hours.
  11. Keep seated or semi-reclined. If alert and stable vital signs, allow position of comfort or supine as tolerated.
  12. Reassure and keep calm.
  13. Treat for shock if necessary.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is done.
  2. Auscultate breath sounds.
  3. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  4. Establish IV with NS (250mL bolus if systolic BP <100) set rate @ TKO.
  5. Obtain 12 lead ECG and transmit to STEMI/PCI receiving facility.
  6. All ECG's suggestive of acute MI should be immediately transmitted to a STEMI/PCI receiving center.

Contact Medical Control for all medications orders:

  1. Baby Aspirin x4 Chewed if not already given.
  2. If 12 lead ECG showed Acute Inferior Wall MI (elevation in II, III and aVF) hold Nitroglycerin until contact Medical Control for directions ( Nitroglycerin can lower preload, a fluid bolus may be required prior to giving Nitroglycerin).
  3. Nitroglycerin 0.4mg Tablet/Spray q 5 minutes to maximum of 3 doses WITH VITAL SIGNS TAKEN BEFORE AND AFTER EACH DOSE (Withhold if systolic BP<110) if not already given and 12 lead does not show an Inferior wall MI.
  4. Normal Saline 250-500mL IV bolus if patient is hypotensive, use caution with a history or evidence of congestive heart failure.
  5. Morphine 2mg IV if chest pain is moderate to severe and unrelieved by 3 doses of NTG if vital signs tolerate and Systolic BP >110.
  6. Lidocaine 1mg/kg IV for multifocal PVC's, PVC's >6 minutes, couplets, or runs of VT.
  7. Initial bolus should be followed with Lidocaine 0.5mg/kg doses q 10 minutes to a total dose of 3mg/kg. A Lidocaine drip may be ordered by OLMC.
  8. Begin transport as soon as possible to allow early reperfusion therapy.
  9. Upgrade call to DELTA/ECHO if not done.
  10. If cardiac arrest develops, use appropriate Protocol.
  11. If nausea-vomiting, see Protocol 509: Pain Control, Nausea, Vomiting, Anxiety.

PARAMEDIC

Ensure all the above has been done

  1. Obtain 12 lead ECG if not already done and transmit.
  2. Avoid use of Nitroglycerin with inferior wall MI, consult OLMC if use is appropriate for a specific patient, consider 250-500mL fluid bolus if appropriate while contacting OLMC.
  3. Continue use of Morphine, and Lidocaine as indicative.

  4. Start Lidocaine gtt @2mg/min if boluses were given using a flow restrictive device.

    • 1mg/min - 15 gtts per mL.
    • 2mg/min - 30 gtts per mL
    • 3mg/min - 45 gtts per mL
    • 4mg/min - 60 gtts per mL

  5. Ensure rapid transport to hospital to facilitate thrombolytic therapy.

  6. Anticipate cardiac arrest and be prepared to give ACLS/advanced airway as needed.
  7. Anticipate arrhythmias and be prepared for cardioversion or pacing as needed.
  8. If cardiac arrest develops, use appropriate protocol.

Medical Control Option

Fentanyl may be substituted in the case of an Inferior Wall MI, as Morphine can cause a drop in preload and cause or worsen existing hypotension.
