GLUCOSE EMERGENCIES: HYPOGLYCEMIA/ HYPERGLYCEMIA

ALL PROVIDERS

Hypoglycemia is defined as blood glucose level <60 mg/dl for adults and children, and (<45 mg/dl for the term neonate (<30 days of age) with any degree of altered mentation.

  1. DO NOT attempt to give oral glucose to those who cannot swallow and protect their airway.
  2. Strongly consider transport of any patient who is at risk for prolonged or recurrent hypoglycemia, such as long-acting insulin or oral hypoglycemic overdoes.
  3. For severe hypoglycemia (<40 mg/dl) or hypoglycemic seizure, recheck blood glucose every 15 minutes to check for recurrent low blood sugar that may need treatment.
  4. Hypoglycemic patient with altered mentation and insulin pump in place
    • a. Care is directed to treating hypoglycemia first, then stopping administration of insulin.
    • b. Turn off insulin pump if able.
    • c. If no one familiar with the device is available to assist, disconnect pump from patient by either:
      • i. Using quick-release where the tubing enters the dressing on patient's skin.
        OR
      • ii. Completely remove the dressing, thereby removing the subcutaneous needle and catheter from under patient's skin.
  5. Criteria for scene release of hypoglycemic patient:
    • a. Return to normal mental status following treatment.
    • b. Paitent is able to take oral glucose, food and liquids.
    • c. Patient does not want to be transported.
    • d. No oral diabetic medications have been taken.
    • e. No sucidiidal ideations or attempt to self-harm involved.
    • f. There is at least one responsible person that can assist the patient and is comfortable with monitoring the patient.
    • g. OLMC has been contacted and agrees to the release.

Hyperglycemia is defined as a blood glucose >300 mg/dL.

A number of medical conditions can cause hyperglycemia, but the most common by far is diabetes mellitus. Other causes include: Pancreatitis, cancer, heart attack and some medications such as beta blockers. The main symptoms of hyperglycemia are increased thirst and a frequent need to urinate. People with type 1 diabetes may develop diabetic ketacidosis (DKA), and those with type 2 diabetes can develop hyperglycemic hyperosmolar nonketotic syndrome (HHNS, also referred to as hyperglcemia hypersomolar state). These so-called hyperglycemic crises are serious conditions that can be life-threatening if not treated immediately. hyperglycemic crises cause about 2,400 deaths each year in the U.S.

Hyperglycemia Treatment

HYPERGLYCEMIA: Noted as Blood Glucose Level >300 mg/dl.
HYPERGLYCEMIA Adult: Give Normal Saline 1000 mL IV/IO over 30-60 minutes (BS >300 mg/dl)
HYPERGLYCEMIA Pediatric: Give Normal Saline 20 mL/kg IV/IO over 30-60 minutes.

Hypoglycemia Treatment

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam
  2. Blood glucose assessment (heel stick is preferred in newborns or infants).
  3. Dextrose Oral glucose 15 grams if patient is able to swallow and protect airway
  4. Repeat in 15 minutes as needed

Advanced EMT/Paramedic

  1. Ensure above is complete
  2. Contact OLMC
  3. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available
  4. Establish vascular access set @ TKO unless hypotensive then give:
    • Adult: 500 ml bolus repeat as needed to max 2 L.
    • Pediatric patients: 20 ml /kg bolus may repeat to max 40 ml / kg
  5. IO access should be reserved for a patient in extremis and not for routine access.
  6. Use commercial Pediatric glucose if available, otherwise dilute to appropriate concentrations.

Hypoglycemia Adult

  1. Dextrose D50 25 grams slow IV/IO for hypoglycemia. May repeat x1 in 5 minutes if no improvement.
  2. Glucagon 1mg IM if no IV/IO access after 2 attempts.

Hypoglycemia Pediatric if Blood glucose is less than 60mg/dl administer Dextrose

  1. Children greater than 1 year: Dextrose 25% (D25W) 2mL/kg IV
  2. Infants up to 1 year: Dextrose 10% (D10/NS) 5mL/kg IV
  3. Neonates <30 days: Dextrose 10% (D10/NS) 2mL/kg IV.

NOTE: If commercially prepared Dextrose 10% is not available:

  • To make D10: Dilute 10mL of D50 in 40mL of NS
  • To make D25: Dilute D50 1:1 with NS

IF UNABLE TO ESTABLISH AN IV IN A HYPOGLYCEMIC PEDIATRIC PATIENT, ADMINSTER: Glucagon 0.1mg/kg IM (max dose of Glucagon for pediatric patient is 1mg)
