PEDIATRIC VENTRICULARE FIBRILLATION

NOTE:

  1. The most common pediatric arrhythmias are Asystole and Bradycardia arrhythmias; however, pediatric rhythm disturbances can be divided into 3 types:
    a. Rhythms producing a fast heart rate.
    b. Rhythms producing a slow heart rate.
    c. Absence of any organized electrical activity (asystole or venrticular fibrillation).
  2. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines as needed.

The first priority is correction of hypoxia. The first 2 types (tachycardia-bradycardia) require urgent reatement only if the patient is symptomatic (hyptension or poorly perfused). For atrial tachycardias-cardioversion should not be performed unless the patient is in extremis and a long transport time is anticipated. Adenosine ( 0.01 mg/kg then 0.2 mg/kg) should be considered for selected patients. OLMC must be contacted before administration of Adenosine.

FOR ALL ARRHYTHMIAS THE FOLLOWING APPLIES:

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess for signs of poor perfusion, hypotension altered mental staus, signs of shock, cheast pain.
  3. Obtain a blood glucose level.
  4. Maintain airway; assist with breathing as necessary, provide oxygen.
  5. Begin chest compressions if persistent heart rate <60/min and signs of poor perfusion following affressive oxygenation and ventilation.
  6. Ensure patient is warm.
  7. Suction as needed.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECCG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Perorm a 12 ECG and transmit to receiving hospital if possible.
  4. Supportive care of airway, vascular access and fluid therapy.
  5. Monitor ECG - determine rhythm. For specific arrhythmia management, see appropriate management protocols.
  6. RYTHMS should be documented with strip.
  7. CONTACT MEDICAL CONTROL.
  8. Monitor SCENE TIME.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is compete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endortacheal tube when indicated or appropriate supraglottic airway.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. No nasotracheal intubateion.
  5. Use appropriate current PALS algorithms.
