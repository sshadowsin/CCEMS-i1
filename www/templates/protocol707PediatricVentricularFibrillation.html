PEDIATRIC VENTRICULARE FIBRILLATION

NOTE:

  1. Begin all cardiac arrest series Protocols with Protocol 705: Pediatric Cardioplumonary Resuscitation .
  2. Follow curret ACLS and PALS guidelines as needed.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess for presence of absence of a pulse.
  3. Assess blood glucose level.
  4. Begin BCLS per current American Heart Guidelines.
  5. AED is only to be used > 1 year of age. Use a pediatric system, if available, for ages 1 to 8 years old.
  6. If arrest is witnessed:
    a. Defibrillate immediately if AED or manual defibrillator is available. 2 J/kg for the first attempt.
  7. If unwitnessed or defibrillator is not immediately available:
    a. Begin high quality CPR.
    b. When AED arrives, attach to paitent and defibrillate if a shock is advised.
  8. Resume CPR immediately after each shock and continue for 2 minutes (5 cyces) or until asked to hold by the AED.
  9. Check pulse and assess rhythm, and shock if advised after each 2 minute cycle of compressions.
  10. Place an NP or OP airway and apply asynchronous BVM breaths at a rate of 1 breath every 4-6 seconds.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Establish IV or IO access.
  3. Defibrillation: 2 J/kg for the first shock with either a monophasic or biphasic defibrillator - Second shock at 4J/kg and subsequent shocks at 4J/kg to a maximum of 10J/kg or the adult dose.
  4. Resume CPR (2 minute cycle) immediately following shock delivery, then perform rhythm analysis.
  5. Persistent or recurrent VF/VT present - May give 1 round of rhythm specific drugs per current AHA PALS guidelines. Per PALS guidelies drug therapy begins after the 2nd shock.
  6. Must contact OLMC for additional medications.
  7. Administer Epinephrine :
    a. 0.01 mg/kg = 0.1 ml/kg (1:10,000) IV or IO push.
    b. Repeat every 3-5 minutes.
    c. May repeat initial dose.
  8. Consider one Antiarrhythmic according to tthe current AHA PALS guidelines and may use any ONE antiarrhythmic available, either Amiodarone or Lidocaine :
    a. Amiodarone :
    • i. 5 mg/kg IV/IO
    • ii. May repeat in 3-5 minutes x 1 for refractory VF/pulseless VT.
    • iii. DO not exceed 300 mg for VFib/Pulseless Vtach.
      b. Lidocaine :
    • i. 1 mg/kg IV/IO
    • ii. May repeat every 3-5 minutes up to 3 mg/kg.
  9. Consider Dextrose (IV or IO): If blood sugar <60. a. Children greater than 1 years: Dectose 25% (D25W) 2 mL/kg IV
    b. Infants up to 1 year: Dextrose 10% (D10/NS) 5 mL/kg IV.
    c. Neonates <30 dats: Dextrose 10% (D10/NS) 2 ml/kg (200mg/kg) IV.

NOTE: If commerically prepared medication is not available:
1. To make D10 - Dilute 10 mL D 50 in 40 mL of NS.
2. To make D25 - Dilute D50 1:1 with NS.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is compete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endortacheal tube when indicated or appropriate supraglottic airway.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. Continute with current PALS Guidelines.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Sodium Bicarb:
    a. Not a first line drug-contact OLMC for permission.
    b. Should be used for severe metabolic acidosis following prolonged arrest unresponsive to ventilation and oxygenation or Sodium channel blocker toxicity such as tricylic antidepressant.
    c. Sodium Bicarb 1 meg/kg of 8.4% solution children >1 month.
    d. Sodium Bicarb 0.5 meq/kg of 4.2% solution for infants < 1 month.
