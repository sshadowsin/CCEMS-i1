Dextrose (D50)

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1008

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 307, 308, 309, 319, 707, 714, 715, 901

Class: Carbohydrate

MECHANISM OF ACTION
1. Rapidly restores blood sugar levels in states of hypoglycemia.
2. Prevents use of fats/proteins as a fuel source
3. Diminishes drain on glycogen stores.

INDICATIONS
1. Insulin shock or decreased LOC due to hypoglycemia (Blood Sugar <60)
2. Refractory Cardiac Arrest if hypoglycemic.
3. Status Epilepticus

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. CVA or any neurological insult
2. Delirium Tremors

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Tissue Necrosis if infiltrates

Dosage and Administration

1. Adults: 50% 25 grams IV/IO titrate to effect for hypoglycemia. May repeat 1x in 5 minutes if no improvement

2. Hypoglycemia Pediatrics:

a. Children greater than 1 year: Dextrose 25% (D25W) 2mL/kg IV
b. Infants up to 1 year: Dextrose 10% (D10/NS) 5mL/kg IV
c. Neonates <30 days: Dextrose 10% (D10/NS) 2mL/kg (200mg/kg) IV

NOTE: If commercially prepared medication is not available :

1.To make D10 - Dilute 10mL of D50 in 40 mL of NS
2. To make D25 - Dilute 50 1:1 with NS.

CONSIDERATIONS
1. Repeat glucoscan 5 minutes after administration.
2. When conscious, give both fast acting sugar (juice, soda), and long acting sugar (cheese, sandwich).
3. Never leave unattended, may become hypoglycemic again.
4. If release, ensure someone will stay with the patient and with permission with medical control. Follow appropriate release protocol.
