SEIZURE

Any patient seen with current or recent generalized seizure activity.

All Providers:

  1. Do not restrain, but provide protection during the tonic-clonic phase.
  2. Spinal motion restriction as appropriate.
  3. Ensure patients experiencing febrile seizures are not excessively dressed or bundled.
  4. Any child <12 months old with seizure activity should be transported to the ED for further evaluation.
  5. Assess scene for possible toxin, overdose or trauma.

EMT

Focused history and physical exam.

  1. Blood glucose, temperature and oxygen saturation assessment.
  2. Question patient/family regarding possibility of pregnacy.
  3. Assess scene for possible toxin, overdose or trauma.
  4. PROTECT the patient from physical harm and embarrassment.
  5. ASSESS LEVEL OF CONSCIOUSNESS and maintain AIRWAY as indicated.
  6. Place patient in lateral recumbent position to prevent aspiration.
  7. SUCTION as necessary.
  8. OXYGEN via NC nonrebreather mask at 15L as needed to maintain pulse-ox >94%

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is completed.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. ASSIST VENTILATION as necessary.
  4. SALINE IV at TKO rate or saline lock.
  5. CHECK GLUCOMETER READING _ IF BELOW 60 mg/dl, give 50% Dextrose D50 IV PUSH.
  6. If no IV access, give Glucagon 1 mg IM.
  7. TRANSPORT in left lateral recumbent position unless an Advanced Airway has been placed.
  8. Monitor neurological status, airway and vitals closely enroute to hospital.
  9. If Actively seizing: chose one Benzodiazepine considering Protocol 509: Pain Control, Nausea, Vomiting, Anxiety.
  10. Contact OMLC before changing medications.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is completed.
  2. If seizure persists despite above therapies: support airway/ventilation. Discuss need for intubation with Control Phsician.
  3. Search for etiology:
    a. Head injury
    b. Cardiac arrhythmia
    c. CNS Infection
    d. Non-compliance with meds.
  4. For seizures that persist despite above therapy:
    a. ENDOTRACHEAL INTUBATION - if patient is unable to protect their airway - nasal route preferred if breathing.
    b. Contact medical control if additional doses of Versed are necessary.

Medical Control Option - Must contact before administration.

For females with advanced pregnancy and seizures: Magnesium Sulfate: 2-4 grams IM or 2-4 grams in 10 ml NS infuse over 15 to 30 min IV. as directed by OLMC.
