UPPER AIRWAY OBSTRUCTION

INDICATIONS

1. Patients with respiratory distress suspected of foreign body upper airway obstruction. Suspect if: sudden onset while eating or other object in mouth, inability to speak, paitent holding neck (universal distress signal), stridor. 2. Patients in cardiac arrest that occurred while eating.
3. Recall that infants and small children are primarily nose breathers; provide oral and nasal suctioning for copious secretions.
4. Keep patient NPO for any respiratory distress and if children have a RR>60.

PATIENT CONSCIOUS

EMT

1. Focused history and physical exam
2. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxgen, jaw-thrust, and positioning
3. Assess blood glucose, temperature and oxygen saturation.
4. Always insure proper care of the C-spine during airway treatment.
5. Keep patient NPO - Stop any tube feedings.
6. Determine adequacy of air exchange. Signs of INADEQUATE air exchange are:

  • Weak, ineffective cough
  • High-pitched noise while inhaling
  • Inability to speak
  • Increasing respiratory difficulty
  • Cyanosis
  • Decreasing Mental status or increasing agitation

  • Pulse-ox reading of <92%

IF ADEQUATE AIR EXCHANGE present:

1. Encourage continued coughing, oxygen, 4-6L NC or 15L via face mask.
2. Suction as necessary.

IF INADEQUATE AIR EXCHANGE:

Follow American Heart Association recommendation for patients with Partial or Total obstructed airway.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Evaluate for and remove any obvious airway obstruction.
    • For choking infants apply a sequence of 5 back blows and 5 chest thrusts until the item is dislodged.
    • For choking adults and children, use the abdominal thrust maneuver.
  3. Maintain airway administer 15L of oxygen vua NRB.
  4. Keep patient NPO for any respiratory disress and if children have a RR>60.
  5. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  6. Consider a 12 lead ECG.
  7. Establish IV access with NS @ TKO or Saline lock unless hypotensive then bolus 250-500 ml and reassess may repeat to max of 2 L
  8. Evaluate for and remove any obvious airway obstruction-follow American Heart Association recommendation for patients with Partial or Total obstructed airway.

PATIENT UNCONSCIOUS:

EMT

  1. Attempt to ventilate patient with BVM.
  2. Remove any easily visible foreign body.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, pulse-ox monitoring, suction as needed.
  3. Contact OLMC and transport promptly.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure all above is compete.
  2. Visualize the upper airway with LARYNGOSCOPE.
  3. REMOVE OBSTRUCTION with MAGILL FORCEPS or SUCTION.
  4. If no obstruction visualized, re-attempt to ventilate.
  5. If patient remains unconscious, INTUBATE per Intubation Protocol.
  6. If unable to ventilate due to unrelieved obstuction and unable to intubate, preform surgical cricothyrotomy to obtain an airway.
  7. TRANSPORT IMMEDIATELY
    9. Contact OLMC for further consultation including manipulation of the FB with the ET tube or stylet.
