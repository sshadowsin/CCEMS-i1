PEDIATRIC FRACTURES/DISLOCATIONS

INDICATIONS:

All patients who have suspected dislocations or fractures, especially of the pelvis or femur.

EMT

  1. Scene and patient management.
  2. Focused history and physical exam.
  3. Obtain a full set of Vital Signs including blood glucose.
  4. PERFORM PRIMARY AND SECONDARY SURVEY, including neurological and vascular exam of extremity distal to injury-document status.
  5. SPLINTING: Use appropriate device (Sam splint, traction, vacuum, pillow...)
  6. Uncomplicated fractures/dislocations with adequate circulation.
    • Splinted in a position of function/comfort.
  7. Fractures/dislocations with circulation deficits or severly angulated.
    • Treat with one attempt at placing the extremity in a position of function/comfort.
    • If unsuccessful, splint in position found and expedite transport.
    • Fractures and joint dislocations without palpable distal pulses are true orthopedic emergencies.
  8. Potential pelvic fractures.
    • Treatent of choice is application of the pelvic binder. If unavailable, a cloth sheet or blanket can be wrapped tightly around the pelvis to stabilize it.
  9. Isolated proximal femur (hip) fractures (especially in the elderly).
    • Best managed with anatomical splinting utilizing a scoop stretcher. Traction splints are not appropriate for any proximal femur fractures.
  10. Femoral shaft fractures.
    • Immobilized utilizing a traction spling unless one of the situations listed below is present.
      • Injuries just proximal to or involving the knee joint.
      • Injury to the pelvis.
      • Partial amputation.
      • Lower leg or ankle injuries.
      • If use would delay transport of a patient with a life-threatening condition.
  11. CHECK DISTAL CIRCULATION (color, temperature, capillary refill and pulses) BEFORE AND AFTER SPLINTING.
  12. Remove all rings and constricting clothing on the affected limb if possible.
  13. Maintain airway; assist with breathing as necessary, provide oxygen.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. If hypotensive bolus with NS 20 ml/kg then reassess - may give up to 60 ml/kg if not hypotensive TKO rate with a MAX bolus of 1 L.
  4. Treat for pain and anxiety, consider Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety . and contact OLMC for medication order:
    • Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg (max 25 mcg per dose) IV/IO.
    • In the event and IV cannot be established, Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg may be given IM up an initial dose of 25 mcg.
    • For additional doses, contact OLMC.

PARAMEDIC:

  1. Ensure the above is complete.
  2. Pain control Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety .
