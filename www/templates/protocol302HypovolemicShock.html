Hypovolemic Shock

INDICATION

  1. Patients who meet SHOCK CRITERIA, and have historical evidence of volume depletion: vomiting, diarrhea, bleeding, thirst, poor oral intake or physical evidence of volume depletion.
  2. Consider Shock in patients with one or more of the following.
    • Vital signs: HR >100, SBP of <90 mmHg for adults, SBP <70+(age in years x 2) mmHg for peds, or RR >20 BPM.
    • Skin signs: cold clammy skin, febrile, or delayed capillary refill.
    • Mental status: altered, lethargic, or irritable (esp. in infants).
    • Dry mucosa, skin tenting, or orthostatic VS.
  3. Evaluate for the source including infection bleeding/trauma, neurologic or cardiac.
  4. Pregnancy >20 weeks gestation - Transport in partial L lateral decubitus position. Place wedge-shaped cushion or multiple pillows under patient's right hip and shoulders to elevate R side 45 degrees.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Blood glucose, oxygen saturation and temperature assessment.
  3. C-spine immobilization, if indicated.
  4. Ensure patient warmth.
  5. Place in supine position.
  6. Control EXTERNAL BLEEDING:
    • Direct pressure and elevation.
    • Pressure point.
    • Tourniquet-Use CAT Protocol 512: EMS TOURNIQUET
    • OXYGEN-Nonrebreather mask at 15L/MIN; ASSIST VENTILATION if necessary.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. APPLY ECG MONITOR - if an arrhythmia is present go immediately to appropriate Protocol.
  4. IV LARGE BORE x-2 if able.

TRAUMATIC SHOCK

  1. Give fluid bolus 500 mL at a time, reassess and repeat as needed to:
    • Maintain SBP to 80-90 mmHg ( without closed head injury)
    • Maintain SBP to 110-120 mmHg ( with closed head injuty)
    • If BP above minimums are noted, patient should have saline lock (or TKO IV) and should NOT be given IV fluid boluses, unless BP falls betwen these limits.

NON-TRAUMATIC SHOCK

  1. Provide 500 mL boluses up to a maximum of 2 Liters and reassess for reversal of the signs of shock.
  2. If the patient remains hypotensive after 2 Liters, call OLMC
  3. INITIATE TRANSPORT as soon as possible. Maximum scene time permitted is 10 minutes unless circumstances dictate a longer time. (Prolonged extrication, extreme patient access, difficulties.. this shall be documented on the PCR).
  4. Insert Advanced Airway if Apneic.
  5. ENROUTE:
    • Consult OLMC.
    • Reassess vitals, LOC, airway and respiratory status (check lung sounds for fluid overload).
    • Start 2nd IV NS if possible.
    • Monitor ECG.
  6. Continue IVF NS wide open if shock persists after first liter and lung sounds clear. Use pressure bag if able, to assist delivery of IVF.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Advanced Airway as appropriate.
