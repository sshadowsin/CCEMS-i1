Asystole and Pulseless Electrical Activity

ALL CARDIAC ARREST VICTIMS SHOULD HAVE:

  1. CPR-Push hard and fast (100 - 120 compressions/minute).
  2. Ensure full chest recoil.
  3. Minimize interruptions in chest compressions.
  4. 1 cycle of CPR: 30 compressions/2 ventilations; 5 cycles=2 minutes.
  5. Avoid hyperventilation, 1 breath every 5-6 seconds (8-10 a minute)
  6. Secure airway and confirm placement, suction as needed
  7. After advanced airway is placed, give continuous chest compressions without pauses.
  8. Check rhythm every 2 minutes
  9. Elevate arm, give fluid bolus when administering a drug during a full arrest
  10. Always search for a reversible cause
  11. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines

EMT

FOLLOW PROTOCOL 101: MEDICAL CARDIAC ARREST- INITIAL CARE

  1. ABC's
  2. Focused history and physical exam.
  3. Assess for presence or absence of a pulse.
  4. Determine rhythm - asystole, pulseless electrical activity, etc.
  5. Begin CPR- if no pulse found
  6. Assess blood glucose level.
  7. Continue high quality CPR.
  8. Check for pulse/shockable rhythm every 5 cycles

ADVANCED EMT

FOLLOW PROTOCOL 101: MEDICAL CARDIAC ARREST- INITIAL CARE

  1. Consider Underlying Causes and Treat
  2. H's - Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Hydrogen ion (Acidosis), Hyper-/hypokalemia, Hypothermia, Hypoglycemia
  3. T's - Toxins (Overdose), Tamponade (Cardiac), Tension Pneumothorax, Thrombosis (ACS or Pulmonary), Trauma

Key Considerations

  1. Pregnancy >20 weeks gestation - Place wedge-shaped cushion or multiple pillows under patient's right hip (L lateral decubitus position)
  2. Pediatric lowest acceptable systolic blood pressures are birth to 1 month = 60 mmHg, 1 month to 1 year = 70 mmHg, 1 year to 10 years is = 70 mmHg + (age x 2) and over 10 years = 90 mmHg.
  3. Ensure airway is patent-use adjuncts-insert appropriate size "King " airway and confirm placement, suction as necessary.
  4. Continue high quality CPR at rate of 30:2
  5. Establish IV/IO access-x2 if possible, give 500 ml NS bolus
  6. Epinephrine 1mg 1:10,000 every 3-5 minutes IV/IO
  7. If asystole, confirm rhythm in 2 leads.
  8. Continuous ECG monitoring, Capnography.
  9. Administer fluid bolus 500 ml, repeat x 1 if no change
  10. AEMT must contact OLMC after initial dose of EPI for additional medication orders.

PARAMEDIC

FOLLOW PROTOCOL 101: MEDICAL CARDIAC ARREST- INITIAL CARE

  1. Ensure all of the above is being done appropriately.
  2. Establish IV/IO access if not done.
  3. Intubate when able and ensure proper tube placement if an advanced airway has not been placed, if an advanced airway is in place do not remove unless unable to ventilate
  4. Administer Epinephrine 1mg 1:10,000 IV/IO every 3-5 minutes.
  5. Continue high quality CPR
  6. Administer fluid bolus if not done (500-1000ml)
  7. Needle decompression if tension pneumothorax is present

MEDICAL CONTROL OPTION- MUST CONTACT MEDICAL CONTROL PRIOR TO ADMINISTRATION.

Sodium Bicarb ** - ADULT DOSE:

Cardiac Arrest: 1 meq/kg IV/IO

  1. use early if pre-existing metabolic acidosis, hyperkalemia, tricyclic antidepressant OD.
  2. prolonged resuscitation if ventilation is adequate.
  3. return of spontaneous circulation after long period of arrest
