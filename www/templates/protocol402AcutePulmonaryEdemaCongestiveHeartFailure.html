Acute Pulmonary Edema/Congestive Heart Failure

INDICATIONS

1. Respiratoy distress with history/exam suggestive or heart failure, such as: chest pain, diaphoresis, crackles in lungs, pink frothy sputum, peripheral edema and/or past history of CHF, MI or heart disease.

2. Hypoxia consisten with an oxygen saturation <90% or end-tidal CO2 >45.

EXCLUSIONS

  1. Cardiac arrythmias - See appropriate protocol Protocol 201: Bradycardia, Protocol 202: Tachycardia.
  2. Shock (poor perfusion or SBP <100mmHg) consider Protocol 303: Cardiogenic Shock.

NOTE:

1. Do not use Nitroglycerin if the patient has taken erectile dysfunction medications in the last 24 hours.
2. In pregnant patients of >20 weeks gestation: Place wedge-shaped cushion or multiple pillows under patients right hip in order to transport in left lateral decubitus position.

EMT

1. Focused history and physical exam.
2. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw thrust, and positioning.
3. Assess blood glucose, temperature and oxygen saturation.
4. SIT PATIENT UP - legs danglig over bed or stretcher (if possible).
5. Maintain airway; assist with breathing as necessary OXYGEN - 6L NC or 15L non-rebreather mask. Maintain pulse-ox >94%.
6. Contact online medical control and if so advised - assist patient with prescribed nitroglycerin SL every 5 minutes, up to 3 doses, as long as symptoms persist and SBP >100mmHg.

ADVANCED EMT

1. Ensure above is complete.
2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
3. Apply ECG monitor - obtain and transmit 12 lead ECG if available.
4. Saline IV @ TKO rate or NS lock if hypotensive give 250mL bolus and reevaluate 5. Establish OLMC for Medication administration which may include

Medications

  • Nitroglycerin 0.4mg every 5 minutes (max of 3 doses) SL if symptoms persist and SBP >100mmHg, maximize Nitroglycerin before Morphine .
  • Morphine 2-5mg IF every 5 minutes (max dose of 10mg) if symptoms persist and SBP >100mmHg.

7. TRANSPORT
8. Monitor level of consciousness, vital, pulse ox, air exchange and ECG enroute to hospital.
9. CPAP - provide CPAP of 5cm H20 to begin with and titrate PEEP slowly to maximum of 10 cm H20. *Higher PEEP can cause a severe reduction in venous return which can result in profound shock. Monitor VS closely. Refer to Protocol 403: Continuous Positive Airway Pressure.

PARAMEDIC

1. Ensure above is complete.
2. Endotracheal intubation if failure to improve on CPAP. This is usually unncessary with aggressive medical care but should be done if patient develops decreased LOC, hypotensionor respiratory failure.

3. If patient becoems agitated with increasing LOC after intubation, may give Versed 2-5mg slow IV every 10 minutes to the desured effect or max dose of 10mg.
4. If patient remains hypertensive and in severe repsiratory distress, Morphine 2-5mg IV bolus over 2 minutes. May repeat this dose based on patient response x1.
5. Observe carefully for respiratory depression after giving Morphine and be prepared to intubate.

6. If patient still has wheezes after above therapies, contact OLMC for immediate consultation and direction.
