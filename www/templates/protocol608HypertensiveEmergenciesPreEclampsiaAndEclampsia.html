HYPERTENSIVE EMERGENCIES: PRE-ECLAMPSIA AND ECLAMPSIA

INDICATIONS:

Paitents in third trimester of pregnancy who were normotensive prior to pregnancy and now have BP >140/90; this may be associated with edema of hands and face. This usually occurs during patient's first pregnancy.

NOTE:

Definitive treatment is delivery of fetus. Thus, rapid transport indicated. Hyper-reflexia and visual changes indicate imminent seizure.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Obtain compete set of vital signs including glucose.
  3. Take OBSTETRICAL HISTORY per Protocol 600: Obstetric Emergencies and inquire whether patient has been diagnosed with hypertension of pregnancy or preeclampsia.
  4. Place patient in comfortable position -- elevate right side if supine.
  5. Administer OXYGEN 4-6 L per NC or 15 L nonrebreather mask.
  6. If patient has generalized (grand mal) seizure: Assure adequate ventilation and oxygenation.
  7. Protect airway - suction as needed.
  8. Do not place anything in the mouth.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Initiate transport and contact PLMC Physician - anticipate seizure.
  4. Monitor vitals, airway and neurological status enroute.
  5. Start IV at TKO rate or saline lock.
  6. For actively seizing refer to Protocol 308: Seizure.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. For actively seizing refer to Protocol 308: Seizure.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. High-risk preterm labor when delivery is imminent, to suppress uterine contractions:
    a. Rapidly infuse 1 liter of NS (AEMT/PM)
    b. Albuterol 2.5 mg via nebulization (AEMT/PM).
    c. Magnesium Sulfate 1 gram IV and titrate per Medical Control (PM only).

  2. For females with advanced pregnancy and seizures:
    a. Magnesium Sulfate - 2-4 grams IM or 2-4 grams in 10 ml NS infuse over 15 to 30 minutes.
    b. PEDIATRIC (<15 years of age, PM only):
    c. Magnesium Sulfate - For pediatric patiens who are pregnant and haveing a seizure contact OLMC for dosage insturctions.
