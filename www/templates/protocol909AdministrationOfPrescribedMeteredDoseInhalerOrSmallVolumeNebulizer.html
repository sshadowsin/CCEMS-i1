Administration of Prescribed Metered-Dose Inhaler or Small Volume Nebulizer

This protocol is for the EMT First Responder only.

Determine Need for PRESCRIBED INHALER:

  1. Refer to Protocol 401: BRONCHOSPASM (WHEEZING)
  2. Patient has history of respiratory emergency and is prescribed an inhaler
  3. Patient exhibits signs and symptoms of respiratory emergency
  4. Shortness of breath
  5. Restlessness
  6. Use of accessory muscles for breathing
  7. Nasal flaring
  8. Tripod position
  9. Patient capable of following directions
  10. Place Patient on high flow O2 via non-rebreather mask
  11. Obtain order from online medical control to administer bronchodilator per protocol

Inquire About Previous Doses:

  1. Total doses taken/administered
  2. When last dose taken/administered
  3. Effects of previous dose
  4. Any other interventions

Obtain inhaler or small volume nebulizer:

  1. Ensure the “six rights” of medication administration:
  2. Right patient – prescribed to patient
  3. Right medication – for patient condition
  4. Right dose – metered dose or ampul
  5. Right route - inhaled
  6. Right time – within expiration date

Contact OLMC prior to administering for order

Prepare and Administer Inhaler or nebulizer:

Inhaler:

  1. Read and follow inhaler manufacturer's directions
  2. Take patient off O2 for duration of treatment
  3. Have patient exhale deeply
  4. Have patient place lips around opening of inhaler
  5. Have patient depress the hand held inhaler while simultaneously inhaling deeply
  6. Instruct patient to hold breath for as long as possible
  7. Repeat per medical direction

Nebulizer:

  1. Insert medication into container, adds sterile saline if needed.
  2. Assemble nebulizer mouthpiece and tubing
  3. Connect to O2 Supply, flow at ≈ 6L/min. to produce mist
  4. Instruct patient to places mouthpiece between lips and inhales deeply
  5. Instruct patient holds inhaled breath for 3-5 seconds
  6. When mist dissipates discontinue treatment
  7. Replace non-rebreather mask with high flow O2

Assess respiratory status and patient‘s response.
