Hypothermic Cardiac Arrest

ALL CARDIAC ARREST VICTIMS SHOULD HAVE:

  1. CPR-Push hard and fast (100 - 120 compressions/minute).
  2. Ensure full chest recoil.
  3. Minimize interruptions in chest compressions.
  4. 1 cycle of CPR: 30 compressions/2 ventilations; 5 cycles=2 minutes.
  5. Avoid hyperventilation, 1 breath every 5-6 seconds (8-10 a minute)
  6. Secure airway and confirm placement, suction as needed
  7. After advanced airway is placed, give continuous chest compressions without pauses.
  8. Check rhythm every 2 minutes
  9. Elevate arm, give fluid bolus when administering a drug during a full arrest
  10. Always search for a reversible cause
  11. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines

INDICATIONS:

  1. Patients unresponsive and pulseless, have no signs of life after sustaining significant cold exposure. This may include elderly and chronically ill patients with prolonged exposure to mild temperatures.
  2. Patients may appear clinically dead due to severe depression of cardiac and CNS function. The patient may feel cold, stiff, and dependant lividity may be present. Despite these findings, resuscitation with full neurological recovery is possible in some cases.
  3. Pulses and respiratory effort may be extremely difficult to detect, so these should be checked for a full 30-60 seconds.

ALL:

  1. Remove wet garments
  2. Protect against heat loss and wind chill (use blankets and insulating equipment)
  3. Maintain horizontal position
  4. Avoid rough movement and excess activity
  5. Monitor core temperature if possible
  6. Assess responsiveness, breathing, and pulses
  7. Apply AED

MILD HYPOTHERMIA (CORE TEMP >93° F (34°C)

  1. BREATHING AND PULSE PRESENT: Passive rewarming (heat packs, warm blankets, radiant heat sources…)

  2. BREATHING AND PULSE ABSENT:

    1. Start CPR-BLS per PROTOCOL 101: MEDICAL CARDIAC ARREST- INITIAL CARE
    2. Give 1 shock if allowed by AED
    3. Resume CPR immediately at rate of 30:2
    4. Additional shocks can be given with the approval from medical control

ADVANCED EMT

  1. ABC's
  2. Establish IV/IO with WARM NS
  3. Monitor arrhythmias- cardiac arrest rhythms
  4. Continuous ECG monitoring, capnography
  5. Rhythm specific drugs, Epinephrine , Amiodarone , or Lidocaine per current ACLS guidelines
  6. If in cardiac arrest-insert appropriate size "King" airway and confirm placement.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure all above is being done appropriately
  2. Intubate and confirm proper tube placement if an advanced airway is not in place, if an Advanced Airway is in place do not remove unless unable to ventilate
  3. Monitor arrhythmias- cardiac arrest rhythms
  4. Rhythm specific drugs per current ACLS guidelines

MODERATE HYPOTHERMIA (CORE TEMP 86-93° F (30-34° C) OR MORE)

ALL

  1. Passive rewarming
  2. Active rewarming of trunk only
  3. Begin BLS

EMT

  1. Begin BLS
  2. Apply AED and give 1 shock if allowed
  3. Resume CPR immediately at rate of 30:2

ADVANCED EMT

  1. ABC's
  2. Establish IV/IO with WARM NS
  3. Monitor arrhythmias- cardiac arrest rhythms
  4. Rhythm specific drugs per ACLS guidelines but space at longer intervals
  5. Repeat defibrillation for VF/VT as core temperature rises
  6. If in cardiac arrest-insert appropriate size "King" airway and confirm placement

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure all above is being done appropriately
  2. Intubate and confirm tube placement if an advanced airway has not been placed, if an Advanced Airway is in place do not remove unless unable to ventilate
  3. Monitor cardiac rhythm
  4. Rhythm specific drugs per ACLS guidelines but space at longer intervals
  5. Repeat defibrillation for VF/VT as core temperature rises

SEVERE HYPOTHERMIA CORE TEMPERATURE <86°F (30°C):

EMT

  1. Begin BLS
  2. Apply AED- limit to 1 shock only for VF/VT
  3. Additional shocks at Medical Control Approval

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Continue CPR-BLS per PROTOCOL 101: MEDICAL CARDIAC ARREST- INITIAL CARE.
  2. Withhold IV Medications until Medical Control has been contacted or until core temperature increases above 86F then Rhythm specific drugs per current ACLS guidelines but space at longer intervals
  3. Monitor cardiac rhythm
  4. If in cardiac arrest-insert appropriate size "King" airway and confirm placement

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure all above is being done appropriately
  2. Intubate and confirm tube placement if an advanced airway has not been placed. If an Advanced Airway is in place do not remove unless unable to ventilate,
  3. Monitor cardiac rhythm
  4. Rapid transport to ED for Active internal warming
