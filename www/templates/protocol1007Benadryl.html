Benadryl

Diphenhydramine
Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1007

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 310, 704.

Class: Antihistamine, CNS Depressant

MECHANISM OF ACTION
1. Blocks H1 release (Which Causes Bronchoconstriction)
2. Blocks H2 release (Which causes peripheral vasodilation)
3. Antidote-dystonic reactions from phenothiazines

INDICATIONS
1. Allergic Reactions
2. Anaphylaxis
3. Dystonic Reactions

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Asthma
2. Pregnancy
3. COPD

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Decreased LOC
2. Hypotension

Dosage and Administration

1. Adults: 25mg IV/IO/IM for moderate to severe allergic reaction.
2. Pediatric: 1mg/kg IV/IO/IM for moderate to severe allergic reaction MAX DOSE 25mg

CONSIDERATIONS
1. Will potentiate CNS depression in presence of ETOH, Narcotics.
2. Should be used after Epinephrine in cases of acute allergic reactions.
3. Give slow IVP. Rapid administration can cause Hypotension
4. Higher doses may be ordered by on-line medical control to treat dystonic reactions cause by phenothiazine reactions.
