MECONIUM ASPIRATION

INDICATIONS:

Meconium is amniotic fluid prior to delivery of fetusl this appears as dark green, thick material in amniotic fluid or on infant upon deliver.

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. Upon noting presence of meconium, notify receiving hospital.
  2. Upon deliver of head, SUCTION MOUTH but not nares with bulb syringe. Avoid stimulating nares to breathe.
  3. Suctioning may be the only intervention required- the presence of meconium does not mean automatic intubation.
  4. Assess airway and respiratory status.
  5. Resuscitation as necessary.
  6. Paramedic Only-If apnea or acute respiratory distress in noted after suction prepare to intubate:
    a. INTUBATE (tube size = 3.5, blade size = 1) and SUCTION through endotracheal tube until there is no meconium.
  7. Remove contaminated tube and re-intubate.

PRIOR TO VENTILATION:

  1. Carefully check for proper tube placement and VENTILATE neonate.
  2. First few breaths may require higher than expected pressure.
  3. Carefully SUCTION nares and oropharynx.
  4. Assess APGAR (see chart below) and pulse oximetry.
  5. TRANSPORT to appropriate facility.
