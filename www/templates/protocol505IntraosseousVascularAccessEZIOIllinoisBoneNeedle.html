INTRAOSSEOUS VASCULAR ACCESS "E_Z (I/O)-Illinois Bone Needle"

ADVANCED EMT/PARAMEDIC (CCEMS RESCUE/AMBULANCE PERSONNEL ONLY)

RATIONAL: This protocol is for the CCEMS Paramedic/Ambulance Advanced EMT and addresses the establishment of I/O access in the adult/pediatric patient where venous access cannot be rapidly obtained. The " E-Z I/O" or Illinois Bone Nede will provide a rapid alternative means of vascular access into the bone marrow of the proximal tibia in the critically unstable adult, or in a cardiac arrest patient. The tibial marrow space serves as a "non-collapsible vein" and provides access to the general circulation for the administration of all ACLS resuscitation drugs and IV fluids.

INDICATIONS:

  1. Attempts at establishing a peripheral line have failed (2 attempts, or 90 seconds or no readily venous access is identified) in the following:
    a. Adult/Pediatric cardiac arrest after attempts at peripheral lines have failed, or no readily venous access is identified.
    b. In an unresponsive critical trauma patient with unstable vital signs and attempts (2) at peripheral lines have failed or no readily venous access is identified.
    c. Consider the Illinois bone needle (18 ga) in neonates (<30 days of life).

EQUIPMENT

  1. E-Z IO or Illinois bone needle.
  2. Alcohol preps and gloves
  3. E-Z stabilizer and tape
  4. Select-3 IV tubing
  5. 1000 ml bag of NS
  6. 10 ml pre-filled NS syringes
  7. Pressure bag

PROCEDURE:

  1. Patient is placed in a supine position.
  2. Assemble all necessary equipment, attach and flush the EZ-IO tubing with a pre-filled NS syringe and a IV set with pressure bag (set up and flush maintaining sterility).
  3. Identify and locate the bony landmarks, the proximal tibia is the only approved site.
  4. Place a rolled towel under knee with a foot facing outward )optional).
  5. The proximal tibial insertion site is approximately 2 cm below the patella and approximately 2 cm medial to the tibial tuberosity (depending on patient anatomy).
  6. Prep site with alcohol.
  7. Assess the tissue depth and insert the the appropriate E-Z IO needle until contact is made with the tibia - ensure a black mark is visible above the surgace of the skin (this will correspond to sufficient depth of the needle to be appropriately placed with the bone cavity.
  8. Firmly hold the E-Z IO at a 90-degree angle to the surface of the skin.
  9. While continuing to hold the bottom part firmly against the tibia, engage the drill and allow the needle to enter the tibia. *Extra force is not required.
  10. Once resistance is gone release the trigger and while holding the needle remove the drill and stylet.
  11. If the Illinois bone needle is used follow the Utah EMSC guidelines/ SOP's for insertion.
  12. It is suggested to attach the E-Z Stabilizer.
  13. Attach the E-Z IO tubing to the cannnula and with the 10 ml pre-filled saline syringe attempt to aspirate bone marrow (not necessary) and flush. *Lack of bone marrow does not mean that the I/O is inappropriately placed, however, it should flush easily. If no bone marrow is aspirated and unable to easily flush with NS then remove the cannula and choose another site.
  14. Connect the IV infusion set using a pressure bag, this should be done quickly to prevent the possibility of air embolization.
  15. Administer fluids and/or drugs as required.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:

  1. Placement of an I/O line in a fractured bone.
  2. Placement of an I/O line distal to a fractured bone (tibial placement with a fracture femur).
  3. Prior I/O puncture in the same bone. Osteogenesis Imperfecta-an inherited condition where the bones are abnormally brittle and subject to fractures.
  4. A prosthetic limb.
  5. Infection or burns are relative contraindications at the intended site-the Medical Control Physicians should be contacted prior to the insertion attempt.

MEDICAL CONTROL OPTIONS:

  1. An E-Z IO may be placed in a conscious patient under the following circumstances:
    a. Paitent is deemed critically ill/injuried with unstable vital signs.
    b. No peripheral vascular access is readily accessible.
Procedure:
  1. Contact online medical control.
  2. Follow above listed procedure concerning placement.
  3. Prior to running fluids administer 40 mg (2 mL) of 2% Lidocaine thru the IO and flush with 10 mL of NS, wait 15 seconds and administer IV fluid.
  4. Contact Medical Control if additional pain control is neccessary.
