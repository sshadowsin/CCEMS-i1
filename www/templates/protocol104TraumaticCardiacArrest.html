Traumatic Cardiac Arrest

INDICATIONS:

All patients found unresponsive and without pulses on initial evaluation. (Note that agonal respirations may be present in recently arrested patients).

ALL CARDIAC ARREST VICTIMS SHOULD HAVE:

  1. CPR-Push hard and fast (100 - 120 compressions/minute).
  2. Ensure full chest recoil.
  3. Minimize interruptions in chest compressions.
  4. 1 cycle of CPR: 30 compressions/2 ventilations; 5 cycles=2 minutes.
  5. Avoid hyperventilation, 1 breath every 5-6 seconds (8-10 a minute)
  6. Secure airway and confirm placement, suction as needed
  7. After advanced airway is placed, give continuous chest compressions without pauses.
  8. Check rhythm every 2 minutes
  9. Elevate arm, give fluid bolus when administering a drug during a full arrest
  10. Always search for a reversible cause
  11. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines

SCENE TIME:

Goal of 10 minutes maximum on-scene time unless pt(s) are entrapped (note in report)

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Patients fitting strict DOA Protocol
  2. Arrest associated with hypothermia- use Protocol 105: HYPOTHERMIC CARDIAC ARREST,
  3. Children <50kg- use Pediatric Protocols.

TRAUMA NOTIFICATION:

Notify medical control at earliest opportunity to activate the trauma system network. Provide a trauma score and classify the patient as a trauma evaluation versus a trauma 1 based on current medical criteria.

EMT

BCLS PRIMARY SURVEY

  1. Airway: Open airway with non-invasive techniques (jaw thrust without head extension if trauma is suspected), suction as needed
  2. Breathing: Look, listen, and feel for adequate breathing. Begin BVM respirations. Do not hyperventilate or over-inflate. Use airway adjunct as indicated (OPA/NPA). Ventilate at a rate of 1 breath every 5-6 seconds. Ensure supplemental oxygen is being used.
  3. Circulation: Check for a carotid pulse for at least 5 seconds but not more than 10 seconds. Provide high quality CPR at a rate of 30:2 while attaching AED. Give 5 cycles before attempting to shock, if in VF. Push hard, push fast (100 compressions per minute) and release completely. Minimize interruptions in compressions. Control severe external bleeding with direct pressure.
  4. Defibrillation: Provide shocks as indicated by the voice prompts on the AED. Follow each shock immediately with CPR, beginning with chest compressions. Check rhythm every 5 cycles. Continue until ALS providers take over or patient starts to move.
  5. Stabilization: Quickly immobilize on a longboard with a cervical collar

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Airway: Maintain airway patency with use of OPA or NPA if not in use, suction if needed. Insert appropriate size "king airway" and confirm placement.
  2. Breathing: Ensure supplemental oxygen is being used. Assess adequacy of oxygenation and ventilation by:
    1. Observing chest rise.
    2. Oxygen saturation
    3. Capnography
  3. Circulation: Obtain IV/IO access. Give fluids if needed (2 large bore if able). Monitor for arrhythmias or cardiac arrest rhythms (VF, VT, PEA, and Asystole). Give appropriate drugs to manage rhythm Epinephrine , Amiodarone , or Lidocaine per ACLS protocol)
  4. Defibrillation: Provide shocks as indicated. Follow each shock with CPR for 5 cycles per PROTOCOL 101: MEDICAL CARDIAC ARREST- INITIAL CARE
  5. Stabilization: Immobilize quickly on long board and cervical collar if not done and prepare for rapid transport.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Airway: Ensure airway is patent, suction as needed. Intubate when appropriate using in-line stabilization if an advanced airway has not been placed.
  2. Breathing: Confirm proper placement of advanced airway by:
    1. Physical examination
    2. Secure the device to prevent dislodgement
    3. Capnography
    4. Use of esophageal detector (Optional).
    5. Auscultation
  3. Circulation: Obtain IV/IO access if not already done. Monitor arrhythmias or cardiac arrest rhythms. Give appropriate drugs to manage rhythms (epi, lidocaine or amiodarone, atropine, magnesium, Sodium bicarb) and blood pressure.
  4. Defibrillation: Rhythm appropriate per ACLS current standards, shock as indicated. Follow each shock with CPR for 5 cycles.

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSIS: Search for/find and treat reversible causes of the arrest.

H- Hypovolemia T- Tablets
H- Hypoxia T- Tamponade, cardiac
H- Hydrogen ion (acidosis) T- Tension pneumothorax
H- Hyperkalemia T- Thrombosis, heart (acute MI)
H- Hypothermia T- Thrombosis, pulmonary (PE)
H-Hypoglycemia T- Trauma

Hypovolemia and hypoxia are the 2 most common and easily reversible causes of PEA.

STABILIZATION: Ensure quick spinal immobilization and begin rapid transport.

EN ROUTE:

  1. Re-assess airway, ventilation, tube position, oxygen supply
  2. Intubate if a King airway has not been inserted on scene
  3. Establish 2nd large bore IV/IO if not done, administer fluid challenge
  4. Notify Emergency Department of inbound trauma level (Trauma Eval. Vs. Trauma 1)
