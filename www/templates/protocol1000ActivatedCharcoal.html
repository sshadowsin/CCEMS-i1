Activated Charcoal

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1000

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Class: ADSORBENT/ANTIDOTE

Reference Protocols: 311.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Activated carbon is used to treat poisonings and overdoses following oral ingestion. It is not effective for a number of poisonings including strong acids or alkali, cyanide, iron, lithium, arsenic, methanol, ethanol, or ethylene glycol.
2. The use of activated carbon is contraindicated when the ingested substance is an acid, an alkali, or a petroleum product.

INDICATIONS
1. Poisonings with: ASA, Amphetamines, Dilantin, Tylenol, Phenobarbital, Barbiturates, Cocaine, TCA's, Mushrooms, and Other Toxins.

CONTRAINDICATIONS

  1. Decreased LOC
  2. Acidic Substance Ingested
  3. Alkalotic Substance Ingested
  4. Petroleium Substance Ingested
  5. Camphor (Vicks Vapo-rub)
  6. Pesticides
  7. Iron
  8. Lithium
  9. Solvents

  10. Alcohol
    Side Effects

    • Vomiting

    • Diarrhea
    • Abdominal Cramping

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Vomiting
2. Diarrhea
3. Abdominal Cramping

Dosage and Administration

a. ADMINISTER CHARCOAL by orders of OLMC Only: Do not give for liquid ingestion or hydrocarbon ingestions
b. Adults: Charcoal 25 grams by mouth if the patient is alert, awake and gag reflex is intact
c.Pediatrics: Charcoal 1g/kg up to 25 grams by mouth if the patient is alert, awake and gag reflex is intact

CONSIDERATIONS

1. PATIENT MUST BE ALERT WITH A GAG REFLEX AND ANTICIPATED TO REMAIN SO DURING TREATMENT PERIOD
2. MAY MIX WITH JUICE OR SODA BUT NOT MILK
3. ANTICIPATE VOMITING
4. BRING ALL CONTAINERS TO ED FOR IDENTIFICATIONS
5. NEVER LEAVE UNATTENDED
