Pitocin

Oxytocin
Approved for Rescue Paramedics Only

Protocol 1021

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 602, 607.

Hormone / Uterine Stimulant

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Causes Contraction of Uterine Smooth Muscle.
2. Decreases Uterine Blood Flow.

INDICATIONS

1. Postpartum Hemorrhage.

CONTRAINDICATIONS

1. Delivery Has Not Occurred.
2. Possibility of Additional Fetus.

SIDE EFFECTS

1. Dysrhythmias.
2. Hypotension.
3. Seizures.
4. Fetal Bradycardia if Given Before Delivery.

Considerations

1. Can Cause Uterine Rupture.
2. Vital Signs and Uterine Tone Should be Monitored.

Dosage and Administration

MEDICAL CONTROL MUST BE CONTACTED PRIOR TO ADMINISTRATION

WARNING: PITOCIN IS NOT AN IV PUSH DRUG AND MUST BE GIVEN AS AN IV INFUSION

Adult Dose

1. IM (Intramuscularly) - If IV Access is Unavailable - Give 10 units IM

2. IV Infusion: May be Started if Bleeding Continues by Adding 40 Units to 1000mL NS Begin Titrating the Infusion to Decrease Bleeding and Improve Patient Comfort.
