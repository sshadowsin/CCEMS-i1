PEDIATRIC MAJOR OR MULTI-SYSTEM TRAUMA

Goals

  1. To maintain the airway and circulatory status of the head injured patient and to protect the spine.
  2. To record an initial examination and GCS.
  3. To institute measures that may reduce intracranial pressure.
  4. To expeditiously present to the hospital team the patient who may require neurosurgical intervention.

Key Considerations:

  1. TBI may be painful: however pain medications can cloud serial neurological assessments. Routine pain medications should not be administered to a patient with altered mental status after TBI.
  2. Patients with TBI may be confused or combative.
  3. Consider restraints if needed to protect everyone.
  4. Loss of memory, prolonged confusion or altered mental status associated with trauma may indicate a significant head injury.
  5. Pediatric lowest acceptable systolic blood pressures are:
    • Birth to 1 month = 60 mmHg.
    • 1 month to 1 year = 7- mmHg.
    • 1 year to 10 years = 70 mmHg + (age x 2).
    • Over 10 years = 90 mmHg.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Full set of Vital Signs including blood glucose.
  3. Provide basic airway manuevers to all comrpomised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  4. IMMOBILIZE and protect the cervical spine with a rigid collar and backboard/CID or backboard with towels and tape.
  5. Provide 100% oxygen to the patient mantaining C-Spine precautions if necessary.
  6. Administer supplemental oxygen for any saturation <90% or if uable to obtain a reliable pulse oximeter reading. In general, maintain an oxygen saturation 90-94%.
  7. ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress. Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  8. Document a GCS for the patient.
  9. If GCS < 8 consider an advanced airway.
  10. Do not hyperventilate excessively. If patient has an obvious unilateral pupillary dilation, increase respiratory rate by 10% above normal targe respiratory rate (RR) until the patient improves.
  11. Open skull fractures should be covered with non-pressure dey sterile dressing.
  12. Control EXTERNAL BLEEDING.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Establish vascular access if able.
  4. Ventilate to maintain EtCO2 between 35-40 mmHg when capnography is available.
  5. Check blood pressure every 5-10 minutes.
  6. Establish IV access-may initiate NS 20 ml/kg for hypotension OR if unable to obtain blood pressure.
  7. If hypertensive patient shows no improvement with initial treatement, may repeat NS 20 ml/kg up to a total of 60 ml/kg.
  8. If actively seizing contact OLMC for mediation order and consider Protocol 714: Pediatric Seizures .
    • Midazolam (Versed) :
      • Dosage is cut in half it the paitent has received narcotics or alcholo.
      • Consider the size of the patient for dosing.
      • IV/IO - 0.1 mg/kg (max dose for 4 mg per dose) SLOW IV.
      • Intranasal or oral - 0.2 mg/kg (max dose 10mg per dose).
      • Contact OLMC for additional doses.

PARAMEDIC:

  1. Ensure the above is complete.
  2. Maintain in-line cervical immobilization (NOT TRACTION).
  3. If GCS <8 consider an advanced airway.
  4. INTUBATION- Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated According to current PALS guidelines.
    • Document _TWO confirmation methods to varify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophogeal Intubation Detector.
    • Insert an appropriately sized endotracheal tube and secure.
    • Consider sedation after intubation Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety .
    • Administer Lidocaine 1.0 mg/kg IV or IO 60-90 seconds prior to intubation (if IV access readily available).
  5. Following intubation, ELEVATE head of backboard 20-30 degrees.
  6. If actievly seizing: Midazolam (Versed) and consider Protocol 714: Pediatric Seizures .
    • Dosage is cut in half if the patient has received narcotics or alcohol.
    • Consider the size of the patient for dosing.
    • IV/IO - 0.1 mg/kg (max dose of 4 mg per dose).
    • Do NOT exceed adult dosing.
    • Intranasal or oral - 0.2 mg/kg (max dose 10mg per dose).
    • Contact OLMC for additional doses.
  7. No nasotracheal intubation.
