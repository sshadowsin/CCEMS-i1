SPINAL CORD INJURY

The goals of this Protocol are:

  • Maintenance of ventilation and perfusion.
  • Prevention of futher injury by proper hadeling.
  • Transport of the patient to a trauma center.

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. Spinal Immobilization criteria - Immobilize patients with a cervical collar and spine board if there is a traumatic mechanism of injury and any of the below criteria are met:
    • Age <8 or >65
    • Patient complains of neck or spine pain.
    • There is any neck or spinal tenderness.
    • There is any abnormal mental staus or GCS<15.
    • There is any evidence of alcohol or drug intoxication.
    • There are other severely painful or distracting injuries present.
    • Any patient that the medic feels should be immobilized, based on the clinical judgment of the patient or situation.
  2. Mechanism suggestive of potential spine injury, such as:
    • MVA
    • Blunt facial trauma.
    • GSW near midline.
    • Fall.
  3. If spinal immobilization is to be applied:
    • Explain the need for spinal immobilization to the patient.
    • Apply appropriate cervical immobilization.
    • Apply appropriate backboard and security straps.
    • PEDS- Use a pediatric specific backboard for those <8 years old or use a towel or pad to raise the child's body (not their head) to insure appropriate spinal alignment on the adult board. Age <2 should be immobilized in a car seat or age appropriate papoose device.
    • Spinal immobilization should be considered a treatment of preventative thearpy.
    • Patients who are likely to beniefit from immobilization should undergo this treatement.
    • Patients who are not likely to benefit from immobilization, who have a low likelihood of spinal injury, may not be immobilized providing they do not meet any of the spinal immobilization criteria as listed above.
    • Ambulatory patients who are alert and cooperative may be safely immobilized on a gurney with cervical collar and straps and will not generally require a spine board unless they meet any of the spinal immobilization vriteria as listed above.
    • Contact OLMC for further instructions if the patient reguses immobilization despite the provider's assessment for the need for spinal immobilization.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical examination.
  2. Blood glucose, Oxygen and Temperature assessment.
  3. Evaluate the mechanism of injury.
  4. Assess exposure to drugs, alcohol or other toxins including environmental toxins.
  5. Assess history of arthritis, cancer, or other possible spine/bone diseases.
  6. Assess environment, location of patietn, and need for extrication.
  7. As appropriate, determine if pregnant and place in left lateral decubitus postion if >20 weeks gestation.
  8. Assess neurological function before, during and after application of spinal immobilization.
  9. SUPPORT AND REASSURE PATIENT. Explain all procedures. make no definitive comments regarding prognosis, extent of injury, etc. MANUALLY IMMOBILIZE C-spine until adequately secured.
  10. AIRWAY-OXYGEN Non-rebreather mask at 15L. ASSIST VENTILATION as needed.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETC02, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. ASSES PERFUSION.
  4. ADEQUATE PERFUSION - IV Large bore NS at slow-moderate rate, goal is to maintain systolic BP within normal range.
  5. POOR PERFUSION (altered mentation, cool extremities, hypotension), then immediately institute following measures:
    a. Run NS wide open for 2 liters; start second IV if possible. Monitor lung sounds and respiratory status.
    b. Rapidly but carefully IMMOBILIZE and EXPEDITE TRANSPORT
  6. EXTRICATE WITH CARE
  7. DOCUMENT carefully initial and subsequent neurolgical exam findings.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Consider advanced airway if airway compromised or unable to keep oxygen or Capnography within normal limits.
  3. Maintain strict in-line stabilization during procedure.
  4. Contact OLMC

Medical Control Options - Must contact prior to administration

Hypotension refractory to fluid boluses

  1. Additional IV Bolus
  2. Small dose of Epinephrine IV as directed by Medical Control Physician.

  3. If patient becomes severely hypotensive (SBP <90) and symptomatic and remains hypotensive despite fluid boluses:

    Epinephrine 2-10 mcg/min start at 2mcg/min. Titrate systolic BP until it reaches 90-100 mmHg. Mix Epinephrine 2mg 1:1000 in 1000 mL NS which yields 0.002mg/mL (2mcg/mL) use dial-a-flow extension set following the chart below.
