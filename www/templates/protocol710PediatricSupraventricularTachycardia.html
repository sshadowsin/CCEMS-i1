PEDIATRIC SUPRAVENTRICULAR TACHYCARDIA

NOTE:

  1. For all arrhythmias begin with the following Protocol 708: Pediatric Cardiac Arrythmias .
  2. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines as needed.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess blood glucose level.
  3. Assess for signs of poor perfusion, hypotension, altered mental status, signs of shock, chest pain, or acute heart failure.
  4. Heart rate-determine ST (Sinus Tach) vs Supraventricular Tach (SVT).
  5. Capillary refill >2 seconds.
  6. Absent or weak peripheral pulses.
  7. Cool, cold, cyanotic extremities.
  8. Poor muscle tone.
  9. Altered level of consciousness (lethargy or failure to recognize parents).
  10. Hypotension (see chart below)
AGE PRESSURE
0-2 Months 60 mm Hg
2 months to 1 Year 70 mm Hg
1 Year 70 + 2 x (years in age)
Over 10 Years 90 mm Hg

Pediatric Tachycardia Heart Rates:

Sinus Tachycardia Supraventricular Tachycardia
Infant: Usually <220 Infant: Usually >220
Child: Usually <180 Child: Usually >180

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Perform a 12 ECG and transmit to receiving hospital if possible.
  4. Identify and treat the underlying cause (e.g. hyptension, pain, medication, heart failure, shokc, fever, hypoxia, etc.).
  5. Determine probable rhythm - sinus tachycardia, supraventricular tachycardia, ventricular tachycardia, etc.
  6. Contact Medical Control.
  7. Establiish vascular access if able.
  8. If hypotensive give NS 20 ml/kg then TKO rate unless directed otherwise by on-line Medical control.
  9. SUPRAVENTRICULAR TACHYCARDIA:
    • In stable patients obtain a 12 lead ECG, if possible and send to receiving hospital to establish a baseline.
      • Infants: SVT rate usually greater than 220 bpm with no variation.
      • Children: SVT rate usually greater than 180 bpm
    • In stable patients may perform maneuvers to increase vagal tone (valsalva, ice pack to face, Tredelenburg position).

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endortacheal tube when indicated or appropriate supraglottic airway.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. No nasotracheal intubateion.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Adenosine
    a. May be given for narrow or wide complex tachycardia if regular and monomorphic.
    b. Administer 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO to max 6 mg.
    c. If no conversion, repeat with 0.2 mg/kg IV/IO to max 12 mg.
    d. May repeat in 5 mintues up to max 3 doses total.

  2. Stable Wide Complex (QRS>120 msec) Tachycardia

Lidocaine IS THE ONLY ANTIARRHYTHMIC TO BE USED FOR RHYTHM CONVERSION. Amiodarone IS NOT TO BE USED.

Lidocaine: 1 mg/kg IV/IO
a. May repeat every 3-5 minutes up to 3 mg/kg
b. If conversion to a Sinus Rhythm begin Lidocaine ggt.

  1. Unstable Tachycardia - Synchronized Cardioversion:

    • If no response to previous internevtions and the patient has "Serious signs of symptoms" such as:
      • Active Congestive Heart Failure/Pulmonary Edema.
      • Altered Mental Status.
      • Lowest acceptable systolicblood pressures are: birth to 1 month = 60 mmHg, 1 month to 1 year = 70 mmHg, 1 year to 10 years is = 70 mmHg + (age x 2) and over 10 years = 9- mmHg.

    • Signs of shock:

      • Cool, clammy, or pale skin.

      • Weak or thready pulse.

  2. Synchronized Cardioversion:

    • Indicated immediately in the unstable patient.
    • Initial energy dose is 0.5-1 J/kg.
    • If no response double energy dose to 2 J/kg.
    • Sedation prior to Cardioversion Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety .
