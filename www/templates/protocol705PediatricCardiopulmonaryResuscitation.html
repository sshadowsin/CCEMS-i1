PEDIATRIC ANAPHYLAXIS

NOTE:

Most cardiac arrests in children are the result of respiratory arrests, and therfore, the first priority of care should be the establishment of adequate ventilation.

Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines as needed.

INDICATIONS:

  1. Apnea, unresponsiveness and pulselessness.
  2. Use adult Protocols for children > 50 kg or 100lbs.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess for presence of absence of a pulse.
  3. Determine probable rhythm - ventricular fibrillation or pulseless ventricular tachycardia.
  4. AED is only to be used > 1 year of age. Use a pediatric system, if available, for ages 1 to 8 years old.
  5. Assess blood glucose level.
  6. BEGIN BLS- Follow current American Heart Guidelines.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Quickly determine ECG rhythm. For specific arrhythmia management, see appropriate management Protocols:
    a. Protocol 706: Pediatric Asystole and Pulseless Electrical Activity (PEA).
    b. Protocol 707: Pediatric Ventricular Fibrillation.
    c. Protocol 711: Pediatric Ventricular Tachycardia.
  3. Assess and manage the airway.
  4. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  5. Establish IV/IO access.
  6. Consider underlying causes and treat when possible:
    a. H's - Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Hydrogen ion (Acidosis), Hyper/hypokalemia, Hypothermia, Hypoglycemia.
    b. T's - Toxins (overdose), Tamponade (Cardiac), Tension Pneumothorax, Thrombosis (ACS of Pulmonary), Trauma.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. No nasotracheal intubation.
  5. Continue apprpriate American Heary PALS Algorithms.
