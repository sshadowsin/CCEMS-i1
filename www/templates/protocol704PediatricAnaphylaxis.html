PEDIATRIC ANAPHYLAXIS

INDICATIONS:

  1. Patients with systemic signs/symptoms of allergic reaction: hives, generalized itching or flushing upper airway irritation or blockage, wheezing, hypotension, urticaria, angioedema or other signs of allergic reaction.
  2. Patients with local reaction (itching, redness, swelling) to known allergens.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess ABC's for evidence of apnea, airway reflex compromise or difficulty in ventilatroy effort.
  3. Assess medical conditions, burns or traumatic injuries that have the potential to compromise the airway.
  4. Safely and rapidly eliminate the source of exposure, as able.
  5. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all comrpomised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  6. In general, maintain an oxygen saturation of 90-94%.
  7. Always insure proper care of the C-Spine.
  8. Keep the patient NPO.
  9. Infants and young children are primary nose breathers.
  10. Suction oral and nasal passages as needed to keep clear.
  11. If the patient has any repiratory distress and is conscious, allow them to achieve a position of comfort, including leaving a child in their parent's lap.
  12. Apply cold pack to bite or sting site.
  13. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory dsitress - consider a nasal or oral airway.
  14. Recommended pediatric ventilatory rates:
    a. Infant (0-12 months): 25 breaths per minutes.
    b. 1-3 years: 20 breaths per minutes.
    c. 4-6 years: 15 breaths per minutes.
    d. >6 years: 12 (same as adult).
  15. Do not hyperventialte the patient.
  16. Assist patient with own Epipen Jr. (0.15mg) IM for respiratory distress and/or shock from anaphylaxis - contact OLMC prior to administering.
  17. Assist patient with own Albuterol inhaler according to the prescription on the inhaler- contact OLMC porior to administering.
  18. Remove allergen if present (e.g., bee stinger).

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Establish IV/IO access if able as soon as possible.
  4. IV NS - 20 cc/kg rapid infusion if hypetensive.
  5. Contact OLMC for medication orders:
    a. Epinephrine 1:1000): 0.01 mg/kg to max 0.3 mg per dose IM for patient with more than mild symptoms.
    i. If sumptoms persist, may repeat every 5-10 minutes to max total of 1.2 mg.
    ii. Be sure to landmark appropriately to avoid nerves and blood vessels. Be sure to aspirate prior to injecting to avoid accidentally giving this into a blood vessel. If blood is returned on aspiration then discard the syringe and redraw the EPI into a new syringe with a clean needle.
    b. Benadryl: 1 mg/kg to max of 25 mg for a single dose IV/IO/IM for moderate to severe allergic reaction.
    c. Albuterol : if significant WHEEZING is present: Albuterol 2.5 mg every 10 minutes via nebulization until symptoms subside. Start with 1.25 mg if patient is <1 year in age.
  6. Reassess airway patency, vital signs, and level of consciousness frequently.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is compete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endortacheal tube when indicated.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. No nasotracheal intubation.
  5. Consider sedation after intubation (benzodiazepine).
  6. After 3 unsuccessful attempts at endotracheal intubation use a supraglottic airway device or BVM with appropriate oral/nasal airway.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Surgical Airway - Cricothyrotomy-only when all other methods of oxyengation, ventilation and securing the airway have failed-Insert an appropriately sized endotracheal tube and secure.
  2. Epinephrine (1:10,000) 0.01 mg/kg or 0.1 ml/kg IV/IO for severe hypotenstion.
  3. Sedation after intubation (benzodiazepine).
