Ventricular Fibrillation/Pulseless Ventricular Tachycardia

ALL CARDIAC ARREST VICTIMS SHOULD HAVE:

  1. CPR-Push hard and fast (100 - 120 compressions/minute).
  2. Ensure full chest recoil.
  3. Minimize interruptions in chest compressions.
  4. 1 cycle of CPR: 30 compressions/2 ventilations; 5 cycles=2 minutes.
  5. Avoid hyperventilation, 1 breath every 5-6 seconds (8-10 a minute)
  6. Secure airway and confirm placement, suction as needed
  7. After advanced airway is placed, give continuous chest compressions without pauses.
  8. Check rhythm every 2 minutes
  9. Elevate arm, give fluid bolus when administering a drug during a full arrest
  10. Always search for a reversible cause
  11. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines

EMT

FOLLOW PROTOCOL 101: MEDICAL CARDIAC ARREST- INITIAL CARE.

  1. ABC's - Focused history and physical exam
    1. Assess for presence or absence of a pulse.
    2. Assess blood glucose level.
  2. Key Considerations
    1. Assess for evidence that resuscitation should not be attempted.
  3. If arrest is witnessed
    1. Defibrillate immediately if AED or manual defibrillator if available.
  4. If unwitnessed or defibrillator is not immediately available.
    1. Begin high quality CPR (100-120 compressions per minute with minimal interruptions, 2 minute cycles)
    2. When AED arrives, attach to patient and defibrillate if a shock is advised
  5. Resume CPR immediately after each shock and continue for 2 minutes or until asked to hold by the AED
  6. Check pulse and assess rhythm -shock if advised after each 2 minutes cycle of compressions
    1. Place an NP or OP airway and a BVM with high-flow oxygen during the first 2-3 cycles of CPR/defibrillation. After 2-3 cycles apply asynchronous BVM breaths at a rate of 1 breath every 5-6 seconds
  7. Continue until ALS arrives or patient regains a pulse
  8. Provide supportive care if pulse returns

ADVANCED EMT

FOLLOW PROTOCOL 101: MEDICAL CARDIAC ARREST- INITIAL CARE.

ENSURE ALL OF THE ABOVE IS BEING DONE APPROPRIATELY.

Key Considerations

  1. Assess for evidence that resuscitation should not be attempted.
  2. Pregnancy >20 weeks gestation - Place wedge-shaped cushion or multiple pillows under patient's right hip (L lateral decubitus position)
    1. Transport these patients to Logan Regional Hospital emergency department without delay while attempting to provide continuous compressions and defibrillation, as there is a potential to perform emergency cesarean section.
  3. AED is only to be used > 1 year of age. Use a pediatric system, if available, for ages 1 to 8 years old.
  4. Pediatric lowest acceptable systolic blood pressures: View Table Below
  5. Assume cardiac origins for all adult arrests unless evidence to the contrary. Consider underlying causes and treat when possible.
  6. H's - Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Hydrogen ion (Acidosis), Hyper/hypokalemia, Hypothermia, Hypoglycemia
  7. T's - Toxins (Overdose), Tamponade (Cardiac), Tension Pneumothorax, Thrombosis (ACS or Pulmonary), Trauma

Resume CPR immediately for 5 cycles.

May use ONE antiarrhythmic available, either Amiodarone or Lidocaine

Lidocaine 1mg/kg IV/IO every 3-5 minutes to maximum dose of 3 mg/kg total OR Amiodarone 300 mg IV/IO push and repeat 150 mg Amiodarone in 3-5 minutes if needed. DO NOT MIX ANTIARRHYTHMICS.

Deliver 1 shock (120-200 J).

Resume CPR immediately for 5 cycles.

Check rhythm if VF/VT continue or refer to specific rhythm algorithm

PARAMEDIC

  1. ENSURE ALL OF THE ABOVE IS BEING DONE APPROPRIATELY.
  2. Establish IV/IO access if not done.
  3. Continue shock-CPR-Drug sequence per current ACLS guidelines.
  4. Intubate after first round of CPR-shock-drug, ensure proper tube placement if airway has not been secured with an advanced airway. If an advanced airway has been inserted do not remove unless unable to ventilate.
  5. Magnesium Sulfate diluted in 10 ml NS give over 5 minutes for Torsades de Pointes.
  6. If conversion of VF/VT to a perfusing rhythm consider IV infusion of an antiarrhythmic

MEDICAL CONTROL OPTION- MUST CONTACT MEDICAL CONTROL PRIOR TO ADMINISTRATION.

Sodium Bicarb - ADULT DOSE:

Cardiac Arrest: 1 meq/kg IV/IO

  1. use early if pre-existing metabolic acidosis, hyperkalemia, tricyclic antidepressant OD.
  2. prolonged resuscitation if ventilation is adequate.
  3. return of spontaneous circulation after long period of arrest
