PROLAPSED UMBILICAL CORD

When umbilical cord precedes fetus through birth canal, it is likely to be compressed between fetal head and maternal pelvis leading to severe fetal ischemia.

INDICATIONS:

Any pregnancy patient with protrusion of part of the umilical cord through the cervix.

EMT / ALL

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Obtain full set of vital signs.
  3. Take OBSTETRICAL HISTORY as described in Introduction to Protocol 600: Obstetric Emergencies.
  4. Place mother in TRENDELEBURG OR KNEE-CHEST POSITION.
  5. DO NOT ATTEMPT DELIVERY
  6. Keep cord moistened with sterile saline.
  7. Apply high flow OXYGEN.
  8. Insert two fingers of gloved hand into vagina and lift fetal head off cord; head may be pushed gently back up into birth canal slightly.
  9. Assure effectiveness of maneuver by palpating pulse in cord.
  10. Pulse in cord represents fetal heart rate. Rate less than 120 indicates fetal distress.
  11. Do not remove hand until transfer of care to hospital personnel.

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Initiate IV NS regular drip TKO or saline lock unless hypotensive then 500 ml bolus and repeat as needed.
  3. IMMEDIATE, RAPID TRANSPORT to appropriate facility per Transport Protocol.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. When delivery is imminent, to suppress uterine contractions:
    a. Rapidly infuse 1 liter of NS (AEMT/PM).
    b. Albuterol 2.5 mg via nebulization (AEMT/PM).
    c. Magnesium Sulfate 1 gram IV and titrate per OLMC (PM only).
