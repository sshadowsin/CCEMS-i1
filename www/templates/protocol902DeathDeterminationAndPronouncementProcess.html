Death Determination and Pronouncement Process

  1. Patients found pulseless and apneic must receive cardiopulmonary resuscitation unless they meet criteria for either this Protocol or have proof of an Advanced Directives.

  2. Patients with history, exam or environmental evidence of pre-mortem hypothermia—use Protocol 105 Hypothermic Cardiac Arrest.

  3. If patient is found in Asystole but does not meet DOA criteria, also refer to Protocol 106 Ceasing Resuscitation Efforts.

ALL PROVIDERS

General Crime Scene Management Principles as appropriate.

  1. Treatment Plan - Contact OLMC immediately

  2. CPR should NOT be started in a pulseless, apneic patient in the presence of:

    1. Obvious death, decomposition, and/or rigor mortis.
    2. Obvious fatal wounds without signs of life
    3. A verbal order pronouncing the patient dead by a licensed physician in the State of Utah who is present on scene.
    4. An order by the OLMC physician.
    5. A Do Not Resuscitate (DNR) written order, bracelet, or necklace from any U.S. State.

    6. A signed Physician Order for Life-Sustaining Treatment (POLST form) from any U.S. State indicating that the patient does not desire resuscitative efforts.

  3. Termination of CPR may be done in the following circumstances with OLMC approval:

    1. A valid DNR or POLST form is discovered after resuscitative efforts were initiated.
    2. Resuscitation efforts initiated when criteria to not resuscitate were present.
    3. A verbal order pronouncing the patient dead by a licensed physician in the State of Utah who is present on scene per State EMS Rule 426-4-600

    4. Order by the OLMC physician.

  4. Following determination of obvious death or termination of resuscitative efforts:

    1. Call dispatch to reduce any responding transport(s) to Non-Emergent.
    2. Complete a full patient care report with particular emphasis to document last time seen alive, scene conditions, any interventions done by EMS, Law Enforcement or bystanders, any suspicious items or circumstances noticed. .
    3. Turn the body over to the appropriate law enforcement agency.
    4. Evaluate for, document, and report any indication of non-accidental trauma to law enforcement
    5. Record an EKG strip showing Asystole in 2 different Leads and attach to the PCR.
    6. Assist if needed in disposition of the patient to the Coroner, mortuary or as indicated by OLMC or Law Enforcement
    7. There will always be patients and circumstances that deserve special consideration (pediatric drowning or pregnant patients, for instance).
    8. OLMC should be consulted if there are ever any questions.
    9. Always be sensitive to the patient’s desires, family concerns, and on-scene environment to insure an understanding by all who observe your actions that everything that should have been done to resuscitate the patient was done.
