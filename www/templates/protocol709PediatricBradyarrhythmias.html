PEDIATRIC BRADYARRYTHMIAS

NOTE:

  1. For all arrhythmias begin with the following Protocol 708: Pediatric Cardiac Arrythmias .
  2. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines as needed.

INDICATIONS:

  1. Assess for signs or symptoms of hypo-perfusion:
    a. Heart rate <60.
    b. Capillary refill > 2 seconds.
    c. Absent or weak peripheral pulses.
    d. Cool, cold, cyanotic extermities.
    e. Poor muscle tone.
    f. Altered level of consciousness (lethargy or failure to recognize parents).
    g. Hypotension

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess for signs of poor perfusion, hypotension altered mental staus, signs of shock, cheast pain, or accute heart failure.
  3. Obtain a blood glucose level.
  4. Maintain airway; assist with breathing as necessary, provide oxygen.
  5. Aggressive oxygenation with high flow oxygen and assisted ventilation with a BVM as indicated.
  6. Begin chest compressions if persistent heart rate <60/min and signs of poor perfusion following affressive oxygenation and ventilation.
  7. Ensure patient is warm.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Perform a 12 ECG and transmit to receiving hospital if possible.
  3. Only treat bradycardia if the patient is symptomatic (hypotension, altered mental status, chest pain).
  4. Attempt to identify and treat the underlying cause of bradycardia:
    a. H's - Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Hydrogen ion (Acidosis), Hyper/hypokalemia, Hypothermia, Hypoglycemia.
    b. T's - Toxins (overdose), Tamponade (Cardiac), Tension Pneumothorax, Thrombosis (ACS or Pulmonary), Trauma.
  5. Establish vascular assess if able.
  6. Initiate transport.
  7. IV NS @ TKO rate-if hypotensive 20 ml/kg may repeate as needed every 5 minutes up to 60 ml/kg.
  8. Contact Medical Control for Medication Order:
    a. Epinephrine: 0.01 mg/kg = 0.1 ml/kg (0.1 mL/kg of 1:10,000) IV/IO
    b. Repeat every 3-5 minutes until pulse or BP improved.
  9. Atropine 0.02 mg/kg IV/IO
    a. Minimum single dose of 0.1 mg.
    b. Maximum single does of 0.5 mg.
    c. Repeate Atropine every 3-5 minutes until pulse or BP improved or until Max. 1 mg for child and 3 mg for adolescents.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated or appropriate supraglottic airway.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. No nasotracheal intubation.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. IF BRADYCARDIA IS EVERE with SIGNS OF POOR PERFUSION:
    a. Transcutaneous pacing (TCP) at an initial rate of 100 beats per minute if the patient does not respond to medications.

  2. Consider Sedation for TCP-post intubation: Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety.
