PEDIATRIC SUCKING CHEST WOUND

INDICATIONS:

All patients who present with chest wounds that is open and leaking.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Consider spinal motion restrictions,
  3. Apply direct pressure to any obvious external hemorrhage.
  4. Cover open chest wounds with occlusive dressing - SEAL wound with Vaseline gauze, plastic wrap, adhesive tape on three sides, preferably at the end-expiration if child can cooperate. DO NOT MAKE AN AIRTIGHT SEAL AS THIS CAN CAUSE A TENSION PNEUMOTHORAX.
  5. Immobilize any ovbious injuries and penetrating object, do not remove penetrating objects.
  6. Impaled objects should be stabilized in place and covered with dry sterile dressings. The exceptions would be:
    • Objects through the cheek where there is the possibilty of airway compromise.
    • Objects that would interfere with chest compressions.
    • Sharp objects may need to be removed if their presence is causing ongoing injury, compromise, or inhibiting CPR.
  7. Tourniquets should be used to control hemorrhage not controlled with direct pressure and consider Protocol 512: EMS Tourniquet .
  8. Maintain warmth to minimize heat loss.
  9. Monitor for shock and hypovolemia.
  10. Assess ABC's for evidence of apnea, airway reflex compromise or difficulty in ventilatory effort.
  11. OXYGEN - high flow (15L/min) by nonrebreather mask or positive pressure ventilation (BVM) when required.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Establish vascular access x2 if possible.
  4. If hypotensive bolus with NS 20 ml/kg then reassess - may give up to 60 ml/kg if not hypotensive TKO rate with a MAX bolus of 1 L.
  5. Treat for pain and anxiety, consider Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety . and contact OLMC for medication order:
    • Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg (max 25 mcg per dose) IV.IO.
    • In the event an IV cannot be establushed, Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg may be given IM up an initial dose of 25 mcg.
    • For additional doses, contact OLMC.
  6. Monitor for tension pneumothorax. Decreasing blood pressure associated with worsening dyspnea, unilateral decreased breath sounds, increasing resistance to ventilation.
  7. If signs of tension pneumothorax develop, LIFT OCCLUSIVE DRESSING to allow air to escape.
  8. Monitor for shock and hypovolmeia.
  9. Assess mental status prior to and every 15 minutes during transport (GCS/AVPU).
  10. Contact Medical Control.

PARAMEDIC:

  1. Ensure the above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION- Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophogeal Intubation Detector.
  4. No nasotracheal intubation.

Medical Control Option-Must contact prior to administration.

  1. Suspected Tension Pneumothorax - Immediate needle decompression using appropriate technique.
  2. Traumatic Arrest- Consider bilateral needle decompression for suspected tension pneumothorax based on mechanism of injury using appropriate technique.
