Nitroglycerin

NTG, Nitro
Approved for Advanced CCEMS Units Only

Protocol 1019

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 300, 305, 402.

Nitrate/Coronary Vasodilator

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Rapid Smooth Muscle Relaxant.
2. Dilates Coronary Arteries - Increases Coronary Blood Flow.
3. Improves Blood Flow to Myocardium.
4. Promotes Pooling of Blood in Systemic Circulation Which Decreases the Workload on the Heart. This is Useful in Treating CHF.

INDICATIONS

1. Chest Pain Associated With Angina.
2. Chest Pain Associated With Acute Coronary Syndrome.
3. Acute Pulmonary Edema (Unless Associated With Hypotension).

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Hypotension (Systolic BP <110mmHg on Initial Reading).
2. Shock
3. 12 Lead ECG Shows Acute Inferior MI.
4. Head Trauma.
5. CVA.
6. Use of Erectile Dysfunction Medication Within 24 Hours (Viagra).
7. If Systolic Pressure <110 mmHg at Any Time During Evaluation/Administration - Stop and Give a 250 mL Bolus and Reassess.

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Hypotension - Tachycardia.
2. Headache.
3. Nausea/Vomiting.
4. Orthostasis (Assist Patient When Standing/Sitting.

Considerations
1. Take Vital Signs Before and After Each Dose. Hold if Systolic BP <110 mmHg.
2. Expect Vomiting if Patient has Ingested ETOH.
3. May Moisten Mouth if Using Tablets.
4. Look or Ask if Patient Using a NTG Skin Patch (Remove if so).
5. Light Sensitive - Replace Frequently.
6. Assist When Changing Position - May Pass Out Due to Vasodilation.
7. Contact Medical Control if Blood Pressure Drops.

Dosage and Administration

Adult Dose
Establish IV Access Prior to Administration

TABLET 1. Place One 0.4mg Tablet Under Tongue and Allow to Dissolve.
2. May Repeat Every 5 Minutes x3 Doses.
3. Monitor Vital Signs Before Each Dose.

SPRAY 1. Have Patient Hold Breath and Spray Into Mouth. Does Not Have to be Given Under the Tongue.
2. May Repeat Every 5 Minutes x3 Doses.
3. Monitor Vital Signs Before Each Dose.

PEDS DOSE: NOT A PEDIATRIC DRUG
