Atrovent

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1006

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 401

Class: Anticholinergic, Bronchodilator

MECHANISM OF ACTION
1. Bronchodilation
2. Onset of 5-15 minutes
3. Duration 4-6 hours

INDICATIONS
1. Treatment for persistent bronchospasms

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. HYPERSENSITIVITY TO IPRATROPIUM, ATROPINE, ALKALOID, SOYBEAN PROTEIN, PEANUTS.

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Nausea and Vomiting
2. Coughing
3. Headache
4. Tachycardia
5. Dry Mouth
6. Can precipitate acute bronchospasms (uncommon with nebulized administration)

PRECUATIONS
1. Should be used with caution in narrow angle glaucoma, prostatic hypertrophy or bladder neck obstruction.

Dosage and Administration

1. Adults: 0.5 mg/3mL NS via small volume nebulizer
2. ADULTS: May mix with 2.5mg Albuterol if meets protocol for a duo-nebulizer treatment
3. ATROVENT IS NOT A PEDIATRIC MEDICATION

CONSIDERATIONS
1. Shake well before use.
2. Rare cases of acute anaphylactoid reactions have occurred.
3. If mixed with albuterol, it must be used within one hour.
4. Best to avoid use of mask when administered to avoid eye exposure.
