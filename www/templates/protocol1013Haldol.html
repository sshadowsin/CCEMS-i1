Haldol

Approved for Rescue Paramedics Only

Protocol 1013

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 803.

Class: Hormone - Anti-psychotic

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Depresses cerebral cortex.
2. Aids in reducing aggression.

INDICATIONS
1. Combative-psychotic patient who will not de-escalate with voice commands or reduction in external stimuli.
2. When a chemical restraint is needed for protection of the patient or EMS.

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Hypersensitivity.
2. Alcohol or sedative drug usage is detected.

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Seizures.
2. Hypotension.
3. Sudden cardiac arrest - more common in elderly or use of psychotropic medications.
4. Hypotension.
5. Laryngospasm-respiratory depression.

Dosage and Administration

Contact Medical Control Prior to Administration

Adult: Haloperidol 5-10 mg IM or 2-5mg IV

Pediatric: Haloperidol
1. 6-12 years old: 1-3mg dose IM.
2. 12 years and older: 5-10 mg IM or 2-5mg IV/IO.
3. <6 years old - NOT RECOMMENDED
