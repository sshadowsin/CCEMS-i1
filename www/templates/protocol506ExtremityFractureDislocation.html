EXTREMITY FRACTURE/DISLOCATION

INDICATIONS:

Suspected fractures or dislocation in extremity

EMT

  1. PERFORM PRIMARY AND SECONDARY SURVEY, including neurological and vascular exam of extremity distal to injury.
  2. Uncomplicated fractures/dislocations with adequate circulation;
    a. Splinted in a position of function/comfort.
  3. Fractures/dislocations with circulation deficits or severely angulated:
    a. Treat with one attempt at placing the extremity in a position of function/comfort.
    • i: If unsuccessful, splint in position found and expedite transport.
    • ii. Fractures and joint dislocations without palpable distal pulses are true orthopedic emergencies.
  4. Potential pelvic fractures_:
    a. Treatment of choice is application of the pelvic binder. If unavailable, a cloth sheet or blanket can be wrapped tightly around the pelvis to stabilize it.
    b. Pelvic stabilization shall be done only once as multiple attempts can cause further injury.
  5. Isolated proximal femur (hip) fractures (especially in the elderly)
    a. Best managed with anatomical splinting utilizing a scop stretcher. Traction splints are not appropriate for any proximal femur fracture.
  6. Femoral shaft fractures:
    a. Immobilized utilizing a traction splint unless one of the situations listed below is present:
    • i. Injuries just proximal to or involving the knee joint.
    • ii. Injuriy to the pelvis.
    • iii. Partial amputation.
    • iv. Lower leg or ankel injuries.
    • v. If used would delay transport of the patient with a life-threatening condition.
  7. CHECK DISTAL CIRCULATION (color, temperature, capillary refill and pulses) BEFORE AND AFTER SPLINTING.
  8. Remove all rings and constricting clothing on the affected limb if possible.

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETC02, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. If patient stable, ANALGESIA PRIOR TO SPLINTING or MANIPULATION of the injured area.
  4. IV NS large bore TKO or NS lock. If femur fracture, multiple fractures, or if needed to administer analgesia, Titrate rate to maintain vital signs within normal limits.
  5. If vascular compromise exists distal to injury, Control Physician may instruct on application of traction and reduction (straightening) of injured limb to improve perfusion.
ADVANCED EMT/PARAMEDIC
