Administration of Prescribed Metered-Dose Inhaler or Small Volume Nebulizer

This protocol is for the EMT First Responder only.

Determine Need for Epinephrine:

  1. Refer Protocol 310: ALLERGIC REACTION / ANAPHYLAXIS
  2. Patient has an allergy history
  3. Patient exhibits signs and symptoms of severe allergic reaction
  4. Patient is in respiratory distress

Inquire About Previous Doses:

  1. Total doses taken/administered
  2. When last dose taken/administered
  3. Effects of previous dose
  4. Any other interventions

Inquire About Medication:

  1. Obtain auto injector (verify single or dual dose injector)
  2. Ensure the “six rights” of medication administration
  3. Right patient – prescribed to this patient
  4. Right medication – for patient condition
  5. Right dose - Adult: 0.3 mg, Infant and Child: 0.15 mg
  6. Right route – Intramuscular (IM)
  7. Right time - Check expiration date Check if medication is discolored (should be clear!)

Obtain Medical Direction:

  1. Obtain order from online medical control prior to administering Epinephrine Auto-Injector

Prepare to Administer:

  1. If possible uncover patient's leg. try and avoid injecting thru clothing
  2. Support patient’s knee with other hand to prevent movement
  3. Remove safety cap from the auto-injector
  4. Place tip of auto-injector against patient’s thigh (laterally, midway between the knee and the hip)
  5. Push injector firmly against the thigh until the injector activates
  6. Hold injector in place for 10 seconds
  7. Dispose of used auto-injector in biohazard sharps container (retain dual dose injector follow manufacturer’s instructions for second dose)

Assess respiratory status and patient’s response
