HEAD TRAUMA

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. The goals of this Protocol are:
    • To maintain the airway and circulatory status of the head-injured patient and protect the spine.
    • To record an initial neurological examination.
    • To institute measures that may reduce intracranial pressure.
    • To rapidly transport patients who may require neurosurgical intervention.
    • TBI (Traumatic Brain Injury) may be painful; however, pain medications can cloud serial neurological assessments. Routine pain medications should not be administered to a paitent with altered mednatl status after TBI.
    • Paitents with TBI may be confused or combative. Consider restraints if needed to protect everyone.
    • Loss of memory, prolonged confusion or altered mental status associated with trauma may indicate a significant head injury.

INDICATIONS:

  1. All patients with head injuries who:
    • Unresponsive to verbal stimuli or major alteration in mental status.
      b. Have a Glasgow coma score (GCS) of 13 or less or deteriorate more than 1 point on the GCS.
      c. Exhibit unilateral or bilateral flexor response (decorticate posturing) or extensor response (decerebrate posturing) to pain.
      d. Develop unequal pupils with a decreased level of consciousness.
      e. Exhibit focal neurological deficits.

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Systolic blood pressure less than 100 or other signs of poor perfusion or significant bleeding, of if any evidence of major non-CNS unjury, use Protocol 501: Major or Multisystem Trauma.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical examination.
  2. AIRWAY: Assure airway patency and adequate ventilation. OXYGEN 15 L/min via NRB. Suction must be readily available.
  3. IMMOBILIZE C-Spine with rigid collar, head immobilization device and backboard. Consider elevating head of spinal board or stretcher 30 degrees.
  4. Document a GCS for the patient.
  5. Open skill fractures should be covered with non-pressure dry sterile dressings.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Do not hyperventilate excessively. If patient has an obvious unilateral pupillary dilation, increase respiratory rate by 10% above normal target respiratory rate (RR) until the patient improves Hyperventilation below a ETC02 of 30 mmHg is discouraged.
  3. BEGIN TRANSPORT and monitor the level of consciousness.
  4. Ventilate to Maintain EtC02 between 35-40 mmHg when capnography is available.
  5. IV NS TKO or saline lock--unless patient develops hypotension or other sign of shock, then run 500cc bolus and contact OLMC.
  6. Check glucose
  7. Document SERIAL VITAL SIGNS, GCS, and NEURO EXAMS.
  8. Continuous ECG, ETC02, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION immedieately if unable to maintain airway patency or adequate ventilation (Use Endotracheal Intubation procedure Protocol.)
  3. Give LIDOCAINE 1 mg/kg IV push 60-90 seconds prior to intubation (if IV access readily available).
  4. Maintain in-line stabilization of cervical spine.
