PRETERM LABOR

INDICATIONS:

  1. Gestation less than 36 weeks.
  2. Sustained contractions (at least every 5 minutes).

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Prolapsed cord--see Protocol 610: Prolapsed Umbilical Cord.
  2. Vaginal hemorrhage--see Protocol 607: Vaginal Hemorrhage.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Obtain current vital signs.
  3. Obtain baseline OBSTETRICAL HISTORY as described in Introduction to Protocol 600: Obstetric Emergencies.
  4. VISUALLY EXAMINE PERIMEUM for prolapsed cord, crowning, mal-presentation, hemorrhage.
  5. If more than 20 weeks gestation, place in left lateral recumbent position.
  6. If delivery occurs provide neonatal care per Protocol 602: Full Term Imminent Delivery/Newborn Resuscitation.
  7. Protect against hypothermia, respiratory depression and hypoglycemia.
  8. Administer Oxygen 4-6L NC or 15L NRM to keep sat. >94%.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Initiate IV NS TKO or saline lock unless hypotensive then 500 ml bolus, repeat as needed.
  4. OLMC and TRANSPORT to appropriate facility.
  5. If delivery occures, provide neonatal care per Protocol 602: Full Term Imminent Delivery/Newborn Resuscitation.
  6. Protect against hypothermia, respiratory depression and hypoglycemia.
  7. Analgesia option: Pain Control Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. If seizing: Versed - Protocol 308: Seizure.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. High-risk preterm labor when delivery is imminent, to suppress uterine contractions:
    a. Rapidly infuse 1 liter of NS (AEMT/PM).
    b. Albuterol 2.5 mg via nebulization (AEMT/PM).
    c. Magnesium Sulfate 1 gram IV and titrate per Medical Control ( PM only)

  2. For females with advanced pregnancy and seizures:
    a. Magnesium Sulfate - 2-4 grams IM or 2-4 grams in 10 ml NS infuse over 15 to 30 minutes IV as directed by OLMC.
    b. PEDIATRIC (<15 years of age, PM only):
    c. Magnesium Sulfate - For pediatric patients who are pregnant and having a seizure contact OLMC for dosage instructions.
