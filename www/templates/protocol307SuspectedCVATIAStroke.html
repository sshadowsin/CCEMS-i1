SUSPECTED CVA - TIA (STROKE)

Stroke is the 3rd leading cause of death and a leading cause of disability. There are 2 Stroke Receiving Facilites within Cache County. Rapid recognition and transport is the key to management. Scene time should be kept to a minimum and interventions done en-route if possible. Alert the receiving emergency department that you are transporting a suspected stroke patient as soon as you have made a destination decision.

  1. If Blood glucose is >60 do not administer Dextrose D50. As it may worsen an underlying neurological problem.
  2. Do not administer Aspirin.
  3. Do NOT treat hypertension in the field unless directed by On-line Medical Control. (the pressure may be needed to ensure cerebral perfusion secondary to increased ICP.
  4. Children can have strokes as well as adults. Some rish factors include; sickle cell disease, congenital and acquired heart disease, head and neck infections, systemic conditions, (e.g. inflammatory bowel disease and autoimmune disorders), head trauma or dehydration.
  5. Avoid lengthy scene times-interventions should be done enroute.

EMS Assessment and Management Guidelines

Prompt stroke recognition and treatment by EMS is a critical component of acute stroke care. As an integral part of the Utah Stroke System, EMS will use a standardized prehospital treatmet protocol for suspected stroke patients.

On Scene:

  1. Perform prehospital stroke assessment using the Cincinnati Stroke Scale.
    a. Facial Droop: (have patient smile
    i. Normal: both sides of face move equally.
    ii. Abnormal     : one side of face does not move as well.
    b. Arm Drift: (Have patient hold arms out for 10 seconds)
    i. Normal: both arms move equally or not at all.
    ii. Abnormal: One arm drigts compared to the other, or does not move at all.
    c. Speech: (Have patient say) - " You can't teach an old dog new tricks"
    i. Normal: Paitent uses correct words with no slurring.
    ii. Abnormal: Slurred or inappropriate words, or mute.

EMT

Focused history and physical exam.

  1. Blood glucose, temperature and oxygen saturation assessment
  2. Keep NPO.
  3. Document symptom onset time or time last seen normal.
  4. Asseess LOC and maintain airway as indicated.
  5. Place pt. in left lateral recumbent position to prevent aspiration if unconscious (spinal immobilization precautions should be considered if neccessary).
  6. Suction as needed.
  7. Assist ventilations if necessary.
  8. If oxygen is needed use NC if possible to keep sat >94%.
  9. Anticipate vomiting and ensure airway is monitored and protected at all times.
  10. Obtain Baseline vitals.
  11. Obtain and bag all patient medicatins in preparation for transport.
  12. Determine last PO intake.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is completed.
  2. Continuous ECG, blood pressure ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. 12 Lead EKG, if available.
  4. Obtain sample history and assess for associated signs/symptoms-establish and record the exact time when patient was "Last Known Normal".
  5. Obtain blood glucose if not already obtained - if below 60 mg/dl them administer 25 gm Dextrose D50 and reassess. If no IV access after TWO attempts consider Glucagon 1 mg IM.
  6. Perform baseline neurological exam including the Cincinnati stroke scale.
  7. Keep head elevated to 45 degrees.
  8. Begin rapid transport if unresponsive or shows a positive sign(s) of a stroke.
  9. Thrombolytic can be administered only 4 hours from time of symptom onset-notify receiving hospital that a suspected stroke paitent is enroute ASAP with an ETA.
  10. Start IV with a #18g catheter if possible to facilitate the pressure of the CT scan start NS @ TKO if possible or a saline lock.
  11. If hypotensive (systolic <100) then may give a 250 ml bolus and may repeat x 1 if needed.
  12. Bring all patients medicatsion to the receiving facility if possible or as complete a list as able including dose and frequency if able,
  13. Ensure oxygen sat >94%, use appropriate device.
  14. Insert airway adjunct (NPA/OPA) as needed to maintain airway if unconscious or lacks a ga reflex.
  15. Transport to a Stroke Receiving Hospital.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure all above is completed.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Start IV with NS TKO or saline lock unless hypotensive (systolic <100) then give 250 ml bolus may repeat x 1.
  4. Obtain a 12 kead EKG if availbale.
  5. CHECK GLUCOMETER READING - IF BELOW 60 mg/dl then administer 25 gm of Dextrose D50. IV PUSH. If no IV access after 2 attempts, consider Glucagon 1 mg IM.
  6. CHECK FOR Narcotic Ingestion (respiratory depression, history of narcotic usage, constricted pupils, drug vials or paraphernalia near patient), If any of these present, give Naloxone 1-2 mg (oper dose) IV/IM/IO/ or intranansal (atomizer). May repeat x 1
  7. Consider physical restraints as needed to protect the patient and/or personnel.
  8. Contact OLMC: Consider Protocol 803: Restraints Physical - Violent Patient/Chemical Sedation.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure all above is completed.
  2. Establish 2nd IV access if able, ensure at least one is with an #18 ga. catheter (needed to administer CT contrast)
  3. Intubate if patient is unresponsive without a gag reflex.
  4. Transport to a Stroke Receiving Hospital.
