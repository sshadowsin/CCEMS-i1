FULL-TERM NON-IMMINENT DELIVERY/SEIZURE

INDICATIONS:

  1. Advanced pregnancy (Gestation of more than 36 weelks)
  2. Contractions more than 3 minutes apart.
  3. Absence of crowning or strong urge to push.

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Prolapsed cord--see Protocol 610: Prolapsed Umbilical Cord.
  2. Vaginal hemorrhage--see Protocol 607: Vaginal Hemorrhage.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Obtain baseline OBSTETRICAL HISTORY as described in Introduction to Protocol 600: Obstetric Emergencies.
  3. If patient feels abnormal sensation or foreign body is perineal area, contractions occure more frequently than every 3 minutes, or vaginal bleeding or ruptures of membranes occures, then with precautions to protect patient privacy, VISUALLY EXAMINE PERINEUM for prolapsed cord, crowning, mal-presentation, hemorrhage (crowning can only be determined during contraction).
  4. Apply OXYGEN 4-6 L NC or 15L NRM to ensure O2 Sat >94%.

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Attempt to maintain a sanitary enviornment.
  4. TRANSPORT in position of comfort. It patient prefers to lie down or if evidence of supine hypotensive syndrome, place in left lateral recumbent position or elevate right side.
  5. Initiate IV NS KVO - bolus as needed.
  6. Contact OLMC
  7. Seizures: Follow Protocol 308: Seizure

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. High-risk preterm labor when delivery is imminent, to suppress uterine contractions:
    a. Rapidly infuse 1 liter of NS (AEMT/PM).
    b. Albuterol 2.5 mg via nebulization (AEMT/PM).
    c. Magnesium Sulfate 1 gram IV and titrate per Medical Control ( PM only)

  2. For females with advanced pregnancy and seizures:
    a. Magnesium Sulfate - 2-4 grams IM or 2-4 grams in 10 ml NS infuse over 15 to 30 minutes IV as directed by OLMC.
    b. PEDIATRIC (<15 years of age, PM only):
    c. Magnesium Sulfate - For pediatric patients who are pregnant and having a seizure contact OLMC for dosage instructions.
