Morphine

Approved for Rescue/Ambulance use only

Protocol 1017

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 305, 402, 509.

Narcotic Analgesic

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. CNS Depressant Causing Sedation and Analgesia.
2. Causes Mild-Moderate Vasodilation.
3. Decreases Myocardial Oxygen Demand.

INDICATIONS
1. Relief of Pain Associated with ACS (Follows Nitroglycerin if Vital Signs are Stable).
2. Acute Pulmonary Edema Associate with CHF.
3. Isolated Orthopedic Injury without Head Injury.
4. Thermal Injury if no Hypotension.

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Allergy to any Narcotic.
2. Abdominal Injury.
3. Head Injury or any Signs of Increased Intracranial Pressure.
4. Asthma or COPD.
5. Altered LOC.
6. Ingestion of any Depressant (ETOH, Narcotic, Antidepressant).
7. Respiratory Depression (Rate <12 and/or Saturation <90%).
8. Pulse <60 and/or Systolic BP < 100.

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Hypotension - Tachycardia.
2. Respiratory Arrest/Depression.
3. Nausea/Vomiting.

Considerations
1. May be Given a Higher Dose SQ with Medical Control Approval if Unable to Establish IV Access.
2. If Hypotension Occurs - Bolus with 250-500 mL of NS. Repeat if Necessary.
3. If Hypoventilation Occurs - Give Oxygen and Administer 0.4mg of Naloxone IV and Titrate Until Respirations Improve.

Dosage and Administration

Adult Dose

1. Hypertensive Crisis: 2mg IV Every 5-10 Minutes to Max Dose 10mg.
2. Acute Pulmonary Edema: 2-5mg IV May Repeat x1 to Max Dose 10mg.
3. Pain Control (Medical Control Option): 2-4mg IV Every 10 Minutes to Max Dose 10mg.

Peds Dose

0.1mg / kg Up to Max Dose of 4mg.
