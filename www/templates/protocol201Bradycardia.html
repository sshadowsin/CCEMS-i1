Bradycardia

INDICATIONS:

  1. Any rhythm disorder with a heart rate <60 beats per minute (heart block or sinus bradycardia) or a rhythm that produces signs/symptoms that are due to the slow heart rate.
  2. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines as needed.

SIGNS MAY INCLUDE:

  1. Chest discomfort or pain, dyspnea, altered LOC, weakness / fatigue, hypotension, dizziness or syncope

SYMPTOMS MAY INCLUDE:

  1. Orthostatic hypotension, pulmonary congestion, frequent PVCs or VT

RHYTHMS MAY INCLUDE:

  1. Sinus Bradycardia
  2. 1st degree AV block
  3. 2nd degree AV block
    1. TYPE 1 ( Wenckebach / Mobitz I )
    2. TYPE 2 ( Mobitz II )
  4. 3rd degree AV block

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Head Injury or suspected CNS event with hypertension
  2. Hypothermia
  3. Cardiac Arrest

Treatment Plan

  1. Only treat bradycardia if the patient is symptomatic (hypotension, altered mental status, chest pain, etc.)
  2. Attempt to identify and treat the underlying causes of bradycardia:
  3. H's - Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Hydrogen ion (Acidosis), Hyper/Hypokalemia, Hypothermia, Hypoglycemia
  4. T's - Toxins (Overdose), Tamponade (Cardiac), Tension Pneumothorax, Thrombosis (ACS or Pulmonary), Trauma
  5. In pregnant patients of >20 weeks gestation: Place wedge-shaped cushion or multiple pillows under patient's right hip in order to transport in left lateral decubitus position.

EMT

  1. Perform focused history and assessment to determine symptomatic bradycardia
  2. Maintain airway, assist breathing as needed
  3. Administer oxygen, monitor oximetry, obtain full set of vitals
  4. Obtain a blood glucose level
  5. Apply AED
  6. Treat for shock if indicated

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is done
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available
  3. Obtain 12 lead EKG and transmit to On Line Medical Control
  4. Establish large bore IV-250 ml bolus if hypotensive
  5. Apply Quick-combo patches (anticipate eventual pacing)
  6. Contact OLMC for medication order: Atropine 0.5 mg IV/IO Repeat as needed every 3-5 minutes until pulse or BP improved. Maximum total dose of 3mg
  7. If possible, establish 2nd IV if it won't delay transport
  8. If patient has adequate perfusion, observe and monitor
  9. If unconscious and unstable vital signs are noted, may use IO if unable to establish peripheral IV after 2 failed attempts (follow IO protocol)
  10. Ensure ALS backup if not dispatched

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is done
  2. If Atropine ineffective, prepare for Transcutaneous Pacing
  3. If pulseless develops, search for and treat possible contributing factors (H's and T's of PEA)

NOTE:

TCP (Transcutaneous Pacing) should be started immediately for symptomatic patients who are unstable; particularly those with a high-degree block (Mobitz type 2, 2nd degree or 3rd degree AV block) Conscious patients may require analgesia for discomfort.

DO NOT assess carotid pulse to confirm mechanical capture because muscular jerking may mimic the carotid pulse. Check for FEMORAL pulse.

Indications for TCP are as follows:

  1. Hemodynamically unstable bradycardia
  2. Unstable clinical condition that is likely due to the bradycardia
  3. Bradycardia with symptomatic ventricular escape rhythms
  4. Readiness in the setting of an AMI:
    1. Symptomatic Sinus Bradycardia
    2. Mobitz type 2
    3. 3rd degree block
    4. New left, right bundle branch block (patient must be symptomatic)

Contraindications for TCA are as follows:

  1. Severe hypothermia
  2. Asystole

Start TCP immediately if:

  1. There is no response to Atropine
  2. The patient is severely symptomatic
  3. Atropine is unlikely to be effective or if IV access cannot be quickly established

Perform TCP by following these steps:

  1. Place pacing electrodes on chest (Anterior/Posterior (preferred if no delay in application or Anterior/Lateral)
  2. Turn Pacer ON
  3. Set demand to between 60-80 BPM. Adjust rate to clinical response when pacing is established
  4. Set current milliamperes (mA) output to 2mA above the dose at which consistent capture is observed (safety margin)
