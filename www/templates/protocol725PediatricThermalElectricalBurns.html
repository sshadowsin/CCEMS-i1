PEDIATRIC THERMAL-ELECTRICAL BURNS

INDICATIONS:

  1. Patients with suspected inhalation injuries.
  2. Patients with partial thickness of thermal burns of more than 10% of body surface area.
  3. Patients will full thickness burns of more than 2% body surface area.
  4. Burns to the face, hands, feet and perineum.
  5. Electrical Burns are frequently more serious then they appear.

ALL PROVIDERS:

  1. Scene and patient management.
  2. Focused history and physical exam.
  3. Assess ABC's for evidence of apnea, airway reflex comrpomise, or difficulty in ventilatory effort.
  4. Assess medical conditions, burns, or traumatic injuries that have the potential to compromise the airway.
  5. Full set of Vital Signs including blood glucose.
  6. Thermal Burns:
    • Stop the burning process.
    • Do not pull material out of the wound but cut clothing around it.
    • Remove all burning or smoldering clothing including jewelry.
    • Safely evacuate patient from electrical source.
    • Do not touch the patient until you are sure that the electrical source is disconnected.
  7. When multiple patients are struck simultaneously by lightning or a high voltage source, those in respiratory and/or cardiac arrest should be given the highest priority of care, even those who appear dead on initial evaluation.
  8. Identifying the source as AC or DC voltage with the amperage will be helpful in the treatment.
  9. Burn patients are TRAUMA patients. Care for traumatic injuries should precede care for the burn.
  10. Potential CO and/or Cyanide exposure (closed space smoke exposure) should receive 100% oxygen by NRB.
  11. Prevent hypothermia due to heat loss from the burns.
  12. Consider Child Abuse as a cause. Circumferential scald burn to hands, feet, buttocks, and genitalia are common burns seen in child abuse (especially in children <5 years old).
  13. Definitions:
    • Superficial (1st Degree) burns - red, painful, without blisters.
    • Partial Thickness(2nd Degree) burns - Red painful/hypersensitive, swollen, with either intact or ruptured blisters.
    • Full Thickness (3rd Degree) burns - dark, leathery, painless, waxy, and does not blanch.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Full set of vital signs including blood glucose.
  3. Identify potential entry and exit wounds for electrical burns - both sites will generally be a full thickness burn site.
  4. Ensure SAFETY at the scene.
  5. Monitor for developing airway compromise.
  6. Initiate early oxygen therapy with high flow O2, this is critical despite level of respiratory distress, maintain SPO2 > 94%.
  7. In the unconscious patient,implement spinal motion restriction.
  8. With electrical burns anticipate heart rhythm irregularities (both ventricular and atrial).
  9. Assess for circulatory compromise from circumferential extremity burns or ventilatory compromise from circumferential chest burns.
  10. Remove items that may constrict swelling tissue.
  11. Estimate size and depth of burn using the percentage chart (above).
  12. Dressings:
    a. Partial or Full Thickness (2nd and 3rd degree) <10% BSA- Wet sterile dressings.
    b. Partial or Full Thickness (2nd or 3rd degree) >10% BSA - Dry sterile dressings.
  13. Closely monitor patients temperature and prevent hypothermia.
  14. ASSESS for other injuries.
  15. IMMOBILIZE if indicated.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Avoid placing monitor attachments over burned skin if possible.
  4. Pediatric lowest acceptable systolic blood pressures are in a table below.
  5. 12 Lead ECG where appropriate and if possible, avoid placing electrodes over burned skin.
  6. Establish vascular access x 2 if possible.
  7. Treat for pain and anxiety, consider Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety and contact OLMC for medication order:
    • Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg (max 25 mcg per dose) IV/IO
    • In the event an IV cannot be established, Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg may be given IM up an initial dose of 25 mcg.
    • For additional doses, contact OLMC.
  8. If hypotensive bolus with NS 20 ml/kg then reassess-may give up to 60 ml/kg ig not hypotensive TKO rate with a MAX bolus of 1 L.
  9. RECORD data accuretely with attention to:
    • Time of burn.
    • Type of environment.
    • Time IV fluids begun.
    • Amount of fluids given.
  10. Initiate rapid TRANSPORT.
  11. Monitor for shock and hypovolemia.
  12. Assess mental staus prior to and every 15 minutes during transport (GCS/AVPU).
  13. Contact Medical Control.
AGE PRESSURE

2 months to 1 Year 70 mm Hg
1 Year 70 + 2 x (years in age)
Over 10 Years 90 mm Hg

PARAMEDIC:

  1. Ensure the above is complete.
  2. Treat for Pain control consider Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety and contact OLMC for medication order:
    • Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg (max 50 mcg per dose) IV/IO
    • In the event an IV cannot be established, Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg may be given IM up an initial dose of 25 mcg.
    • For additional doses, contact OLMC

Medical Control Option-Must contact prior to administration.

  1. Sedation after intubation (benzodiazepine).
  2. Treat for pain and anxiety, consider Protocol 509 - PAIN CONTROL/NAUSEA/VOMITING/ANXIETY and contact OLMC for medication order:
    a. Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg (max 50 mcg per dose) IV/IO.
    b. In the event an IV cannot be established, Fentanyl 1 mg/kg mcg may be given IM up an initial dose of 25 mcg/kg.
    c. For additional doses, contact OLMC.
