PEDIATRIC FEVER CONTROL

INTRODUCTION:

Normal body temperature ranges from 97 degrees (36.1 C) to 100.0 degrees (37.5 C). Fever is the natural way of fighting infection. Rapid temperature changes may cause seizures-especially in children (febrile seizures).

INDICATIONS:

A fever is a temperature of 100.4 (38.0 C) or greater.

EXCLUSIONS:

  1. Pediatric Seizures with a normal body temperature from 97 degrees (36.1 C) to 100.0 degrees (37.5 C).
  2. Contraindications include abdominal pain, allergy to medications, vomiting, active bleeding or concern from parents.
  3. Avoid acetaminophen in patients with liver problems.

EMT

  1. Focused history and Assessment.
  2. Full set of vital signs including blood glucose.
  3. Assess temperature.
  4. Oxygen: 15 L/min by mask, nasal cannula or blow-by to maintain SPO2>94%.
  5. Febrile Seizures: These seizures may mimic epilepsy but reassure parents that the occurrence of a bebrile seizure does not indicate the child will have epilepsy nor will the child suffer brain damage. A typical febrile seizure may last from a few seconds to several minutes. If a seizure does occur in the context of a recent cold/illness then:
    a. Place child on their side to promote drainage of secretions.
    b. Remove anything from the mouth (bottles, pacifiers.).
    c. Ensure the child will not strike anything while having a seizure.
    d. Apply blow-by oxygen (15 L/min) with a non rebreather mask.
    e. If seizure lasts >3 mintues begin rapid transfer-follow Protocol 714: Pediatric Seizures .

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. If temperature is >100.4 F (>38.0 C) and the patient does NOT have any contraindicationsm consider antipyretic medications.
  4. Advanced EMT-Contact OLMC for medication order.
  5. If the patient is awake and able to maintain their airway without assistance then the following steps may be instituted:
    a. Remove extra clothing-keep in T-shirt / diaper.
    b. Prevent overheating by not wrapping in blankets.
    c. Child's Tylenol shall be given if it had not been administered withing the last 4 hours *(Children's Tylenol normal dosing is q 4 hours).
    d. Dosing syringes are one time use only.
