PEDIATRIC VENTRICUAR TACHYCARDIA

NOTE:

  1. If no pulse present, trate as VF using Protocol 707: Pediatric Ventricular Fibrillation .
  2. For all arrhythmias begin with Protocol 708: Pediatric Cardiac Arrythmias .
  3. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines as needed.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess blood glucose level.
  3. Pediatric lowest acceptable systolic blood pressures:
    • Birth to 1 month = 60 mmHg.
    • 1 month to 1 year = 70 mmHg.
    • 1 year to 10 years = 70 mmHg + (age x 2) over 10 years - 90 mmHg.
  4. Provide 100% oxygen to the patient.
  5. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress- In general, maintain an oxygen saturation 90-94%.
  6. Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  7. Recommended pediatric ventilatory rates:
    • Infant (0-12 month): 25 breaths per minute.
    • 1-3 years: 20 breaths per minute.
    • 4-6 years: 15 breaths per minute.
    • >6 years: 12 (Same as adult).
  8. Do not hyperventilate the patient.
  9. Keep the patient NPO.
  10. Assess for signs or symptoms of hypo-perfusion:
    • Capillary refill >2 seconds.
    • Absent or weak peripheral pulses.
    • Cool, cold, cyanotic extremities.
    • Poor muscle tone.
    • Altered level of consciousness (lethargy or failure to recognize parents).
    • Hypotension (see chart below).
    • AGE - Abnormal (Low) PRESSURE.
AGE PRESSURE
0-2 Months 60 mm Hg
2 months to 1 Year 70 mm Hg
1 Year 70 + 2 x (years in age)
Over 10 Years 90 mm Hg

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  3. Obtain 12 lead if able and transmit to appropriate facility.
  4. Obtain vascular access if able-if hypotensive 20 ml/kg then TKO rate unless otherwise directed.
  5. Contact Medical Control for medication order:

Antiarrhythmic medication: Lidocaine IS THE ONLY ANTIARRHYTHMIC TO BE USED FOR CONVERSION. Amiodarone IS NOT TO BE USED

Stable Wife Complex (QRS > 120 msec) Tachycardia

Lidocaine : 1 mg/kg IV/IO
1. May repeat every 3-5 minutes up to 3 mg/kg.
2. If conversion to a Sinus Rhythm begin Lidocaine gtt.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is compete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotacheal tube when indicated or appropriate supraglottic airway.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. No nasotracheal intubation.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Unstable Tachycardia - Synchronized Cardioversion:

    • If no response to previous interventions and the patient has "Serious Signs or Symptoms" such as:
      • Active Congestive Heart Failure /Pulmonary Edema.
      • Altered Mental Status.

    • Signs of Shock.

      • Cool, clammy, or pale skin.

      • Weak or thready pulse.

  2. Synchronized Cardioversion:

    • Indicated immediately in the unstable patient.
    • Initiaitial energy dose is 0.5-1 J/kg.

    • If no response double energy dose to 2 J/kg.

  3. Sedation prior to Cardioversion - Protocol 509: Pain Control/Nausea/Vomiting/Anxiety .
