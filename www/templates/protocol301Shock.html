Shock

This Protocol addresses the management of patients with poor generalized perfusion caused by one of the following:

The goals of chest pain protocol include:

  1. FLUID problems.
  2. PUMP problems - Inadequate pumping of the heart, through weakness or death of cardiac muscle, a valvular dysfunction, or abnormal rhythms (i.e., cardiogenic shock).
  3. VESSEL problems - Loss of vessel tone due to autonomic imbalance or biochemical effects on vascular smooth muscle (e.g., neurogenic, septic, and anaphylactic shock) or vascular obstruction (e.g., pulmonary embolism, tension pneumothorax, or cadiac tamponade).

Patients in "SHOCK" for this Protocol will be defined as:

  1. All patients with a SYSTOLIC BLOOD PRESSURE OF 80mmHg or less
    OR

  2. Patients with SYSTOLIC BLOOD PRESSURE OF 80-100mmHg AND any of the following:

    a. Mental status change (decreased LOC, agitation, restlessness).
    b. Cool, diaphoretic or pale skin.
    c. Heart rate greater than 100/min.
    d. Active bleeding or recent history of bleeding from bowel, stomach, or major vessels.

EXCLUSIONS

  1. Patients with significant bradycardia or tachycardia (other than sinus tachycardia) should be treated initially with the appropriate CARDIAC ARRHYTHMIA PROTOCOL.
  2. Anaphylaxis or allergic reaction - follow PROTOCOL 310: Allergic Reaction/Anaphylaxis
  3. Spinal cord injury - follow PROTOCOL 503: Spinal Cord Injury
