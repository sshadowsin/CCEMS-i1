Phenergan

Promethazine
Approved for CCEMS Rescue Paramedics Only

Protocol 1020

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 509.

Antihistamine / Antiemetic

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Blocks Histamine Receptors.
2. Controls Nausea.
3. Potentiates Narcotics.

INDICATIONS
1. Nausea & Vomiting.
2. Motion Sickness

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Altered LOC.
2. Prior Recent Use of a Phenothiazine Drug (Compazine).
3. Prior Allergy or Known Phenothiazine Intolerance.
4. Cannot Be Given if the IV is Established in the Hand or Wrist - THIS IS AN ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION.

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Sedation.
2. Tachycardia/Bradycardia.
3. Hyptension if Given Rapid IV.
4. Severe Tissue Necrosis with Extravasation.

Considerations

1. Administer Through a Running IV Line at the Port Furthest From the IV Site.
2. Use Large Vein if Possible.
3. Monitor Site. Ask Patient to Report Burning or Pain at the IV Site. If Noted, Stop Immediately and Remove IV.
4. Do Not Use in Patients <2 Years of Age Because of the Potential for Fatal Respiratory Depression. Exercise Caution When Giving to Patients 2-17 Years of Age, Use Lowest Dose.
5. Avoid Concomitant Adminstration With Other Medications That Cuase Respiratory Depression.

Dosage and Administration

Adult Dose

1. IV: 12.5mg If SBP >100.
Dilute Phenergan with 10mL of NS and Administer Slowly Over 60 Seconds With a Wide Open IV Running so as to Dilute it While it is Adminsitered. IV MUST be ABOVE the Wrist, if not then Phenrgan May Not be Given!

2. IM: 25mg if no IV Access.

Peds Dose

NOT RECOMMENDED For Pediatric Patients, Requires OLMC Contact Prior to Administering.
