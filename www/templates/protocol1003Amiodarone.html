Amiodarone

Cordarone

Approved for Advanced CCEMS units only

Protocol 1003

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 102, 707.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Acts directly on the myocardium to delay repolarization
2. Depresses conduction velocity and slows conduction of the AV node
3. Some of the alpha and beta blockade activity and vasodilatory effects

INDICATIONS
1. Recommended after defibrillation and epinephrine in cardiac arrest with ventricular fibrillation and pulseless ventricular tachycardia.

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Sick Sinus Syndrome
2. AV block type II or III
3. Bradycardia that has caused syncope
4. Pregnancy

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Hypotension
2. Bradycardia
3. Cardiac arrest-cardiogenic shock
4. AV block

Dosage and Administration

1. Adults
a. VF/VT Cardiac Arrest unresponsive to CPR, Shock, Vasopressor
b. 300 mg IV/IO push
c. Repeat @ 150mg in 3-5 minutes 1x.

2. PEDS:
a. VF/VT Cardiac Arrest unresponsive to CPR, Shock, Vasopressor
b. 5mg/kg IV/IO bolus
c. May repeat 5mg/kg bolus in 3-5 minutes x1 dose (max dose 300mg)

CONSIDERATIONS

1. If Amiodarone is the first antiarrythmic used, then all repeat doses shall be of the same drug.
2. DO NOT ALTERNATE WITH LIDOCAINE, CAN CAUSE ASYSTOLE
3. Follow administration with a fluid bolus to enhance distribution
4. Elevate the extremity to facilitate drug delivery
