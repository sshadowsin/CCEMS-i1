DROWNING OR SUBMERSION

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. Safely remove patient from the water.
  2. Place patient supine.
  3. Remove wet clothing.
  4. Ensure patient warmth.
  5. Spinal motion restriction - Particular care to cervical spine immobilization should be given to patients with the possibility of a diving injury or drowning in shallow water.
  6. Scuba divers "Dive computer" or "Dive Log Book" should be transported with the patient.
  7. Airway maintenance is the primary consideration.
  8. There can be co-existing conditions depending on the type of submersion injury including trauma, hypothermia and intoxication.
  9. Hypotension is associated with a worse outcome, monitor closely and treat/prevent it.
  10. Submersion in cold water will often cause severe hypothermia, notify receiving hospital so that appropriate resources and warming equipment can be mobilized.
  11. CPR and other resuscitative efforst should be continued until arrival at the hospital as drowining patients may sometimes recover after prolonged resuscitative efforts.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Blood glucose, core body temperature and oxygen saturation assessment.
  3. Assess the scene for other environmental issues or possible toxins.
  4. Begin BCLS (using current AHA guidelines) if patient is pulseless and apneic - follow rythm specific 100 Series Protocol.
  5. If breathing spontaneously apply oxygen at 15L/Min via non-rebreather mask to maintain oxygen saturations >95%.
  6. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress - Consider a nasopharingeal or oropharyngeal airway.
  7. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust and positioning.

ADVANCED EMT / PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Follow rythm appropriate protocols if in cardiac arrest, Follow 100 series protocols.
  3. Establish vascular access IV/IO set at TKO rate unless hypotensive then give NS 250-500mL bolus for adult pateint or 20mL/kg for pediatric patient to max of 60ml/kg.
  4. Maintain airway with spinal stabilization-suction as needed.
  5. Advanced airway if necessary.

  6. Contact OLMC for drug adminstration orders:

    • Adult - Albuterol 2.5mg every 10 minutes via nebulization for bronchospasms/wheezing until symptoms subside.

    • Albuterol 2.5mg every 10 minutes via nebulization for bronchospasms/wheezing until symptoms subside. Start with 1.25mg if age <1 year.
  7. Reassess patient after each dose to determine need for additional dosing.
  8. Follow appropriate Trauma Protocol as appropriate.
