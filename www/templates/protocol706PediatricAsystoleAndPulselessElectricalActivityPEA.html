PEDIATRIC ASYSTOLE AND PULSELESS ELECTRICAL ACTIVITY (PEA)

NOTE:

  1. If rhythm is unclear and possible ventricular fibrillation, follow instructions for Protocol 707: Pediatric Ventricular Fibrillation.
  2. Begin all cardiac arrest series with Protocol 705: Pediatric Cardioplumonary Resuscitation.
  3. Follow current ACLS and PALS guidelines as needed.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Assess for presence of absence of a pulse.
  3. Assess blood glucose level.
  4. Begin Basic life support (BLS) per current American Heart Guidelines.
  5. Maintain airway patency via positioning and suctioning as necessary.
  6. Ventilate with 100% oxygen.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Determine rhythm - asystole, pulseless electrical activity, etc.
  3. Contnuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  4. Insert age appropriate advanced airway.
  5. Consider underlying causes and treat when possible:
    a. H's - Hypovolemia, Hypoxia, Hydrogen ion (Acidosis), Hyper/hypokalemia, Hypothermia, Hypoglycemia.
    b. T's - Toxins (overdose), Tamponade (Cardiac), Tension Pneumothorax, Thrombosis (ACS of Pulmonary), Trauma.
  6. Establish IV/IO access as able.
  7. Begin 20ml/kg bolus of NS, reassess and repeat if needed to a Max of 60cc/kg.
  8. Administer Epinephrine
    a. 0.01 mg/kg=0.1 ml/kg (1:10,000) IV/IO push.
    b. Repeat every 3-5 minutes as long as patient remains pulseless.
    c. Contact OLMC before terminating efforts in the field.
  9. Administer Dextrose if:
    a. IV or IO if blood glucose <60
    b. Age <12 years: D25 2cc/kg *(Dilute D50 1:1 with sterile NS if necessary.).
    c. Age >12 years: D50 2cc/kg

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. INTUBATION - Consider orotracheal intubation using an endotracheal tube when indicated.
  3. Document TWO confirmation methods to verify endotracheal placement of ET tube (e.g. EtCO2, CO2 Detection Device or Esophageal Intubation Detector).
  4. No nasotracheal intubation.
  5. Contact OLMC before terminating efforts in the field.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Suspected Tension Pneumothorax-Immediate needle decompression of affected side.
  2. Traumatic Arrest-Consider bilateral needle decompression based on mechanism of injury.
  3. Routine administration of Sodium Bicarb is not recommended in cardiac arrest. Contact Medical Control-consider in case of severe metabolic acidosis or prolonged down time. If ordered:
    a. Sodium Bicarb - 1 mEq/kg (1cc/kg of 8.4% solution) in children >1 month.
    b. Sodium Bicarb - 0.5 mEq/kg [0.5 cc/kg] in neonates (<30 days old). diluted 1:1 with NS or use 4.2% solution.
