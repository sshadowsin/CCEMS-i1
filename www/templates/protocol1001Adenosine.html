Adenosine

Approved for PARAMEDICS ONLY

Protocol 1001

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Class: ANTIARRHYTHMIC

Reference Protocols: 202, 710

.

MECHANISM OF ACTION

1. Slows conduction through the AV node
2. Can restore NSR in patients with SVT
3. Rapid onset with a duration of 1-2 minutes

INDICATIONS
1. Hemodynamically stable Paroxysmal Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)
2. Tachycardia associated with Wolff-Parkinson-White Syndrome

CONTRAINDICATIONS
1. Hypersensitivity
2. 2nd or 3rd degree heart block
3. Sick Sinus Syndrome
4. Atrial Flutter or Atrial Fibrillation

SIDE EFFECTS
1. Apprehension
2. Chest Pain
3. Hypotension
4. Bronchospasm

Dosage and Administration

MEDICAL CONTROL MUST BE CONTACTED PRIOR TO ADMINISTRATIONS

Each dose of Adenosine must be injected into the IV port closest to the patient's core. Each dose is to be followed by a 20-30 mL flush and the patient's arm should be raised to aid absorption

1. Adults 6mg administered rapid IVP with 20-30 mL Bolus of NS at the same time
a. If no conversion in 5 minutes, repeat dose @ 12 mg x1
b. Treat recurrence SVT with ADENOSINE 12mg x1
c. 3 doses total

2. PEDS: 0.1 mg/kg rapid IVP x1 dose (max dose of 6mg)
a. If no conversion in 5 minutes repeat dose at 0.2 mg/kg (max dose of 12mg x1)
b. Treat recurrence SVT with ADENOSINE 0.2 mg/kg (max dose 12mg) x1
c. 3 doses total
