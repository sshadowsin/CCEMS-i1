TEMPERATURE AND ENVIRONMENTAL EMERGENCIES

ALL PROVIDERS

  1. Scene and patient management
  2. Remove patient from hot or cold environment, when possible.
  3. Focused history and physical exam.
  4. Body temperature and blood glucose assessment.
  5. Assess level of consciousness.
  6. Assess for underlying causes; medications, toxins, CNS lesions or other medical conditions.
  7. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and pulse oximetry monitoring when available.

Treatment Plan

  1. Heat Related (Hyperthermia)
    • Temperature elevation WITHOUT altered mental status ( Heat Exhaustion)
      • Slow cooling with ice packs, wet towels, and/or fans to areas in the vicinity of head and nek, axillae, and groin.
      • If patient is alert and not nauseated, oral rehydration with water or balanced electrolyte solution.
      • Severe muscle cramps may be relieved by gentle stretching of the muscles.
    • Temperature elevation WITH altered mental staus ( Heat Stroke)
      • Aggressive cooling to unclothed patient utilizing fine mist water spray and fans in conjuction with ice packs to head and nect area, groin and axilla while maintaining modesty. NOT recommended for children and infants.
      • Aggressive cooling should be stopped if shivering begins.
      • Monitor closely for dysrhythmia, recognize and treat with the appropriate protocol.
  2. Cold Related (Hypothermia)
    • Protect patient from further heat loss (application of blankets, warm environment, etc.).
    • Suspicion of cardiac arrest in cold environments: Utilize 30-45 seconds to confirm pulselessness.
      c. Confirm body temperature and treat accordingly.
      • Severe <86F(30C)
        • No active external rewarming (no heat, forced hot air, warm packs, etc.)
        • Limit defibrillation attempts to 3 and NO external pacing.
        • Rapid but gentle transport (rough movement may preciptiate arrhythmias).
      • Moderate 86-93F (30-34C)
        • Use warm packs to head and neck, axillaw, and groin.
      • Mild >93F (34C)
        • Warm with blankets, warm environment, etc.
  3. Frostbite precautions - Do not rub or use dry external head. Re-warm with 40C water if possible.
  4. Avoid refreezing. It is better not to rewarm frostbite if refreezing is a possiblility.

ADVANCED EMT/PARAMEDIC

  1. Adult:
    • Advanced airway, vascular access and fluid therapy as necessary.
    • Warm or cool IV fluids, if available, should be begun for moderate to severe hypothermia or hyperthermia, respectively.
    • Cold emergencies - withold antiarrhythmic meds until temperature >86F (30C)
    • Administer Epinephrine according to standard ACLS algorithm.
  2. Pediatric Patient:
    • Advanced airway, vascular access and fluid therapy NS 20ml/kg bolus.
    • Warm or cool IV fluids, if available, should be begun for moderate to severe hypothermia or hyperthermia, respectively.
    • Cold emergencies - withold antiarrhythmic meds until temperature >86F (30C)
    • Administer Epinephrine according to standard ACLS algorithm.
