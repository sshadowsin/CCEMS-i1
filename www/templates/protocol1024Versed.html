Versed

Midazolam
Approved for Rescue/Ambulance Units Only

Protocol 1024

NOT a treatment protocol. See reference protocol listed below for use.

Reference Protocols: 201, 310, 402, 509, 714, 718, 726.

Anticonvulsant / Sedative

Mechanism of Action:
Also known as “Midazolam” is a short acting benzodiazepine medication used for anesthesia, procedural sedation, severe agitation, and to control seizures. It works by increasing the action of gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA), the major inhibitory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system. Midazolam depresses the limbic system, thalamus, and hypothalamus resulting in profound sedation and muscle relaxation. Versed can be given by mouth, intravenously, or injection into a muscle, or by spraying into the nose. When given intravenously, it typically begins working within five minutes; when injected into a muscle, it can take 15 minutes to begin working, intranasal is faster. Effects can last between 1-6 hours.

Description: Versed is a Short Acting Benzodiazepine. The Medication Can be Given Intravenously or Intranasally.

INDICATIONS

1. Seizures (Must be Having Active Seizure).
2. Adjunct to Cardioversion or External Cardiac Pacing.
3. Sedation Post Intubation.

CONTRAINDICATIONS

1. Known Hypersensitivity to Versed or Other Benzodiazepines.
2 Respiratory Depression or Failure.
3. Shock.
4. Pregnancy.
5. Suspected Febrile Seizures.
6. Head Trauma.

Cautions:

1. Use Carefully in the Setting of Other Respiratory Drugs Such As Alcohol, Narcotics, Sedatives...
2. Use Carefully in the Elderly Who Will be More Sensitive to the Effects.
3. Titrate the Drug to the Desired Effect.
4. Monitor Oxygen Saturation/Capnography Continuously.
5. Stop Immediately if the Patient Develops Agitation, Hyperactivity, or Involuntary Movements.

Considerations

1. Have Oxygen, BVM, and Intubation Ready.
2. Establish IV if Able.
3. Give Medication Slowly, Stop if Achieve Desired Effect.
4. Monitor Oxygen, Mental Status, Vital Signs and Reaction.
5. Assess for Other Causes of Seizures (Dysrhythmias, Hypoglycemia, Trauma)...
6. Constantly Reassess Patient and Vital Signs.
7. Do Not Exceed Adult Dosing.
8. Contact OLMC for Additional Doses.

Dosage and Administration

Adult Dose

1. IV/IO - 2-5mg SLOW IV Every 10 Minutes to the Desired Effect or Max Dose of 10mg.
2. IM - 5-10mg
3. IN (Intranasal) 10mg Divided Dose Between Nostrils (Use Atomizer).

Peds Dose

1. IV/IO 0.1mg/kg (Max Dose of 4mg per dose) SLOW IV.

2. Intranasal or Oral 0.2mg/kg (Max Dose 10mg per Dose).
