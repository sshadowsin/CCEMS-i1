PEDIATRIC SEIZURE

INDICATIONS:

  1. Must be actively seizing for administration of Versed or Valium.
  2. Alteration in consciousness associated with eye deviation, loss of bowel/bladder control, non-purposeful rhythmic or tonic body movements.

EMT

  1. Focused history and physical exam.
  2. Blood glucose, Oxygen Saturation and Temperature assessment.
  3. Assess scene for possible toxin overdose or trauma.
  4. Do not restrain, but provide protection during the tonic-clonic phase.
  5. Spinal immobilization if indicated.
  6. Ensure patients experiencing febrile seizures are not excessively dressed or bundled.
  7. Provide basic airway maneuvers to all compromised airways, i.e. oxygen, jaw-thrust, and positioning.
  8. Ventilate with BVM when apneic or exhibiting respiratory distress - Consider a nasal or oral airway.
  9. Recommended pediatric ventilatory rates:
    a. Infany (0-12 months): 25 breaths per minute.
    b. 1-3 years: 20 breaths per minute.
    c. 3-5 years: 15 breaths per minute.
    c. >6 years: 12 (same as adult).
  10. Do not hyperventilate the patient.
  11. SUCTION as needded.
  12. OXYGEN 15 L/min via NRM. Maintain SPO2 >94%.
  13. ASSESS vitals, mental status, neurolgical examination.
  14. Place in lateral position to prevent aspiration.

ADVANCED EMT

  1. Ensure above is complete.
  2. Establish vascular access.
  3. Continuous ECG, ETCO2, and Pulse Oximetry monitoring when available.
  4. If blood glucose is less than 60 mg/dl:
    • Children geater than 1 year: Dextrose 25% (D25W) 2 mL/kg IV.
    • Infants up to 1 year: Dextrose 10% (D10/NS) 5 mL/kg IV.
    • Neonates <30 days: Dextrose10% (D10/NS) 2 ml/kg (200 mg/kg) IV.

NOTE: If commercially prepared mediction is not available:

  • To make D10 - Dilute 10 mL D50 in 40 mL of NS.
  • To make D25 - Dilute D50 1:1 with NS.

IF UNABLE TO ESTABLISH IV ACCESS:
Glucagon 0.1 mg/kg (max dose of 1 mg) IM if no IV/IO access.

Contact medical control for medication order:

Midazolam (Versed)
1. Dosage is cut in half if the patient has received narcotics or alcohol.
2. Consider the size of the patient for dosing.
3. IV/IO - 0.1 mg/kg (max dose of 4 mg per dose) SLOW IV
4. Intranasal or oral - 0.2 mg/kg (max dose 10 mg per dose).
5. DO NOT exceed adult dosing.
6. Contact OLMC for additional doses.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Ensure above is compete.
  2. For persistent or recurrent seizure activity:
    • Diazepam (Valium)
      • May be used as an alternative- IV/IO 0.1 mg/kg (max dose of 5 mg)
      • Rectally - 0.3 mg/kg PR max. dose 10 mg - May be used as an alternative.
  3. Follow the same safety parameters with Midazolam - Do NOT exceed adult dosing.

Medical Control Option - must contact prior to administration

  1. Magnesium Sulfate - For female pediatric patients who possible are pregnant and have a seizure contact OLMC for dosing instructions ( PARAMEDIC ONLY)
